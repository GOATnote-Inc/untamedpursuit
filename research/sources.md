### San Francisco 1847 (Book 2, Act III)

- SF Museum, "San Francisco — Before the Gold Rush — 1847" — http://sfmuseum.org/hist1/early.html (accessed 2026-03-06). Population, building counts, settlement growth.
- Library of Congress, "View of San Francisco, formerly Yerba Buena, in 1846-7" — https://www.loc.gov/resource/g4364s.pm000332/ (accessed 2026-03-06). Contemporary map/illustration.
- FoundSF, "WHY SAN FRANCISCO??? CITY ORIGINS: 1835-1849" — https://www.foundsf.org/index.php?title=WHY_SAN_FRANCISCO%3F%3F%3F_CITY_ORIGINS%3A_1835-1849 (accessed 2026-03-06). Civic origins, commerce, street development.
- SFGenealogy, "The Beginnings of San Francisco" (Chapters XV, XVII, Appendix B) — https://legacy.sfgenealogy.org/sf/history/hbbeg15.htm (accessed 2026-03-06). Streets, buildings, Clay Street spring, grist mill.
- FoundSF, "Sand Dunes of Early San Francisco" — https://www.foundsf.org/Sand_Dunes_of_Early_San_Francisco (accessed 2026-03-06). Natural landscape before development.
- NPS, "'Sand' Francisco" — https://www.nps.gov/places/000/11-sand-francisco.htm (accessed 2026-03-06). Dune ecosystem extent and character.
- NOAA, "Climate of San Francisco Technical Memorandum" — https://www.weather.gov/media/mtr/climate/SFD_CLIMATE.pdf (accessed 2026-03-06). Summer fog, temperature ranges, wind patterns.
- SF Examiner, "The incredible story of William Leidesdorff" — https://www.sfexaminer.com (accessed 2026-03-06). First Black millionaire, town treasurer 1847.
- Wikipedia: "Yerba Buena, California," "History of San Francisco," "Portsmouth Square," "Yerba Buena Cove," "Jasper O'Farrell" (accessed 2026-03-06). General reference.
- California State Parks, "Monterey State Historic Park" — https://www.parks.ca.gov/pages/575/files/montereyshpfinalweb080814.pdf (accessed 2026-03-06). 1847 Monterey buildings and settlement.
