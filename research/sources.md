### San Francisco 1847 (Book 2, Act III)

- SF Museum, "San Francisco — Before the Gold Rush — 1847" — http://sfmuseum.org/hist1/early.html (accessed 2026-03-06). Population, building counts, settlement growth.
- Library of Congress, "View of San Francisco, formerly Yerba Buena, in 1846-7" — https://www.loc.gov/resource/g4364s.pm000332/ (accessed 2026-03-06). Contemporary map/illustration.
- FoundSF, "WHY SAN FRANCISCO??? CITY ORIGINS: 1835-1849" — https://www.foundsf.org/index.php?title=WHY_SAN_FRANCISCO%3F%3F%3F_CITY_ORIGINS%3A_1835-1849 (accessed 2026-03-06). Civic origins, commerce, street development.
- SFGenealogy, "The Beginnings of San Francisco" (Chapters XV, XVII, Appendix B) — https://legacy.sfgenealogy.org/sf/history/hbbeg15.htm (accessed 2026-03-06). Streets, buildings, Clay Street spring, grist mill.
- FoundSF, "Sand Dunes of Early San Francisco" — https://www.foundsf.org/Sand_Dunes_of_Early_San_Francisco (accessed 2026-03-06). Natural landscape before development.
- NPS, "'Sand' Francisco" — https://www.nps.gov/places/000/11-sand-francisco.htm (accessed 2026-03-06). Dune ecosystem extent and character.
- NOAA, "Climate of San Francisco Technical Memorandum" — https://www.weather.gov/media/mtr/climate/SFD_CLIMATE.pdf (accessed 2026-03-06). Summer fog, temperature ranges, wind patterns.
- SF Examiner, "The incredible story of William Leidesdorff" — https://www.sfexaminer.com (accessed 2026-03-06). First Black millionaire, town treasurer 1847.
- Wikipedia: "Yerba Buena, California," "History of San Francisco," "Portsmouth Square," "Yerba Buena Cove," "Jasper O'Farrell" (accessed 2026-03-06). General reference.
- California State Parks, "Monterey State Historic Park" — https://www.parks.ca.gov/pages/575/files/montereyshpfinalweb080814.pdf (accessed 2026-03-06). 1847 Monterey buildings and settlement.

### San Francisco 1847 — Harbor Commerce, Alcalde, Waterfront, Dispatch (Research Supplement, 2026-03-08)

- FoundSF, "Yerba Buena Cove Early Development" — https://www.foundsf.org/Yerba_Buena_Cove_Early_Development (accessed 2026-03-08). Water lots, building counts, cove development.
- Wikipedia, "California hide trade" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_hide_trade (accessed 2026-03-08). Trade goods, hide values, shipping routes.
- Encyclopedia.com, "Alcaldes" — https://www.encyclopedia.com/history/dictionaries-thesauruses-pictures-and-press-releases/alcaldes (accessed 2026-03-08). Alcalde powers, Mexican-to-American transition.
- Wikipedia, "Edwin Bryant (alcalde)" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwin_Bryant_(alcalde) (accessed 2026-03-08). Water lot sales, term of office.
- SFGenealogy, "Alcaldes and Mayors" — https://www.sfgenealogy.org/doku.php?id=san_francisco_history:alcaldes_and_mayors (accessed 2026-03-08). George Hyde biography, alcalde succession.
- SFSD History, "Before California Statehood 1846-1850" — https://www.sfsdhistory.com/eras/before-california-statehood (accessed 2026-03-08). Judicial structure, constables.
- SFGenealogy, "The Beginnings of San Francisco, Chapter XVII" — https://legacy.sfgenealogy.org/sf/history/hbbeg17.htm (accessed 2026-03-08). O'Farrell survey, water lot dimensions and prices.
- EPA, "Bay Fill in San Francisco: A History of Change" — https://semspub.epa.gov/work/09/1137835.pdf (accessed 2026-03-08). Cove geography, tidelands.
- Wikipedia, "El Camino Real (California)" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Camino_Real_(California) (accessed 2026-03-08). Mission spacing (~30 mi), route.
- SF Museum, "William T. Sherman and Early California History" — http://sfmuseum.org/hist6/sherm40s.html (accessed 2026-03-08). Sherman's 1847 horseback journey Monterey→SF.
- Library of Congress, Sherman, W.T., "Recollections of California, 1846-1861" — https://www.loc.gov/item/45007048/ (accessed 2026-03-08). Primary source: two-day ride, Gilroy overnight.
- Western Cover Society, "California Mails, 1821-1849" — https://www.westerncoversociety.org/literature/books/mails-of-the-westward-expansion-1803-to-1861/mails-of-the-westward-expansion-1803-to-1861-chapter-4/ (accessed 2026-03-08). Kearny's semi-weekly mail, Mexican-era mail routes.
- Wikipedia, "Samuel Brannan" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Brannan (accessed 2026-03-08). California Star Express, private courier rates.
- Wikipedia, "Interim government of California" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interim_government_of_California (accessed 2026-03-08). Military governors, alcalde appointments.
- Encyclopedia.com, "Hide and Tallow Trade" — https://www.encyclopedia.com/history/dictionaries-thesauruses-pictures-and-press-releases/hide-and-tallow-trade (accessed 2026-03-08). Goods exchanged for hides.
- GoldenNuggetLibrary/SFGenealogy, "George Hyde" — https://goldennuggetlibrary.sfgenealogy.org/sfbhyde.htm (accessed 2026-03-08). Hyde biography, land grants, judicial actions.
