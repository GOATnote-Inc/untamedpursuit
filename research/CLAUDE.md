# Research

## Purpose

This directory contains historical research that supports the series. Every factual claim in the books should be traceable back to a source documented here.

## Structure

- `sources.md` — Master bibliography of all sources consulted
- `historical-events/` — Research on specific events referenced in the story
- `period-details/` — Daily life, culture, technology, language research
- `fact-checks/` — Verification of claims made in drafted chapters

## Conventions

- One topic per file, named descriptively: `roman-road-networks.md`, `medieval-marriage-customs.md`
- Every file must include a Sources section with full citations
- Mark the reliability of each source: primary, secondary, or tertiary
- Note scholarly disagreements — don't present contested claims as fact
- Cross-reference with `bible/world/` when research informs world-building

## Using the /research Skill

The `/research [topic]` skill automates the research workflow:
1. Checks existing research first
2. Searches for reliable external sources
3. Documents findings with citations
4. Updates the master bibliography
5. Proposes bible updates when relevant
