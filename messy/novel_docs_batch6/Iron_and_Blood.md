# Chapter One: Iron and Blood

Storm disliked trains.

Eve felt it in the horse as they watched her parents coach being loaded. Kate insisted on rail. More efficient. Safer.

Eve looked down at Ghost. Watch him for me?

Jack adjusted his Winchester. Though I doubt Thompsons leg will thank us for the rail vibration.

Kate checked her medical bag. Better some discomfort than gangrene.

Theatrical as ever, Jessie called from the station shadows. Queen moved beside her.

Watch the northern pass, Jack told Jessie. Wire said activity.

She had no way of knowing it would be the last time she saw it that way.

---

## The Attack

The attack came two hours later.

The first shot shattered the window. Ghost was up. Jack moved. Down!

The Gatling gun rattle drowned everything.

Kate shoved her toward the door. Go! Now! We hold here. This is our train.

Training took over. Last glimpse: Jack aiming, Kate working on wounded, Ghost between them.

The blow came from nowhere. Rifle stock to the temple. Eve staggered.

Thunder on stone. Different thunder. Familiar thunder. Hoofbeats.

---

## Dynamite Creek Canyon

Jessie was making out with Thomas Mitchell by the creek when Queens ears snapped forward. Gunfire started. Gatling gun.

Head back to town now, Jessie snapped. Unless you fancy catching a stray round.

The train sat below like a broken snake. Flash of familiar red hair stopped Jessie cold. Eve bleeding stumbling still fighting.

They took the ridge at a gallop. Jessie picked targets with casual precision.

You can be so needy, Jessie whispered, gathering her friend.

First dynamite stick marked SPECIAL. Half the canyon wall came down. Second throw was pure art. Third was just because.

---

## Echoes

Ghosts howl shattered the evening silence. Three days since the attack.

Storm filled the clinic doorway keeping watch. He refused to leave.

Some things did not need words.

Ghost howled again and Eve let the sound carry grief and love to whatever stars her parents now watched from.

Dawn would come soon enough.
