# Episode I

25 year old Clara Chen stood at the edge of her private dock, breathing in the complex mixture of tar, rope, salt, and violence. George, her massive black Continental Shepherd, sat alertly at her side, his intelligent eyes scanning the waterfront perfectly synchronizing with hers. Night had settled over Philadelphia's port, and a forest of ship masts seemed to shimmer with the star-filled sky. The rigging creaked softly in the evening breeze, a familiar song.

Her London-tailored leather coat, cut to the latest fashion while concealing multiple Colts, moved silently with each breath of wind. When Harper approached, George's ears twitched slightly.

Miss Chen. Harper's voice carried quiet concern. The carriage is ready.

Clara didn't turn, her silver-handled sword cane tapping absently against the weathered planks while George remained perfectly still beside her.

Mr. Harper, I am far from defenseless, she said softly, noting George's subtle shift toward movement in the shadows near the cargo stacks. A rat, nothing more.

I've yet to meet anyone who can match your accuracy with a pistol, Harper admitted, much less your speed with multiple weapons.

And the sword? Clara's slight smile held memories of countless dawn sessions.

While I cannot argue with your expertise in any weapon and the skill of George, numbers can beat talent.

You are not wrong. Clara turned to face him. What if I start on the northernmost aspect of this yard, and you take a team and start at the southernmost aspect of town?

May we walk with you?

No, Harper. Tonight, I wish to walk alone. The moon is beautiful. The water lapping is speaking to me. I also wish to be a young woman and artist tonight. I acknowledge the risks of walking alone.

Yes, Miss Chen. Harper's resignation carried years of watching her outshoot every security expert he'd hired.

Clara began her walk, boots clicking softly against the cobblestones. George moved like a shadow beside her.

A scream shattered the night.

George's muscles tensed beneath his thick coat. Not the usual drunken bellow of sailors, but something far worse: a child's raw terror.

She moved like a jaguar, George pacing beside her without command. At Clara's subtle hand signal, George split away, circling to approach from another angle.

Tonight, they would defend those who could not defend themselves.

## Episode 1 Act II

Just one more night, Samuel Taylor muttered, watching the Mercy Jane's crew from a hidden position near his newly purchased warehouse.

Children's whimpering, followed by one terrified scream, carried across the docks.

Sam moved. Let them go, he called out, stepping into the moonlight. Whatever you're being paid, I'll double it.

Six men. More than expected. The closest one spat. Mr. Taylor. Told the captain you were asking too many questions.

Last chance, Sam said quietly. Release the children.

The first attack came from behind. Sam sidestepped, using the man's momentum to send him sprawling. But the others rushed him together, and a wooden plank caught his gun hand with numbing force.

A flash of movement caught his eye - a woman in an elegant coat, moving with lethal grace. Before he could process this, a massive black dog materialized from the shadows like an avenging spirit.

Her sword cane transformed into a lethal blade with liquid grace. Her left hand produced a Colt with impossible speed, and two more traffickers fell to precisely placed shots.

She had dropped an empty Colt, and a new Colt was half-spent before the other hit the ground.

George, hold, the woman commanded softly. The dog stilled instantly.

Hi there. I'm Clara Chen, she said, as casually as if they were meeting at a social function rather than standing among cooling corpses.

Samuel Taylor, he managed, tasting blood from his split lip.

A man emerged from the shadows. Miss Chen. Mr. Taylor. Shall I arrange cleanup?

Yes, Clara confirmed. And ensure the children's families understand they'll be under our protection going forward.

Tea tomorrow? Her smile held a hint of challenge. Three o'clock at this tea house, she said while handing him her card.

You just saved my life and the life of these two girls. For this I am eternally in your debt. You also just killed 12 or more men in what seemed to be a blur. Miss Chen, I am surely dead if I am not at tea tomorrow.

## Episode I: Act III - The Aftermath

Thomas's office occupied the ground floor of Clara's townhouse - a space that balanced understated elegance with practical efficiency.

How did the Mercy Jane get past our guard? Clara asked, standing at the window.

Thomas looked up from his desk. Manifests. Legitimate cargo layered with illegitimate. Unfortunately, this story is common and the bane of our efforts.

What suggestions do you have that may stem this tide?

Short term? Tighten ship inspections. Levy a tax that requires inspectors to see all cargo. Create law that hangs perpetrators. Long term? End poverty. Educate all regarding the dangers. Make sure people no longer view slavery as acceptable.

What are we doing to fund these efforts?

I don't know of anyone doing more. Thomas's voice softened. You seem to be giving away the fortune of many kings to achieve these goals. It's not enough.

I have two tasks for you to consider. First, fix this. Lay out your best plan. Create a list of the top ten things we can do. Then, name the most important thing I can help with on that list.

And the second task?

By our weapons practice in the early afternoon, I want everything you can find out about Samuel Taylor. I have tea with him at 3 pm.

Thomas's eyebrows rose slightly. Professionally? Consider it done. A smile tugged at his lips. Personally... Sam Taylor is handsome as hell. Tea? Okay. What are you wearing? What are you serving? Where? Tell me.

Clara laughed - a rare, genuine sound that made George's tail thump once against the carpet. I love you, Thomas.

And well, you should. He began gathering papers. Now, about Mr. Taylor's background - some interesting patterns exist in his property acquisitions. Rather like someone else I know...

The grandfather clock struck midnight as Thomas began his investigation into Samuel Taylor. Tomorrow promised to be very interesting indeed.
