# FORTUNE'S TIDE
## Episode 1: "The Night Watch"

### TEASER

FADE IN:

EXT. PHILADELPHIA DOCKS - NIGHT - 1846

Fog rolls thick between warehouses. Moonlight catches on wet cobblestones. The Delaware River laps against creaking ships.

SUPER: "PHILADELPHIA - 1846"

Through the mist, a FIGURE emerges - CLARA CHEN (28, Chinese-American, elegant but with hidden steel in her movements). She walks with purpose, a silver-handled cane in hand.

MR. HARPER (O.S.)
Miss Chen, please. Take the carriage.

CLARA turns. MR. HARPER (40s, weathered face, loyal eyes) stands in the shadows.

CLARA
(gentle but firm)
The night air helps me think, Mr. Harper.

HARPER
These docks aren't safe after dark. Wallace's men are getting bolder.

CLARA
(touching her cane)
Then perhaps they need a reminder of why they once feared the night.

She continues walking. Harper melts back into the shadows - following, watching.

INT. WAREHOUSE OFFICE - NIGHT

Through a grimy window, we see Clara passing below. Inside, THOMAS ARLINGTON (30s, sharp-dressed, sharper mind) reviews documents by lamplight.

A KNOCK. Thomas quickly covers certain papers.

THOMAS
Enter.

A DOCK WORKER bursts in, breathless.

DOCK WORKER
Sir! By the Irish quarter - Wallace's men. At least fifteen of them.

Thomas grabs his coat.

EXT. DOCKS - IRISH QUARTER - NIGHT - CONTINUOUS

Clara pauses, hearing raised voices. She moves closer, staying in shadows.

REVEAL: A group of THUGS (15-20 men) surrounding SAMUEL TAYLOR (30s, powerful build beneath fine clothes) and several IRISH DOCK WORKERS - including an elderly man and his teenage son.

ELDERLY IRISH WORKER
(thick accent)
We've paid our dues to the dock master! We've proper papers!

THUG LEADER
Papers don't matter tonight. Mr. Wallace has new terms.

SAM
(stepping forward)
These men work my section. Their contracts are with me.

THUG LEADER
Then consider this a hostile takeover, Mr. Taylor.

More thugs emerge from the shadows, carrying clubs, knives, and pistols. They begin to encircle Sam and the workers.

Clara watches, counting. Her hand shifts on her cane, thumb pressing a hidden catch.

SAM
(to Irish workers)
When I move, run for the warehouse.

The first thug SWINGS. Sam blocks, counters - his movements revealing boxing training. He drops two men quickly, but there are too many.

A plank CRACKS across his jaw. He staggers.

Clara steps from the shadows. In one fluid motion, she draws her sword from the cane and a pistol from her skirts.

CLARA
Gentlemen. You seem lost.

Every head turns. The nearest thug lunges at her with a knife. Clara's blade FLASHES - his hand falls to the cobblestones.

All hell breaks loose.

Clara moves like water through the mob. Her sword describes lethal arcs while her pistol CRACKS with precision. Each shot finds its mark.

Sam recovers. His powerful strikes complement her blade work but she is taking on 4x what he is.

A thug rushes them. Clara's blade takes his throat while Sam's fist drops another.

Harper appears with reinforcements. Gunfire erupts from the warehouse windows - Thomas has positioned sharpshooters.

The remaining thugs flee, leaving their dead and wounded.

CLARA
(over her shoulder)
Your face appears broken.

SAM
(through clenched teeth)
Thanks for your concern.

Silence descends. Clara calmly wipes her blade as Harper's men secure the scene.

CLARA
(to Irish workers)
Mr. Harper will see you safely home.
(to elderly worker)
Tell the others - my docks remain open to honest work.

The Irish workers hurry off with Harper's men. Sam watches Clara sheath her sword.

SAM
(touching his jaw)
That's a remarkable weapon.

CLARA
Yes it is.
(studying him)
You're either very brave or very foolish to face those odds alone.

SAM
I could say the same.

CLARA
(small smile)
Then we should discuss our mutual foolishness over tea and get some ice for... you she says while looking at his numerous wounds head to toe.

CUT TO MAIN TITLES

---

INT. CLARA'S TOWNHOUSE - STUDY - NIGHT

Elegant but practical. Maps on walls. Shipping ledgers on shelves. Weapons displayed with both art and practicality - a mixture of East and West.

Clara pours tea while Sam holds a cold compress to his jaw. Thomas paces, agitated.

THOMAS
Nineteen men. Wallace is escalating.

CLARA
He's testing boundaries.
(to Sam)
However, I'm curious about why he chose your section to test.

SAM
Because I refused his offer last week.
(winces as he sips tea)
He wanted to "partner" on the Gold Rush preparations.

CLARA
(sharp interest)
California.

SAM
Wallace knows what's coming. He's trying to control the ports before the rush begins. Goods produced in Philadelphia, such as tools, machinery, and food, will be exported through its ports to San Francisco

Thomas exchanges a look with Clara.

THOMAS
Miss Chen, if I may - I'd like to discuss further when Mr Taylor is not present.

CLARA
(cutting him off)
Mr Samuel Taylor's reputation suggests that he builds with skill, not force.
(to Sam)
Though clearly, you can handle yourself in a fight.

SAM
As can you. That sword work...

CLARA
I believe in being prepared. But this many men on my docks surprised me.

SAM
(thoughtful)
Preparation. Yes.
(meets her eyes)
I would like to discuss more about San Francisco with you. Can we do tea again tonight after I have cleaned up? I would like to bring a list of questions for you and I intend to share more if my condition allows.

CLARA
Lets create a list of questions now for tonight's tea. That way, we agree upon the agenda.

They both began to write as, through the window, dawn touched the ships in harbor. A new day - and perhaps a new alliance - beginning.

FADE OUT.
