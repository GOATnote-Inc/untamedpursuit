# Chapter: The Art of Dissection

Jessie, I am a dying woman. I have grown to love you. If you would allow I would like to tell you how I came to know Eve's mom Kate.

I hope in me telling you this story it helps you to know a little more about Kate, who died a fucking legend. You love Eve, this may help you to know more about Eve.

The anatomy lab smelled of camphor and formaldehyde, mixing with the crisp October air seeping through the high windows. Kate Morrison moved quietly, checking her cadaver's markings against her meticulously detailed notes. She had began to call the cadaver Anna affectionately over the past few weeks. She would thank Anna nightly for donating to her medical education, and she would not take this donation lightly. Each incision she'd made over the past weeks was exactly where it should be, each structure was identified and labeled carefully.

The door creaked open well past midnight. Kate didn't look up—only one other student came to the lab this late. Clara Chen arrived in a rustle of silk and perfume that seemed oddly out of place among the specimens, as if she'd come directly from some social engagement. Again.

Clara's usually immaculate appearance was slightly disheveled tonight. Her hands trembled almost imperceptibly as she lit the lamp at her workstation. Kate noticed—she'd trained herself to notice everything in this room—but said nothing.

"Posterior triangle of the neck," Clara muttered, fumbling with her textbook. "Boundaries and contents..."

The words hung in the still air between them. Kate knew why. The afternoon's meeting with Professor Matthews and the anatomy faculty had echoed through the halls: "Miss Chen, your performance on the examination was... concerning. Perhaps medical school isn't the appropriate path..."

Kate had heard of similar meetings before. They were usually the end. Students would pack their things, disappear quietly, their dreams of becoming doctors fading like morning mist. But something about Clara's reaction had been different. Where others showed defeat, Clara's eyes had flashed with... determination? Fury? Something that suggested she wasn't finished yet.

"The sternocleidomastoid forms the anterior border," Clara recited, her voice tight with frustration. "The trapezius... the trapezius..."

"Posterior border," Kate said quietly, still focused on her own work. "The clavicle forms the inferior border."

Clara's head snapped up. It was the first time Kate had spoken to her directly, despite sharing the lab for weeks. "I know that," she said, then immediately softened. "I do know it. I just can't seem to keep it all straight when—"

"When you're trying to manage three business ventures, two social circles, and whatever that thing is you do down at the docks at odd hours?" Kate finally looked up, her steady gaze meeting Clara's startled one.

"How did you—"

"You smell like silk trading houses on Tuesdays. Your hands have dock tar traces on Thursdays. And you've been speaking with a slight Cantonese accent this week instead of your usual Mandarin, which suggests you're dealing with new shipping contacts." Kate shrugged. "I observe. It's what makes me good at this."

Clara's surprise turned to something like respect. "And here I thought I was being subtle."

"You are. To everyone else." Kate wiped her hands and moved to Clara's table. "Show me your dissection from last week."

Clara hesitated, then pulled back the sheet. Kate examined the work in silence.

"Your technique is excellent," she said finally. "These cuts are precise. You understand the relationships between structures. You're not failing because you can't do the work."

"I'm failing because I'm not here enough to do it properly," Clara finished.

"Yes." Kate's directness was refreshing after weeks of polite suggestions from professors.

Clara straightened her spine. "I can't abandon my other responsibilities. They're... important."

"I'm not suggesting you should." Kate returned to her own table, but her voice carried clearly. "The posterior triangle contains the spinal accessory nerve, branches of the cervical plexus, and the external jugular vein. The scalene muscles form its floor."

Clara blinked at the sudden return to anatomy.

"You'll find," Kate continued, "that the structures are easier to remember if you understand not just where they are, but why they're there. Everything has a purpose. Everything connects. Medicine and anatomy both have a large amount of pattern recognition" She glanced up. "Rather like your business ventures, I imagine."

A slow smile spread across Clara's face. "Are you offering to help me study?"

Kate's hands moved surely over her cadaver. "I start at midnight every night. Come or not, I will be here."

Clara's voice held new energy. "Thank you..."

"Kate Morrison."

The anatomy lab fell silent except for the scratch of pencils and the whisper of pages turning. Two women worked steadily through the night, learning to see patterns in nerves, muscle and bone. Professor Matthews noticed Clara's next examination score was the second highest in the class—just a point behind Kate's.
