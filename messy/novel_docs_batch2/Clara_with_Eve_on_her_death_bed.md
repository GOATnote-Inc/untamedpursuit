# Clara with Eve on Her Death Bed

Sam held her
Clara's days became more painful and less lucid.
Eve was out on her first mission.
Sam was with Clara. He refused to have any further interruptions on wednesday.
By sunday her breathing was rapid and shallow.

Clara and Eve said their goodbye's
Clara preferred for Eve to remember her the way she was most her life.
Eve stated something beautiful in response.
"Clara you are not someone in my life whose memory is so fickle that one or two weeks with you would have much of a change.
In my life Clara you are like a river of life. You brought life to areas that had none. You brought sustenance in periods of drought. You have provided me with places of refuge again and again. Visiting you during your dying days is like seeing a river in summer time as the snow melts cease and it becomes more like a creek. Clara I have not forgotten and will not forget you. Clara there is no gift fit for you. There is no trinket that I might provide that can encapsulate the depth of our relationship. Death will come for you, it seems sooner than me. But life with you has been a gift to me and to many others who surround you.
I will make no commitment in regard to my future, but I suspect this is no suprise to you.

As Clara lay in her bed, her breathing shallow and labored, the room was steeped in an aura of quiet solemnity. Sunlight slipped through the curtains, casting soft, golden beams over her face, lined with the years of teaching and nurturing a small army of apprentices. Eve knelt by her side, holding Clara's hand in her own, her voice steady, her presence calm.

Clara's once sharp eyes softened as she looked at Eve, seeing in her the culmination of years of wisdom, strength, and compassion. A faint smile curled on her lips as she whispered, "My fierce, resilient girl... you remember our motto?"

Eve's green eyes glistened, but she did not waver. She nodded, her voice gentle yet strong, as she spoke the words with deep reverence: *"Homo sum, humani nihil a me alienum puto... I am human, therefore nothing human is foreign to me."*

Clara's gaze became distant, and she murmured, "And you understand it now... more than I ever hoped you would. When I taught you that, I only hoped you'd carry it with you, that you would look at the world with open eyes, with a heart willing to see the humanity in all." She took a shallow breath, pausing as if gathering her last reserves of strength.

Eve gripped Clara's hand tightly, her voice catching as she replied, "You gave me the wisdom to see beyond judgment, to understand that everyone carries a story, that every face I meet has struggled and suffered, like me. It's your teaching that guides me."

Clara smiled faintly, pride flickering in her eyes. "Then I did what I was meant to do."

Eve leaned close, speaking the words once more, allowing them to fill the room, as if sealing Clara's legacy within her. "I am human, and therefore, nothing human is foreign to me. I will carry it forward, Clara. I plan to live it, as you did."

With a final, contented sigh, Clara closed her eyes, her grip on Eve's hand loosening, as though passing the torch to the next generation.

In that moment, Eve knew her life's purpose would be forever intertwined with her mentor's teachings, carrying Clara's wisdom forward.
