# Clara Teaching Awareness to 16 Year Old Eve

Clara paused, her voice steady but firm. "When you look at people so consumed in their world, they don't notice the wake they create affecting all those around them?"

Eve followed Clara's gaze to the nearby road, where a rider galloped too close to a group of children playing with a hoop. The children scattered, their shouts turning to cries of alarm. The rider barely noticed, urging his horse forward without a backward glance.

"That," Clara said, gesturing with her chin. "Another example. Recklessness is a failure to consider the consequences of one's actions on others."

Eve's green eyes flashed with anger. "It's selfish. They could have been hurt."

"Exactly," Clara said. "But blaming him outright won't change his behavior. Marcus Aurelius, the stoic philosopher, taught us first to understand why people act as they do. He wrote, *'You have power over your mind-not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength.'*

That rider is likely unaware of the harm he might cause. That ignorance is the root of his selfishness."

Clara gestured for Eve to follow her to the shade of a sprawling cypress tree, where they sat on a low stone wall. "Think of disease," she said, her tone shifting to somber. "Do you know how many die because someone refuses to think beyond themselves?"

"Smallpox," Eve said, her voice low. "Cholera. So many deaths could be avoided if people just... cared more."

"Or if they understood the consequences of their actions," Clara said. "We can control how we respond. Observe, anticipate, and act with care. Be the balance the world lacks."

Eve nodded slowly. "But how do I respond without letting their ignorance consume me? It feels so... endless."

"By remembering that you are not powerless," Clara replied. You can't fix everyone, but you can set an example."

"Awareness," she said. "That is the difference between someone who acts with purpose and someone who merely reacts. Keep this lesson close, Eve. You'll need it-for yourself and for those who may one day follow your example."

As the sun dipped toward the horizon, Eve sat alone, cleaning her rifle. Clara's words lingered in her mind: *You can't fix everyone, but you can set an example.* She thought of the children, the rider, the man in New York. Perhaps she couldn't change their carelessness, but she could counter it with her own vigilance.

For the first time, Eve felt the weight of her responsibility-not as a burden, but as a purpose. She resolved to live by Clara's teachings, embodying the awareness the world so desperately lacked.
