# Chapter One: A Debt of Life

1875

Dr. Eve Garrett kneeled beside the bed, her hands gently pressed against Clara Chen's shoulders. Her voice was steady, but her eyes brimmed with emotion. "Clara Chen, you are brilliant. You know this is a tumor in your left breast. How long have you known?"

Clara looked at her with eyes that had seen too much, full of both resignation and a trace of that familiar fire. She smiled faintly, then winced as the movement pulled at her chest and back. "Oh, Eve. The back pain was the giveaway, wasn't it? Fuck."

Eve's eyes glistened. "You've been told?"

Clara nodded. "Months. Months is what they said I likely have left, if that."

Eve's heart seemed to splinter in her chest. The words were a knife, sharp and brutal, cutting through the professional mask she so often wore. She leaned forward, wrapping her arms around Clara, her embrace as gentle as a whisper. Clara, her mentor, her friend, her teacher, the person who had shaped her for nearly two decades. "Oh, my friend," Eve whispered, "oh, my mentor. You taught me so much. And now..."

Clara let out a sigh, but her eyes softened as they met Eve's. She wrapped one arm around Eve's shoulders, her fingers brushing against the back of Eve's neck. "There's still time, my dear girl. Time to love, to teach, to share."

Eve pulled back, her tears finally falling as she looked into Clara's weary but bright gaze. "You taught me to share my thoughts and feelings with those I love as I feel them. And I'm telling you now... you know how much you mean to me, Clara. You know how much these past seventeen years have meant. You know what you've done for me."

Clara smiled. Her eyes were glistening too, but with warmth, not pain. "Evelyn Garrett," she said, her voice like velvet, still rich even as her body failed her, "your mother saved my life. She gave me hope when no one else would. I owed her a life debt for her work, but you know something?"

"What?" Eve whispered, her breath catching.

Clara took a shaky breath and laid her head back against the pillow, her eyes closing for a moment. "I received more than I could ever give. From the moment I decided to pay that debt... your mother's spirit came alive in you, and you gave me so much more."

Eve rested her head on Clara's shoulder, the two of them letting silence wrap around them like a warm blanket. Eve wept softly, feeling Clara's gentle breaths and the rise and fall of her chest against her own.

"What do we do now?" Eve finally asked, her voice muffled against Clara's nightgown.

Clara chuckled softly. "Now? Now, we live, my dear. Whatever days are left for me, we live. We love. You'll keep on healing others. And I'll watch you shine until my eyes no longer open." She paused, then added, "And you'll take over the lessons, won't you? Teach those girls everything. You'll give them your fire."

Eve nodded, lifting her head, her tears dried, her eyes fierce. "Yes. I will."

Clara smiled again, closing her eyes. "That's my girl."

The room was silent again. Eve knew that the road ahead would be rocky, filled with sorrow and difficult choices. But in this moment, with her mentor beside her, she felt both the gravity of impending loss and the strength it took to carry on. Clara had shown her that even in the face of death, there could be beauty, resilience, and love-the things that made life worth every breath.
