# Chapter: Seeds of Summer

The clinic was quiet as Morgan helped Eve inventory medical supplies. Outside, Storm's shadow crossed the windows at regular intervals, maintaining her endless vigil.

"I've always wondered," Morgan said, her hands moving with characteristic efficiency through bandage rolls. "How did the summer visits with Clara begin? She and your mother seem such different people at first glance."

Eve smiled, memories washing over her. "That's quite a story. One that started before I was born, actually. During the fever outbreak of '54..."

---

Kate Garrett's hands didn't shake as she counted her remaining quinine supply. Twenty-three doses. Forty-seven patients. The mathematics of tragedy.

The epidemic had hit Silver Creek hard that summer. Kate had been the only doctor within fifty miles willing to treat both white and Chinese patients. Now her clinic was overwhelmed, supplies running low.

The door opened, bringing heat and dust. A young Chinese woman entered, carrying medical supplies with practiced grace.

"Dr. Garrett?" Her English was perfect, her manner direct. "Clara Chen. My family's trading company received word you were running low on critical medicines."

Kate's eyes narrowed, assessing both supplies and bearer. "Your family has access to quinine?"

"Among other things." Clara set her bags on the examination table. "Our networks extend across the Pacific. We believe healing shouldn't be restricted by artificial boundaries."

Kate checked the medicines with professional scrutiny. Pure quality. More than she could have hoped for.

"Your price?"

"Cooperation." Clara's eyes held steel beneath silk. "My family believes in building connections. And you, Dr. Garrett, are someone worth connecting with."

Before Kate could respond, shouts erupted from outside. One of her more difficult patients - a man who objected to sharing clinic space with Chinese workers - had returned. With friends.

Clara moved with fluid grace, positioning herself beside the door. Kate noted her stance - this merchant's daughter had combat training.

The man burst in, words slurring with fever and hatred. His companions crowded behind.

What followed happened too fast for normal eyes to follow. Clara's practice sword appeared from nowhere, Kate's surgical precision found pressure points, and four men found themselves efficiently deposited outside without serious injury.

"Impressive anatomy knowledge," Clara noted, returning her weapon to hidden position.

"Interesting combat technique," Kate replied, already checking her supplies hadn't been damaged.

They shared a look of mutual appreciation. Then turned together to treat the men they'd just subdued - fever victims all, regardless of their prejudices.

That night, working side by side to save lives without discrimination, a friendship was born.

---

"Wait," Morgan interrupted. "Clara just happened to arrive with exactly what was needed?"

Eve smiled. "Nothing 'just happened' with Clara. She'd been watching Mother's work for months, gathering intelligence, assessing character. The fever outbreak simply provided proper opportunity."

"For what?"

"For building something larger than either of them initially imagined..."

---

The fever broke two weeks later. Kate found Clara in the clinic's small garden, precisely measuring herbs.

"Your 'family trading company,'" Kate said without preamble. "It's more than that, isn't it?"

Clara's smile held approval. "Just as your clinic is more than simple frontier medicine. You're building something here - a place that treats everyone equally. That values healing over hatred."

"And this interests your family?"

"It interests me." Clara's eyes met Kate's directly. "My family's networks could help you reach more people. Help more effectively. While your medical knowledge could benefit our... other operations."

Kate studied the younger woman, seeing layers within layers. "You're very good at this."

"So are you." Clara gestured to the clinic. "You've created something remarkable here. I'm simply offering resources to help it grow."

"And in return?"

"Partnership." Clara's voice held conviction. "There are things happening - forces gathering - that will require people of principle working together. People who understand that healing takes many forms."

Kate thought about her dwindling supplies. About patients turned away for lack of resources. About a woman who brought not just medicine, but skill and understanding and possibility.

"Partners then," she said finally.

Clara's smile widened. "I look forward to a long and productive friendship, Dr. Garrett."

Neither could have known then how deep that friendship would grow. How it would shape not just their lives, but the lives of generations to follow.

---

"That's how it began," Eve told Morgan. "Simple recognition between two women who saw past surface differences to deeper truths. Everything else - the summer visits, the training, all of it - grew from that foundation."

Morgan's hands stilled on the bandages. "And your father?"

Eve laughed softly. "Oh, that's another story entirely. One involving a very surprised Pony Express rider discovering his wife's new friend had some rather unique ideas about home security..."
