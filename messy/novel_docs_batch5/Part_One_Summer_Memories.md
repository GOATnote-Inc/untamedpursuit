# Part One: Summer Memories

Eve had never seen Jessie look so uncomfortable with solid ground beneath her feet. The coast road to Monterey wound along cliffs that would have made any normal rider nervous, but Jessie's unease had nothing to do with the height.

You get used to it, Eve said, watching her friend eye the endless Pacific.

Doubt that. Jessie shifted in her saddle. How many summers did you spend here?

The question stirred memories: Clara standing at the helm of her sailboat, teaching teenage Eve about wind patterns and tide charts. Latin lessons over breakfast that somehow always turned into discussions of local wines. Sign language practice that evolved into secret conversations during formal dinners.

All of them, Eve answered finally. Can't remember a summer without her.

Your parents taught you how to survive, Clara had said once. I'm teaching you how to live.

There. Eve pointed to where the road curved around a final bend. Clara's house.

The Anderson estate commanded its own private cove, the main building a masterpiece of redwood and glass that seemed to grow from the cliffside itself.

Fancy, Jessie said.

Clara would hate that word. She calls it functional beauty - everything chosen for a purpose, nothing for show.

The front door opened as they approached. Eve's heart caught - Clara stood in the doorway, but something was wrong. The straight-backed elegance Eve remembered had given way to a subtle stoop.

My dear girls. Clara's voice was unchanged, still carrying that blend of refined education and genuine warmth.

Clara's hug felt different too - frailer, but somehow more fierce. When they separated, Eve saw her own realization mirrored in Clara's eyes.

Don't, Clara said softly. I know your brave face, dear one. I helped you perfect it, remember?

Your parents kept something from you, Clara said when they'd both steadied. As have I. But that ends tonight. There's wine breathing in the study, and we have much to discuss.

# Deeper Waters

Clara's study smelled of leather, salt air, and aged red wine.

Your father, Samuel said without turning, has completed fourteen missions for me in the past seven years. Specialized missions. Cross-border situations that required particular skills.

Eve felt Jessie go absolutely still.

This isn't just about law enforcement or politics. This is about protection, Clara finished. On a scale most people never see.

A team, Samuel added. Each member bringing unique skills. Medical expertise. Combat ability. Intelligence gathering. All working together.

When do we start? Eve asked.

My dear, you started years ago. This is just the moment you finally noticed.

# The Deeper Truth

There's more, Samuel said. Some faces you already know.

The study door opened. Eve felt her breath catch as Colt stepped in, followed by Morgan.

Six years, Eve said softly, looking at Morgan. You appeared during the fever outbreak.

While protecting your family's interests, Morgan corrected. And learning from the finest surgeon in three territories.

The door opened. Silas entered with his characteristic quiet grace, followed by a distinguished-looking gentleman and Dink.

One by one, the pieces clicked into place. Every significant figure from their lives, carefully positioned.

Six months, Samuel said quietly. Maybe eight. Which means decisions need to be made while Clara can help shape them.

I'm in, Jessie said. Been looking for something bigger than bounty hunting.

Eve looked at Clara. The mentor who'd given her so much, asking for one last commitment.

When do we start?

Clara's smile carried both triumph and relief. My dear, you started years ago. This is just the moment you finally noticed.

Time to see what they could become together.
