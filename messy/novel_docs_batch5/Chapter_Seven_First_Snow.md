# Chapter Seven: First Snow

The clinic door opened onto white silence. Eve checked her watch - 4 AM. Ghost lifted his head from Jacks old spot by the stove.

The first snow had come early this year, muffling Silver Creek in a blank canvas. Too quiet.

Riders, Jessie said. Northern pass.

Eve reached for her fathers Winchester. Ghost was already moving.

Six, maybe seven. Moving slow.

They rode out into darkness. The northern pass opened before them. The first rider emerged from the white wall.

Her mare moved precisely three steps left as the lead rider fell. Queen carried Jessie wide around the right flank. Ghost materialized from nowhere.

What followed was a dance theyd practiced a hundred times. Two women, three mares, one dog - moving like they shared a single mind.

Four minutes later, it was over.

Cattle raiders, Jessie said. Moving south for winter.

Eve was already pulling medical supplies. Two of the raiders still breathed.

Dawn broke as Eve finished the last bandage. Some moments needed no words at all.
