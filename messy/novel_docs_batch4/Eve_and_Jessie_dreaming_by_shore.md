# Eve and Jessie Dreaming by the Shore

The waves came in steadily, each one following its own rhythm, rising and falling in perfect, unpredictable cadence. Some crashed with a forceful slap against the shore, sending a spray of salt and sand into the cooling air, while others rolled gently, their crests dissolving in a quiet fizz as they reached her feet. Eve sat cross-legged on the sand, her gaze fixed on the ocean stretching endlessly before her, the soft hues of sunset mingling with the rising silver glow of a nearly full moon.

She wrapped her shawl tighter around her shoulders, feeling the faint chill of the evening settling in. Clara's mansion stood some distance behind her, a fortress set among dunes and cypress trees, its lights glimmering as shadows stretched long and slender across the beach. Despite its comforts, Eve found herself drawn to the solitude of the shore, the open expanse where she could listen to the pulse of the waves and breathe in the briny air.

Fishermen passed by now and then, moving in slow, steady steps, nets slung over their shoulders or dragging in the sand behind them. Each man received a brief nod of acknowledgment or a sharp glance from Clara's watchful protectors, two men who stood nearby, silent and still but with an air of alertness.

Eve's gaze flickered to them momentarily, then drifted back to the horizon. Tonight, the moon seemed to rise hand in hand with the sun's last rays, an unusual pairing that bathed the ocean in hues of deep purple and silver.

Here, in this quiet, she could almost forget the dangers that loomed beyond the horizon, the threats that waited in every shadow and town. Here, she felt peace - a rare and fragile thing, like the shells scattered along the sand, each one delicate yet enduring.

She thought about humanity - its steady march toward progress, how each new advancement came at a cost, sometimes more than people cared to acknowledge. Trains now cut across the landscape, breaking the vast, untamed plains into lines and territories. They made travel faster, connected places and people in ways unheard of, yet each mile of track seemed to pierce the land, tearing through ancient forests and leveling hills, leaving scars in its wake.

Then her mind wandered to property - an idea as old as civilization, but one that seemed so different here on the coast. The ocean belonged to no one and yet to everyone, its waves rolling in without permission or restraint. Property rights in towns and cities felt like a convenient illusion, drawn up by men who drew lines on paper and argued over rights that never truly belonged to them.

She wondered, too, about the people who inhabited these shores, their lives interwoven with the rhythm of the tides and the whims of nature. The fishermen, stoic and steady in their work, trudging along the sands without a thought, it seemed, for anything beyond their nets and their craft.

Her thoughts drifted to Jessie, her closest friend, and her man on the coast, the one Jessie rarely spoke about but whose presence had always seemed woven into her. Jessie's laughter, her wild stories, her resilience - they all hinted at a history Eve had only glimpsed.

She looked up, drawn back by the quiet hum of footsteps, and there was Jessie, her smile soft in the moonlight. She reached into her pocket, pulled out a small coin, and pressed it into Eve's hand with a playful glint in her eyes.

"A coin for your thoughts," Jessie said, her voice warm and low, like the embers of a dying fire.

Eve turned the coin over in her hand, feeling its cool weight against her skin. She smiled, though it was a faint, pensive expression. "What would you like to know?" she asked, looking out over the water, where the moonlight danced across the surface in ripples of silver.

"Oh, I don't need to know," Jessie replied, her gaze following Eve's out to the waves. "I just figure, if you're sitting here thinking, it must be something worth hearing."

"I was thinking about life, I suppose. How everything's changing so fast. The trains, the towns... People staking claim to land." She paused, turning the coin over again. "I wonder if it'll all mean something in the end, or if it's just... noise."

Jessie chuckled softly, the sound rolling out with the waves. "Everything changes, Eve. Whether it's men and their trains or fish and their tides. Only difference is, one keeps coming back, and the other just keeps going further away."

After a moment, Jessie plopped down beside her in the sand, crossing her legs and looking out at the horizon. "So, is the coin buying me anything else?" she asked with a grin, tilting her head toward Eve.

Eve laughed, feeling the last of her heaviness lift. She looked at Jessie, seeing not just her friend but a reflection of the strength she hoped she'd carry with her, no matter where she went or what choices lay ahead. She knew, as the waves lapped at the shore and the stars began to prickle through the fading light, that these were the moments that mattered - the people beside you, the land beneath you, the questions you dared to ask and the answers that never came.
