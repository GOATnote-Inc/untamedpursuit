# Jessie Considers Art

"One quarter a year to focus on art? What do you do, just paint and make pots and talk about feelings?"

"Yes," Eve said, repacking her saddlebags. "You got it."

"That sounds dumb to me." Jessie sprawled in the hay, arms behind her head.

"You are dumb." Eve did not look up, but her lips curved slightly. Ten years of friendship had earned them the right to this kind of honesty.

"You will love Emma," Eve added. "She is the only other woman I know who is topless more than you and openly talks about her sex life."

Jessie perked up. "What is the cost for room and board?"

"Jessie, Clara has offered to cover all your living costs the minute your training with her began."

"Seven years ago," Jessie mused, "and I still cannot figure out why she picked me."

"Because you are brilliant when you are not being deliberately obtuse." Eve checked her ammunition one last time. "Come with me? I already asked Lily. She said you could train separately, work individually."

Jessie stretched, catlike. "Ok, I will come. But I need to stop by Reno first."

Eve paused. "Reno? That military officer you have been writing to?"

"Captain Andrews," Jessie confirmed, a wicked gleam in her eye. "He has got three days leave and a private cabin in the woods."

"Just be careful. Something feels off about those troop movements Clara mentioned." Eve secured her last pistol. "I am taking the canyon route to check it out. Meet you at Lily's in four days?"

"If I can walk by then," Jessie winked, already mounting her horse. "Try not to start any firefights without me."

Eve watched her friend ride north toward Reno, while she turned Storm south toward the canyon. Ghost whined, clearly torn about which woman to follow.

"Go with Jessie," Eve ordered. "Make sure she actually makes it to Lily's eventually."

Ghost barked once and took off after Jessie. Storm shifted uneasily.
