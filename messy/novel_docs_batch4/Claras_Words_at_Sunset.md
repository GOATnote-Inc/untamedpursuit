# Chapter: Words at Sunset

The sun painted the Monterey Bay in shades of amber and rose as Clara Chen stood before her gathered friends. The event was held on the estate's western stone porch, and for a moment, Eve thought she saw the same commanding presence that had first greeted her in this room seventeen years ago. But when Clara reached for Samuel's hand, her fingers trembled slightly.

"Thank you for coming on such short notice," Clara began, her voice carrying easily across the porch. "Thank you for being my friends."

Humpbacks could be seen surfacing.

"I am dying soon." Clara's directness, unchanged by circumstance. "You know, it would seem my left tit seems to have been a little too jealous of its formerly bigger right companion."

A ripple of pained laughter moved through the room.

"Back to the speech, less tit jokes. More serious shit." Clara's lips curved slightly. "I am dying."

She paused, her gaze moving across the gathered faces - Margaret, straight-backed but with reddened eyes; Emma, sketching quick lines in her notebook as if to capture every moment; Silas, standing guard by the door despite there being nothing to guard against.

"This world can be harsh and lonely." Clara's voice caught. "Fuck. This world can be harsh and lonely."

The curse word hung in the air like a prayer.

"You all helped. You all helped make it brighter for me. I learned from all of you." Her eyes met Eve's for a moment, carrying decades of shared lessons.

"I will not be talking about my husband during this brief speech." Another pause, heavy with meaning. "You all know how I feel about Samuel. He knows how I feel about him."

Through the window, Eve could see Sam in the garden below, his face turned toward the setting sun. Even now, he gave Clara space to do things her way.

"I will give this speech tonight and then I wish to share more personal moments with you all if able."

Clara gestured to the music floating through the room. "Hans has prepared the musical score for us to listen to this weekend, and since it is playing now with the sunset and the humpback whales it seems to be making me cry."

She stopped, collecting herself. No one moved to help. They knew her too well for that.

"Emma has prepared some art to share." Clara nodded toward the covered canvases along the wall. "The food we will be serving is some of the favorites. The wine, the best I know."

A whale breached again, which seemed to be impossibly synced with Hans's music.

"Please make our estate your temporary home, as it has been mine." Clara's voice grew softer, but no less clear. "The agenda of this week and weekend is for you to enjoy yourself. There is no obligation. There is no need for words."

Eve felt tears tracking down her cheeks, but made no move to wipe them away.

"You all have loved me with your actions. You all have made me feel loved." Clara's hands, steady now, folded before her. "Thank you. Thank you."

The sun touched the horizon, setting the bay aflame.

"I have heard that some of you wish to share words publicly. I have heard that this process can help the grieving." A ghost of her familiar wry smile. "This is not an expectation."

One final "Thank you," and Clara sank into her chair. The music swelled, and Sam appeared silently at her side.

Hans adjusted the volume of his composition. Margaret moved to check Clara's comfort with professional discretion. And Eve remained still, watching the sunset paint the sky.

The world might be harsh and lonely, but now, in this moment, it was neither. It was filled with music and art, with friendship and memory, with love.
