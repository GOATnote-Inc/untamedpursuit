# Chapter: First Assessment

Jack Garrett studied the town's defensive positions while Ruby worked the perimeter. His Golden had better instincts than most scouts he'd known, and right now those instincts were mapping every approach route with quiet efficiency.

Clara's people had approached him carefully - first asking for tactical advice, then gradually revealing more about their operation. The way they'd vetted him impressed him more than any direct show of force could have.

"Eve's treating Martin's widow," their messenger had explained, referencing a former Silver Creek resident who'd relocated here. "Fever's hit the town hard."

Jack had noted how they mixed medical need with strategic information. Letting him know they were aware of his daughter's movements while simultaneously demonstrating legitimate reason for that knowledge.

Damn, he liked these people. Their intelligence was solid, their methods professional. When they warned about likely assault on the town, he took it seriously.

Ruby's ear flicked - she'd found something. Jack moved to investigate, discovering traces of recent chemical tests in the soil near the town's well.

"So that's the ticket," he murmured. The fever outbreak wasn't natural. Someone was testing delivery methods for something worse.

The bounty they'd offered for capturing one of the perpetrators alive spoke volumes - five times the reward for dead ones. These people wanted information more than vengeance.

# Chapter: Building Defense

They'd given him complete tactical control. Five experienced shooters plus authorization to bring in two additional long-range specialists he trusted.

Jack noted how they'd already equipped their people with top-quality weapons, all maintained with military precision. Daily cleaning wasn't just required - it was ingrained habit.

Ruby padded between positions as Jack outlined his strategy.

"The wind patterns favor an approach from the northeast," he explained to Clara's lieutenant. "We'll use that. Let them think they have the advantage."

The man nodded, already seeing the trap Jack was laying. These people weren't just following orders - they understood tactical thinking.

# Chapter: First Warning

Ruby noticed first. Just a slight shift in her posture, barely a sound. But Jack had learned to trust his dog's instincts above most human intelligence.

"They're testing our response," he told Clara's people. "Probing for weaknesses."

The quality of their weapons proved itself in that first exchange - precision shots driving the scouts back without revealing their full defensive positions.

Later, reviewing the incident with Clara's lieutenant, Jack found himself deeply impressed. These people thought long-term, planned for contingencies, maintained professional standards he rarely saw outside elite military units.

"Your organization," he said carefully. "It's not just about commerce."

The lieutenant smiled slightly. "Ms. Chen would like to discuss that with you personally. And with your wife, given her medical expertise."

Ruby's ears pricked forward - she'd already decided she trusted these people. Jack had learned to trust her judgment about character as much as about danger.

"I think," he said slowly, "that would be a very interesting conversation."
