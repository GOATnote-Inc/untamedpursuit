# Eve and Gus

Dialogue for a quest. For Silas, Jessie and Eve.

The early morning wind curled through the valley, carrying the scent of sage and approaching riders. Eve felt Gus shift beneath her, his attention fixed on the eastern ridge before she heard anything herself.

"Easy, boy," she whispered, letting him pick their path through the shadow-striped canyon.

Queen's distinctive stride echoed off the rock walls first, followed by Daisy's lighter step. Jessie and Silas appeared around the bend, their horses moving with practiced care over the loose shale.

"Anything?" Jessie asked, Queen settling naturally beside them.

Eve shook her head. "Too quiet."

Silas studied the valley ahead, one hand resting on Daisy's neck. "Wind's changing."

The first shot cracked against stone exactly where they would have been if they had continued forward. Gus was already moving. Queen and Daisy likewise shifted, three horses and riders becoming smoke in the morning light.

What followed was the kind of dance that could not be taught - only earned through absolute trust between horse and rider. Each time Eve thought they were cornered, a sudden swirl of dust would blind their attackers.

Later, around the evening fire, Silas cleaned trail dust from Daisy's coat while Jessie brushed Queen's gleaming flanks. Eve watched Gus grazing nearby, his dark coat painted gold by sunset.

"Your boy earned his oats today," Jessie said, following her gaze.

Eve smiled. "He always does."

"How did you and Gus meet?" Silas said.

"Long story."
