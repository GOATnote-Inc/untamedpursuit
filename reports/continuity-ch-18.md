## Errors (must fix)

### 1. Crew Count Discrepancy: 12 vs. 8 ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Changed "twelve" to "eight" (line 107) and "twenty people" to "seventeen people" (line 157). Matches ch-20 and ch-21.
- **Category**: Factual inconsistency (cross-chapter)
- **Draft says**: "A crew of twelve under a captain Thomas had selected through three months of inquiry" (line 107). Samuel also calculates "twenty people for five months at sea" (line 157), which requires 12 crew + 8 passengers = 20.
- **Later chapters say**: Ch-20 (line 59): "The crew -- eight men, all chosen by Thomas." Ch-20 (line 61): "how much water fifteen souls consumed per day." Ch-21 (line 76): "The crew stood in a loose line -- eight men."
- **Location**: Lines 107, 157
- **Impact**: The manifest is a formal document read on-page. "Twelve" is stated clearly. But ch-20 and ch-21 both independently confirm "eight" crew members. The total headcount also shifts: with 8 crew, the company is 16 people (Clara, Samuel, Harper, 4 Harper men, Lily, captain, 8 crew = 16), not 20. The "fifteen souls" in ch-20 is close to 16 but not exact -- possibly excluding the captain or Lily from the water calculation. Either ch-18's "twelve" or ch-20/21's "eight" must be corrected. Since two later chapters agree on eight, the error is most likely in ch-18.
- **Recommended fix**: Change "twelve" to "eight" in ch-18 line 107, and change "twenty people" to a consistent number (likely "sixteen" or "fifteen") in line 157.

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. "Five months at sea" Provision Estimate
- **Category**: Travel time / logistics
- **Draft says**: "Provisions for five months" (manifest, line 109) and "feed twenty people for five months at sea" (line 157).
- **Concern**: The voyage from Philadelphia to the California coast via Panama was typically 4-6 months total in this era, but the company will disembark the Caroline at the Chagres River (ch-23 confirms the Caroline returns to Kingston under the captain while the company crosses overland). The sea portion is Philadelphia to Panama (~3-4 weeks) plus Panama City to California (~6-8 weeks), totaling roughly 2-3 months of actual sailing. Five months of provisions is generous but defensible as a safety margin for delays, weather, and the possibility of extended coastal sailing. However, "five months at sea" slightly overstates the actual sea time. This is a judgment call for the author.

### 2. Callaghan's Knowledge: "Miss Chen's man"
- **Category**: Scene-internal knowledge
- **Draft says**: Callaghan relays "Miss Chen's man was by. Two o'clock at the townhouse" (line 33).
- **Concern**: This implies Callaghan knows (a) who Clara Chen is, (b) that she has "a man" who acts as a messenger, and (c) that Samuel meets at her townhouse. This is plausible given Samuel has been working with Clara's network since ch-12 (cargo transfer through the warehouse), and Callaghan is now managing the warehouse with full knowledge of the accounts. However, ch-18 also says Callaghan "had not asked where Samuel was going, or why, or for how long" (line 37), which characterizes him as discreet. The tension between knowing enough to relay Clara's message and not asking questions is consistent with his character (established in ch-12 and ch-15). No error, but worth noting that Callaghan's awareness of Clara's network is growing -- this should be tracked.

### 3. Thomas's Physical Change Across the Winter
- **Category**: Character description
- **Draft says**: "Thomas was leaner, as though the winter had burned something in him that had not been replaced" (line 63). "A tightness in the shoulders, perhaps. A restraint in the face that had not been there when the grin came easily."
- **Bible says**: Thomas profile does not describe weight change or physical transformation. However, the jealousy arc calls for visible signs of emotional toll.
- **Assessment**: This is new characterization, not a contradiction. The physical change is narratively appropriate for the jealousy seeds. Track as a new fact if the chapter is approved.

### 4. "The same room Samuel remembered from the autumn" / "a side table that had not been there in October"
- **Category**: Timeline / setting
- **Draft says**: Samuel remembers the office from "the autumn" and notes a side table "that had not been there in October" (line 61).
- **Previous chapters**: Samuel was in or near Thomas's office in ch-07 (Autumn 1846, briefly passing through) and definitively inside the office in ch-15 (Autumn 1846, invited by Clara). Both are set in autumn. "October" as the reference point is plausible for ch-07 (autumn) or ch-15 (also autumn, but more likely November-ish based on sequence). The reference is slightly imprecise but not contradictory.
- **Assessment**: Minor. The author may wish to clarify whether "October" refers to the ch-07 visit or the ch-15 visit. Either works within the autumn 1846 window.

### 5. Townhouse Description: "brick facade" vs. "red brick"
- **Category**: Physical description
- **Draft says**: "brick facade, white shutters, the garden wall along the side" (line 53).
- **Bible says**: "red brick, white shutters, green (old copper) front door" (locations.md). Ch-07 (line 26): "three stories of red brick with white shutters and a front door painted the green of old copper." Ch-10 (line 29): "the red brick warming in autumn sun, the white shutters."
- **Assessment**: Ch-18 says "brick facade" without specifying color. This is not a contradiction -- Samuel has seen the house many times and "brick facade" is a natural shorthand. However, every other description specifies "red brick." The omission of "red" is a stylistic choice, not an error. No fix required, but the author may wish to add "red" for consistency with the established visual.

### 6. George's Greeting: Tail Wag
- **Category**: Animal behavior consistency
- **Draft says**: George greets Samuel with "his tail moving once" (line 53).
- **Previous chapters**: Ch-02 (line 112): George's "tail thumped once against the carpet and was still." Ch-07 (line 30): "George's muzzle pressed into his palm, warm and brief." Ch-08 (line 97): "one press of the muzzle against Samuel's palm." Ch-09 (line 134): "nosing his wrist once." George's greetings have been progressively more casual/abbreviated as his familiarity with Samuel grows.
- **Assessment**: The tail wag without a muzzle press represents a further evolution -- George is so familiar with Samuel that a minimal tail movement suffices. This is consistent with the pattern of increasingly casual greetings. No error.

### 7. "Three months of inquiry" for the Captain
- **Category**: Timeline consistency
- **Draft says**: Thomas selected the captain "through three months of inquiry" (line 107).
- **Timeline**: The decision to go west was made in ch-16 (Late Autumn 1846 / November). Ch-18 is Late March 1847. That is approximately 4-5 months, not 3. Thomas's captain search may have started after the initial decision was made but not immediately -- perhaps January, after the fleet vessel was further along. This is plausible but slightly compressed.
- **Assessment**: Minor. "Three months" is defensible if Thomas began the captain search in December or January rather than immediately upon the November decision.
