## Errors (must fix)

### 1. [Scene-internal knowledge] Thomas's singing assessment may exceed his information ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Changed "I haven't heard you sing like that in a while" to "Harper tells me you sang like you used to" — Thomas's assessment now attributed to Harper (who was backstage at the performance).

- **Draft says** (line 76): Thomas says "I haven't heard you sing like that in a while. Confident. Bold."
- **Issue**: Thomas was NOT present at the Golden Compass performance in Ch-04. The audience consisted of Samuel, the ~60 subscribers, and George. Clara has not described her singing quality to Thomas at any point in this scene -- she has only reported that Samuel chose the Golden Compass, recognized the curation, and wept. Thomas's assessment of her singing as "Confident. Bold" implies direct knowledge of this specific performance's quality.
- **Bible says**: Thomas has attended Clara's previous Golden Compass performances (thomas.md continuity notes: "Has attended Clara's previous Golden Compass performances"). This makes "I haven't heard you sing like that in a while" defensible as a comparison to past performances he attended. However, "Confident. Bold" applied to *this* performance requires him to have heard it or been told about it. The most charitable reading: Thomas infers the quality from Clara's affect and the "exceeded its plan" remark. But "Confident. Bold" is a specific aesthetic judgment, not an inference about outcome.
- **Location**: Line 76
- **Recommendation**: This sits on the border of error and warning. If the author intends Thomas to be inferring from Clara's demeanor (she looks transformed, rested, the evening exceeded expectations), a small clarification could anchor the line -- e.g., Thomas could frame it as inference ("You must have sung like...") or Clara could briefly mention the performance's intensity before Thomas responds. Alternatively, if Thomas is referring to a different recent performance he attended, the phrasing "like that" is misleading since it appears to reference the Golden Compass evening being discussed.

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. [Timeline] Thomas's plan delivery timing vs. Ch-03 promise

- **Ch-03 says** (line 79): Thomas promises "I will have the strategic plan -- your first task -- ready by this evening."
- **Ch-05 says** (line 98): Thomas says "I have spent the better part of a week on this, and the investigation required to support it has not been trivial."
- **Issue**: Thomas promised the plan the same evening he delivered Samuel's dossier (Ch-03). Ch-05 is set approximately a week later (morning after Ch-04, which is ~one week after Ch-01). The plan appears to have taken significantly longer than promised. Thomas's Ch-05 statement explains this ("the investigation required to support it has not been trivial"), but the missed deadline is never acknowledged on-page. Given Thomas's characterization as meticulous and reliable, it would be in character for either Thomas to note the delay or Clara to notice the discrepancy.
- **Location**: Line 98

### 2. [Scene-internal knowledge] Clara's claim about Samuel recognizing the Rembrandt "on his first visit" ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Removed "on his first visit" — now reads "Four visits — and he spent each one studying the Rembrandt before anything else." Visit count defensible (Clara owns the venue).

- **Draft says** (line 62): Clara tells Thomas "He recognized the Rembrandt on his first visit."
- **Issue**: This specific detail -- that it was his *first* visit -- was never stated on-page in Ch-04. Ch-04 says Samuel studied the Rembrandt on "four separate visits" (ch-04, line 58) but does not specify when he first recognized it as a Rembrandt. Clara could have learned this during the off-page dinner conversation (Ch-04 ends at the threshold of the private dining room), which is a plausible source. However, since the dinner itself was never narrated, this is unattributed knowledge unless the author considers the off-page dinner sufficient justification.
- **Location**: Line 62

### 3. [Scene-internal knowledge] Clara's knowledge of Samuel's specific visit count and timeline

- **Draft says** (lines 62--63): Clara says "He has been attending for three months. He recognized the Rembrandt on his first visit. He has returned four times to study it."
- **Source verification**: Ch-04 establishes Samuel found the Golden Compass "three months ago" (ch-04, line 52) and studied the Rembrandt on "four separate visits" (ch-04, line 58). These are details from Samuel's POV chapter that Clara could reasonably have learned during conversation (either during the private gallery scene in Ch-04 or the off-page dinner). This is defensible but sits in the off-page acquisition category.
- **Location**: Lines 62--63

### 4. [Scene-internal knowledge] "Departed through the private entrance"

- **Draft says** (line 136): Thomas states Samuel "was seen departing through the private entrance."
- **Issue**: Ch-04 does not describe a departure. The chapter ends at the threshold of the private dining room (ch-04, line 174--176). Thomas's intelligence about the departure route is plausible (his network or the Golden Compass staff could have reported this), but it introduces details about events beyond what has been narrated.
- **Location**: Line 136

### 5. [Characterization] Thomas's "Seen by you. Tip of the hat." phrasing

- **Draft says** (line 82): Thomas says "Seen by you. Tip of the hat."
- **Issue**: This phrasing is somewhat modern/colloquial for 1846. "Tip of the hat" as a metaphorical expression (meaning acknowledgment/respect) is period-adjacent but its figurative use feels more 20th-century. Thomas's speech patterns are established as analytical and witty, and this line is brief enough to be idiomatic, but it may warrant a period-language check. (Note: this is normally the historian's domain, flagged only because it also relates to characterization.)
- **Location**: Line 82
