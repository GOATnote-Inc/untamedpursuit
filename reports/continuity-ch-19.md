## Warnings (review needed)

### W1 -- "Five years" duration (lines 27, 51, 157) ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Added clarifying clause at line 51: "Five years — the first alone, the last four with Thomas at that desk." This establishes Clara arrived one year before Thomas joined, resolving the five/four tension. "Five years" for Clara's total Philadelphia tenure and "four years" for the Thomas/Harper partnership are now both correct and explicitly anchored.

- **Category**: Timeline / duration
- **Draft says**: Clara has lived in the townhouse and walked the wharves for "five years" (lines 27, 51, 157). Line 51: "Five years. A fortune spent."
- **Bible says**: Clara arrives in Philadelphia and builds her empire "by age 25" (~1846). Thomas has worked from her ground-floor office for "four years" (consistently across ch-08, ch-09, ch-11, ch-13, ch-14, ch-16, ch-17). Harper has served for "four years" (ch-08, ch-17, ch-20, ch-21, ch-23, ch-24).
- **Assessment**: "Five years" could refer to Clara's total time in the townhouse (arriving ~1842, age ~20), with Thomas joining her a year later (~1843) for the "four years" of their office partnership. This is plausible but creates a distinction never established elsewhere in the text. All prior references use "four years" for the partnership and Clara's Philadelphia operations. The reader has no prior anchor for "five years." The author should decide whether to: (a) align with "four years" for consistency with all other chapters, or (b) establish the one-year gap between Clara's arrival and Thomas joining her somewhere earlier in the book.
- **Location**: Lines 27, 51, 157

### W2 -- "Seven months" since meeting Samuel (line 119)

- **Category**: Timeline calculation
- **Draft says**: Clara has known Samuel's expression "across seven months" (line 119).
- **Bible / timeline says**: Clara and Samuel met during the Mercy Jane rescue in Autumn 1846 (ch-01). Chapter 19 is April 3, 1847.
- **Assessment**: If the Mercy Jane rescue occurred in September 1846, then seven months to April 1847 is correct. If it occurred in October or November, the count would be closer to 5-6 months. The ch-01 frontmatter says "Autumn 1846" without specifying the month. The ch-02 frontmatter also says "Autumn 1846." Ch-16 is "Late Autumn 1846." This is consistent with "seven months" only if the rescue is placed in September. The same "seven months" reference appears in ch-20. The author should verify the intended month for the ch-01 rescue; if September, seven months is accurate. If October or later, adjust to "six months" or "half a year."
- **Location**: Line 119

### W3 -- Clara knowing about "Samuel's father's drawer" (line 131)

- **Category**: Scene-internal knowledge / POV knowledge
- **Draft says**: "Somewhere in that mass of brick and stone, Callaghan was opening Samuel's father's drawer." (line 131). Clara thinks this in her POV interiority.
- **Source of the detail**: "Samuel's father's drawer" -- the counting-table drawer "salvaged from the Wexford counting house" -- is introduced as Samuel's private reflection in ch-18 (lines 43-44, Samuel POV). Clara was not present for this reflection. She was at the townhouse when Samuel was at his warehouse in ch-18.
- **Assessment**: Clara has visited the warehouse (ch-06, ch-12, ch-13, ch-14, ch-15) and would have seen the counting table. She knows Samuel's father was an Irish dock worker from Wexford (Samuel told her in ch-06, line 104). Over seven months of partnership, Samuel could plausibly have told her the significance of this specific drawer off-page. However, this specific detail -- that the drawer belonged to Samuel's father and was salvaged from Wexford -- is never exchanged on-page in Clara's presence. This is borderline: plausible backstory knowledge from their off-page relationship, but technically unattributed.
- **Location**: Line 131

### W4 -- "Folio" vs. "leather case" (line 161)

- **Category**: Internal consistency / object description
- **Draft says**: Line 65: "The leather case under one arm." Line 161: "The folio still under his arm."
- **Assessment**: The object is the same -- Thomas's leather case established in ch-18 (line 127). The revision notes (continuity-notes.md, line 1441) indicate this was a deliberate variation: "leather case 5->varied (case, folio after 2nd use)." However, a "folio" is a book or folder of loose sheets, while a "leather case" is a container. Thomas packed folios *into* the leather case (ch-18, lines 127-128). Calling the case itself a "folio" conflates the container with its contents. The author may want to use "the case still under his arm" or "the leather case" for clarity, since the shift could confuse attentive readers.
- **Location**: Line 161

### W5 -- POV projection onto Samuel (line 181)

- **Category**: POV discipline
- **Draft says**: "Samuel still at the stern, watching the last of Philadelphia disappear -- the city he had closed behind him three days ago." (line 181).
- **Assessment**: "The city he had closed behind him three days ago" attributes an interior psychological framing to Samuel. Clara is the POV character. She could know the factual element -- she was present at the manifest meeting three days ago (ch-18), so she knows Samuel formally closed his affairs. But "closed behind him" is an emotional characterization of Samuel's experience. This is borderline: Clara could reasonably infer this, but it edges into Samuel's interiority. The author should consider whether this reads as Clara's reasonable inference or as an accidental POV break.
- **Location**: Line 181
