## Warnings (review needed)

### W1. **[Scene-internal knowledge]**: ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Added "I've spoken with both of them" — establishes Clara's first-hand knowledge. Clara names Callaghan and Brennan to Harper without on-page source

- **Draft says** (line 75): "Callaghan manages the warehouse floor. Brennan keeps the watch outside. Their workers handle the cargo inside."
- **On-page source**: In ch-12 (Samuel's POV, lines 145 and 157), Samuel thinks about Callaghan and Brennan by name, but their names are never spoken in dialogue to Clara during the on-page conversation. Clara and Samuel's dialogue in ch-12 (lines 139-168) discusses "your dock section," "your workers," and "the ones you trust completely" -- but Callaghan and Brennan are never named to her on-page.
- **Mitigation**: Between ch-12 and ch-13, there is a substantial time gap (roughly 8am to 3:30pm). The night operation requires detailed coordination -- Clara would naturally need to know the names and roles of the key workers. An off-page planning conversation with Samuel is almost certain. Clara also visited the warehouse in ch-06, where "A foreman touched his cap" (ch-06, line 38) -- though that foreman is not named.
- **Assessment**: Low risk. The time gap and operational necessity make this highly plausible. Author may wish to add a brief interior beat (e.g., "the names Samuel had given her that morning") to close the gap cleanly, but the chapter functions without it.

### W2. **[Scene-internal knowledge]**: Clara states inspection order (Meridian first, Cathay, Osprey last) without on-page source

- **Draft says** (line 181): "The inspectors will begin with the Meridian. Day after tomorrow. Thomas will manage the legal defense at each hearing. The Cathay follows. The Osprey will be last."
- **On-page source**: Thomas's Scene 1 dialogue (lines 41-46) discusses the filing timeline and inspector names but does not specify the inspection order. Harper's ch-11 report (line 93) names the three ships but does not specify order. The order (Meridian, Cathay, Osprey) is new information that first appears when Clara states it.
- **Mitigation**: As fleet owner and strategic planner, Clara could have decided the order herself or discussed it with Thomas during the afternoon. The strategic logic is clear: the Osprey goes last because it is the vulnerable ship, giving Thomas maximum time to build defenses.
- **Assessment**: Low risk. Author may wish to add a line in the Thomas scene establishing that they discussed the inspection sequence, or frame Clara's statement as her own strategic decision.

### W3. **[Continuity -- minor]**: "The property we sold Taylor. Last year. My recommendation" -- Thomas's specific recommendation not established in earlier chapters

- **Draft says** (line 55): Thomas claims the warehouse sale was "My recommendation, as I recall."
- **Earlier chapters say**: Ch-02, line 122: "We sold him a warehouse last year." Thomas delivers this information to Clara, but the pronoun "we" and the context do not specify who recommended the sale. The bible (samuel-taylor.md) says "Clara's network sold him a warehouse at below-market price" without attributing the recommendation.
- **Mitigation**: Thomas manages Clara's holdings (established in his character profile). It is entirely consistent with his role that he would have recommended the sale. The continuity notes (line 1027) flag this as a new detail that should be added to Thomas's profile. The detail also serves dual-frequency purposes: Thomas built the tool that replaced him.
- **Assessment**: Low risk. Not a contradiction -- it's a new detail that fills a plausible gap. The bible should be updated to note that Thomas recommended the warehouse sale to Samuel.

### W4. **[Physical detail]**: George described near "cold fireplace" in Clara's rooms at line 69, but at "hearth" in Thomas's office earlier

- **Draft says** (line 69): George is "in his place near the cold fireplace in her rooms." Earlier (line 33): George "crossed to the hearth and dropped beside the fireplace" in Thomas's office.

The transition from Thomas's office to Clara's private rooms is clear (Scene 1 ends at line 63, Scene 2 begins at line 67 in "the quiet rhythm of a day"). George moving between rooms is normal behavior.
- **Assessment**: No contradiction. Noted for clarity -- the "cold fireplace" detail confirms Clara's rooms are a separate space from Thomas's office.
