## Scene-Internal Knowledge Verification

### 1. Samuel knows about Clara and Lily finding each other "in a hold in the dark" (line 111)

**The claim**: "the two instruments finding each other as the two women had found each other in a hold in the dark"

**Source trace**: This is Samuel's interiority. The kidnapping of Clara and Lily (six days in a ship's hold) is described in Clara POV chapters (ch-17, ch-19) and in the bible. I searched all Samuel POV chapters prior to ch-20 (ch-04, ch-07, ch-10, ch-12, ch-15, ch-18) and found no on-page scene where Samuel is told the details of the kidnapping. He knows Lily (met her at the Golden Compass, ch-04) and has spent seven months in Clara's circle, so off-page acquisition is plausible. However, the specific phrasing "found each other in a hold in the dark" implies detailed knowledge of how they met during captivity, which has not been shown on-page for Samuel.

**Verdict**: Warning (not hard error). The knowledge is plausible given seven months of courtship and close proximity, but the specific detail of how Clara and Lily "found each other" has never appeared in a Samuel POV chapter. This is borderline -- it could be backstory Samuel has absorbed, but it reads as though the narrator is importing information from Clara's POV. Consider either: (a) adding a brief reference in an earlier Samuel chapter where Clara or Lily mentions the hold, or (b) softening to something Samuel could observe rather than know (e.g., "the bond forged in something deeper than rehearsal").

### 2. Samuel knows Lily "survived six days in a hold" (line 153)

**The claim**: "A woman who had survived six days in a hold, chosen to cross an ocean, asked to learn weapons."

**Source trace**: Same issue as above. This appears in Samuel's interiority following Harper's dialogue about Lily's weapons training. Harper does not mention the "six days" or "the hold" in his dialogue in this scene -- he says only "Miss Montgomery asked. I teach." and calls her "the bravest person on this ship." The specific fact "six days in a hold" is never stated on-page to Samuel or in his presence.

**Verdict**: Warning. Same reasoning as above. The detail is plausibly known to Samuel at this point in the story, but the specific "six days" figure has not been established in any Samuel POV or dialogue he's witnessed.

## Errors (must fix)

### 1. Head count: "fifteen souls" may be arithmetically incorrect
- **Draft says**: "how much water fifteen souls consumed per day" (line 61)
- **Draft also says**: "The crew -- eight men, all chosen by Thomas" + "Harper's four men" + the captain (described separately from the crew, line 59) + Harper (described separately from his men, line 79) + Clara + Samuel + Lily = 17 people
- **Outline says**: "the arithmetic of sustaining twelve people" (outline ch-20, beat 2)
- **Location**: Line 61
- **Analysis**: The natural reading of the chapter's own text produces 17 humans aboard (captain + 8 crew + Harper + 4 Harper's men + Clara + Samuel + Lily). The number "fifteen" works only if the captain is counted among the "eight men" crew AND Harper is counted among "his four men" -- but the text grammatically separates them: "The captain ran his ship" (separate subject) and "The crew -- eight men" (different group); "Harper... directing his men" (separate from the four). Meanwhile the outline says "twelve," which is a third different number. This needs reconciliation.
- **Recommendation**: Either adjust the text to clarify who is included in the count (e.g., "the captain and crew of eight, Harper and his four men, Clara, Lily, and himself -- fifteen souls" if that's the intended grouping) or correct the number to match the actual complement described. If the captain is one of the eight, and Harper is one of the four, the total is 8 + 4 + 3 = 15, which works -- but the text should not describe the captain and Harper as separate entities with separate subordinates if they are meant to be counted within those groups.
