## Voice Distinctiveness

### Samuel Taylor (POV)
- **Voice consistent with profile?** Yes
- **Speech pattern match**: Samuel's narration is precise, candid, and observant without being verbose. His dialogue is direct and weighted: "I would prefer not to be sent below again" carries his characteristic combination of restraint and clarity. The self-awareness in "the uselessness" — naming his own impotence during the fight — matches his profile's "candid and self-aware" descriptor. His humor is absent in this chapter, which is appropriate given the emotional weight of what he is processing.
- **Could this dialogue be swapped with another character?** No. Samuel's dialogue is distinctly his — spare, honest, comfortable with silence. His line "Then I will be beside you" is a commitment delivered without ornament, which is quintessentially Samuel.

### Clara Chen
- **Voice consistent with profile?** Yes
- **Speech pattern match**: Clara speaks in measured, layered sentences. "I sent you below because Lily needed someone she trusted in the hold. And because the provisions and the arms are worth more than a boarding line" — surface pragmatism concealing deeper care. "The Isthmus will be different. There will be no below" carries subtext beneath a factual statement. Her dark humor appears in "Harper is an optimist." Her father's voice surfaces naturally in the Kingston chandlery exchange.
- **Could this dialogue be swapped with another character?** No. Clara's silk-and-steel register is distinct throughout.

### Harper
- **Voice consistent with profile?** Yes
- **Speech pattern match**: Professional, restrained, deferential. "Miss Chen gives the orders. But I will note your preference" — the formal address, the quiet acknowledgment, the silence that communicates more than words. His Isthmus briefing is stripped-down and factual: "Five days if the weather holds. Seven if it does not. Ten if someone falls ill." No wasted words.
- **Could this dialogue be swapped with another character?** No. Harper's professional cadence is unmistakable.

### Lily Montgomery
- **Voice consistent with profile?** Yes
- **Speech pattern match**: Poetic, contemplative, grounded. "Rifles together — that is discipline. Single shots — those are Clara's" demonstrates her observational precision. "He wanted to teach me what to hear. So I would never have to wonder. I used to wonder" — spare, weighted with experience, natural imagery absent here because the context is violence not nature, but the quiet firmness is present.
- **Could this dialogue be swapped with another character?** No. Lily's voice is distinctly her own — gentle surface, iron beneath.

## Motivation & Behavior

### Samuel Taylor
- **Action in this chapter**: Processes being sent below during the fight; goes ashore with Clara in Kingston; asks Harper not to be sent below again; shares what Lily told him with Clara; sits with Clara at the rail in the evening.
- **Documented motivation**: To build something ethical and enduring; to be a competent companion and partner to Clara.
- **Alignment**: Strong. Samuel's request to Harper ("I would prefer not to be sent below again") serves his desire to be useful and present rather than protected. His sharing of Lily's words with Clara is an act of candor — giving Clara information about her own people that she might not have received otherwise. His willingness to sit in silence with Clara serves the courtship arc.
- **If misaligned**: N/A — all behavior is well-motivated.

### Clara Chen
- **Action in this chapter**: Inspects goods in Kingston; explains her father's connection to the harbor; tells Samuel why she sent him below; reaches for his hand at the quay.
- **Documented motivation**: Strategic brilliance masked by grace; need for control; building something that protects the vulnerable.
- **Alignment**: Strong. Clara's explanation for sending Samuel below is both strategic and personal — she names the tactical reasons while her body language communicates the emotional ones. Her hand finding his on the crate marks an incremental release of control in the courtship.

### Harper
- **Action in this chapter**: Arranges reprovisioning; briefs Samuel on the Isthmus; acknowledges Samuel's request.
- **Documented motivation**: Quiet, unwavering loyalty; duty to Clara's safety.
- **Alignment**: Strong. Harper preparing for the Isthmus with meticulous detail is the man doing what he was made for. His deference to Clara's authority ("Miss Chen gives the orders") is consistent while his acknowledgment of Samuel's request shows his capacity for personal connection.

### Lily Montgomery
- **Action in this chapter**: Translates the sounds of the fight for Samuel from the hold; reveals Harper's teaching.
- **Documented motivation**: Resilience expressed through beauty; choosing strength over fear.
- **Alignment**: Strong. Lily translating violence into information — using Harper's teaching in a real crisis — is the direct payoff of "Not out of fear. Out of choice." She is not passive in the hold; she is applying her training to a different register.

## Internal Contradictions (Positive)

1. **Samuel's combat training vs. his helplessness in the hold**: A boxing-trained man with nightly weapons practice stood in a hold with a pistol he never fired. The contradiction between his capability and his enforced passivity drives the chapter's emotional engine. He knows why Clara sent him below (tactically sound), and he hates it anyway. This is authentic — understanding a decision rationally does not prevent it from wounding emotionally.

2. **Lily's gentleness vs. her combat literacy**: The artist who heals through beauty sits in a hold during a battle and translates gunfire into tactical information. She is both vulnerable (holding her sketchbook as comfort) and formidable (identifying Clara's Colts by their firing pattern). The contradiction is earned by her arc — "Not out of fear. Out of choice."

3. **Clara's tenderness through pragmatism**: "I sent you below because Lily needed someone she trusted" — framing emotional care as tactical deployment. Clara cannot say "I wanted to keep you safe" because that would acknowledge a vulnerability she isn't ready to name. So she names the strategic reasons, and her eyes carry the rest.

4. **Samuel's generosity toward Thomas**: Samuel writes Thomas's coded letter, uses Thomas's ink, follows Thomas's instructions — and feels gratitude for the man's infrastructure. The reader, armed with the jealousy thread, sees the irony that Samuel cannot. A generous man being generous toward a man who will betray him.

## Experiential Grounding

### 1. Samuel's memory of the pirate fight from below
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel stood in the provision hatch with a pistol he never fired, listening to the sounds of the battle above — shots, George's weight hitting something, a shout cut short, a pike scraping across planking.
- **On-page source**: Ch-21 (Clara POV), lines 88-96: Clara sends Samuel below with instructions to secure the provision hatch. Lines 157-158: Clara goes below after the fight and finds "Samuel standing with a pistol and Lily sitting with her back to the bulkhead."
- **Match**: Yes. Samuel was sent below (confirmed in ch-21, line 90-96). The sounds he describes — volleys, single shots, George attacking, grappling — are consistent with the battle described in ch-21 (lines 117-134). His description of hearing them through the planking is consistent with being below.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED. Samuel was present in the hold. The sounds match the events described in ch-21 from Clara's perspective. The experience of hearing but not seeing is a valid rendering of the same scene from below.

### 2. Samuel's memory of Lily in the hold during the fight
- **Claimed experience**: Lily sat with her back against the bulkhead, Ajax pressed against her leg, sketchbook closed in her lap.
- **On-page source**: Ch-21 (Clara POV), line 158: Clara finds "Lily sitting with her back to the bulkhead and Ajax pressed against her leg and her sketchbook closed in her lap."
- **Match**: Yes. Samuel's memory matches Clara's observation exactly — which it should, since they were in the same space.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED. Both Samuel and Clara describe the same scene from different vantage points. Samuel was present and would have observed Lily's position.

### 3. Samuel's knowledge that Lily "survived six days in a ship's hold" (line 46)
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel thinks of Lily as "A woman who had survived six days in a ship's hold."
- **On-page source**: No on-page scene shows Samuel being told this specific detail. The "six days" appears in:
  - Ch-17 (Clara POV), line 154: Clara thinks "the survivor who had endured six days"
  - Ch-19 (Clara POV), line 139: "Six days in the dark"
  - Ch-20 (Samuel POV), line 153: "A woman who had survived six days in a hold" — but Harper's dialogue (line 151) only says "She is the bravest person on this ship," never mentioning "six days"
  - No Samuel POV chapter prior to ch-20 contains this information being shared with him
- **Match**: No on-page source for Samuel knowing the specific duration of "six days."
- **Verdict**: UNGROUNDED. This is the knowledge leak flagged in the user's context. Samuel knows Lily survived something (Lily's own words in the hold, "I used to wonder," imply a previous traumatic experience with similar sounds), and he may know broadly that she was kidnapped. But the specific detail of "six days in a ship's hold" has never been shared with him on-page. Harper did not mention the duration. Lily's dialogue in this chapter ("I used to wonder") implies a past experience but does not specify six days. This appears to be author knowledge leaking into Samuel's interiority. **Note**: This same issue also exists in ch-20, line 153, where it first appears in Samuel's narration.

### 4. Samuel's memory of Clara after the fight — "Clara's boots on the companionway steps, and her face in the hatch, and a stillness behind her eyes"
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel remembers Clara coming down to the hold after the battle.
- **On-page source**: Ch-21 (Clara POV), lines 157-158: "had gone below before anything else, found Samuel standing with a pistol... Samuel had not asked what happened. He had looked at her face and said nothing."
- **Match**: Yes. The events match — Clara came below, Samuel saw her face. The "stillness behind her eyes" is Samuel's interpretation of what he saw, which is a valid internal reaction to observable behavior.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED. Samuel was present. Clara came to him. His observation of her expression is experientially valid.

### 5. Samuel's memory of "the first night" — Clara looking at the children pressed against George, her hands still warm from fighting (line 152)
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel remembers seeing Clara's expression on the first night (the Night Watch rescue), when she looked at the children pressed against George and her hands were still warm from fighting.
- **On-page source**: Ch-01 (Clara POV), lines 98-106: After the fight, George positions himself near the girls. The smaller girl buries her fingers in his coat. Clara gives the hold signal and glances at George "still standing guard, the smaller girl now sitting against his side." Line 132: Samuel looks at "the girls still huddled against George."
- **Match**: Partially. Samuel was present at the scene and could have observed Clara looking at the children near George. The children being "pressed against George" is confirmed (ch-01, lines 98, 106, 132). However, "her hands were still warm from the fighting" is a tactile detail Samuel could not directly know — he did not touch her hands in ch-01 (Clara gives him a card at line 130, but there is no description of their hands touching). This is a reasonable inference (she had just been firing Colts), but it reads as tactile knowledge rather than inference.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED WITH MINOR CAVEAT. The visual memory (Clara looking at children pressed against George) is grounded — Samuel was present and could see this. The phrase "pressed against George" now correctly matches the source material (the previous "kneeling beside children" error has been fixed). The "hands still warm from the fighting" is a borderline inference — poetically defensible (he knows she was just shooting), but technically a tactile detail he did not experience. This is a stylistic choice rather than a factual error, and most readers would accept it as Samuel's reasonable reconstruction.

### 6. Samuel's awareness of Harper's teaching of Lily — "Harper taught me what those sounds mean"
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel learns in this scene (from Lily's own words) that Harper trained Lily to identify combat sounds.
- **On-page source**: This is present-tense dialogue in ch-22 — Lily tells Samuel directly. Additionally, in ch-20 (Samuel POV), lines 149-151, Harper confirms he has been training Lily: "Once a week through the autumn and winter. Rifle first. Then the knife. Then the dog."
- **Match**: Yes. Samuel has been told by both Harper (ch-20) and Lily (this chapter) about Harper's training.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED. Samuel is receiving information directly from Lily in the scene, and he already knew about Harper's training from ch-20.

### 7. Samuel's knowledge about Jamaica's emancipation — "Jamaica had ended slavery nine years ago. Samuel knew this through the ledger"
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel knows about Jamaica's emancipation through his work as a businessman and trader.

- **Match**: Yes. A shipping businessman trading in the 1840s would know about Jamaica's emancipation — it affected shipping routes, trade regulations, and labor markets. "Through the ledger" is consistent with Samuel's character.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED. This is within Samuel's professional knowledge base.

### 8. Samuel's knowledge of Clara's father trading in Kingston — "Your father traded here"

- **On-page source**: Ch-06 (Clara POV), lines 118-120: Clara tells Samuel about her father's trading connections ("Canton, Japan, Philadelphia"). ### 9. Samuel's thought about Thomas's correspondence codes

### 10. Samuel's observation that Clara "bought the way she fought — the transaction completed before the seller had finished his pitch"
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel compares Clara's purchasing style to her combat style.
- **On-page source**: Samuel witnessed Clara fight in ch-01 (the Night Watch). He has also been aboard the Caroline for weeks and has seen Clara in various operational contexts.
- **Match**: Yes. Samuel has seen Clara fight and can draw this comparison.
- **Verdict**: GROUNDED. Samuel has the experiential basis for this comparison.

### 11. Lily's line "I used to wonder" and Samuel's understanding of its weight
- **Claimed experience**: Samuel understands that Lily's words refer to a past experience of hearing violence she could not see.
- **On-page source**: Lily's dialogue in this chapter provides the context directly. She says Harper taught her "what those sounds mean" so she would "never have to wonder." The implication of a prior traumatic experience is clear from her words.
- **Match**: Partially. Samuel could reasonably understand that Lily had a previous experience where she wondered about sounds of violence. However, the specificity of his thought at line 46 ("six days in a ship's hold") exceeds what Lily has shared with him.
- **Verdict**: The emotional understanding is GROUNDED (Lily's words imply it directly). The specific factual claim ("six days in a ship's hold") is UNGROUNDED (see item 3 above).

### Arc stagnation
- None. Samuel's arc advances meaningfully: from passive acceptance of being sent below to active assertion ("I would prefer not to be sent below again"), and from unspoken attraction to physical intimacy with Clara.

### Ungrounded interiority
- **Samuel's knowledge of "six days in a ship's hold" (line 46)**: Samuel thinks of Lily as having "survived six days in a ship's hold." This specific detail — the duration and the location — has never been shared with Samuel on-page. Harper's dialogue in ch-20 mentions Lily's bravery and training but not the six-day duration. Lily's dialogue in this chapter implies a prior traumatic experience but does not specify its nature or length. The "six days" is author knowledge leaking into Samuel's interiority. This is NOT a new error in ch-22 — it was first introduced in ch-20 (line 153) and is carried forward here. Both instances should be addressed.
  - **Suggested fix**: Replace "A woman who had survived six days in a ship's hold" with language that reflects what Samuel actually knows — e.g., "A woman who had survived what she had survived" or "A woman whose steadiness had been forged in something she did not name" — allowing the reader to supply the detail from earlier Clara POV chapters while keeping Samuel's knowledge honest.

### Verification of previously reported fix
- **"looked at the children pressed against George" (line 152)**: The user reports this line was previously "Clara kneeling beside children," which was a false memory error. The current text reads: "when she looked at the children pressed against George and her hands were still warm from the fighting." This matches the source scene in ch-01 (lines 98-106, 132), where the children are indeed pressed against George (the smaller girl "buried her fingers in his thick coat," later "sitting against his side with her head resting on his ribs," and Samuel himself sees "the girls still huddled against George"). Clara is not described as kneeling. The fix is accurate and passes the experiential grounding check.
