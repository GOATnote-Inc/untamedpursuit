## Chapter Summary

**Title**: The Windward Passage
**POV**: Clara Chen
**Timeline**: April 15-16, 1847
**Location**: The Caribbean Sea -- the Windward Passage (between Cuba and Hispaniola)

The Caroline enters the Caribbean. Clara shifts to close-quarters readiness. A pirate schooner closes on them in the Windward Passage. Clara commands the defense -- Colts, George, Harper's men -- and the company fights off the boarding. In the aftermath, Clara notes one crew member (Tierney) who did not fire and the suspicion that the pirates came directly, as though they knew where to find her. She dismisses both observations.

## Scene-Internal Knowledge Verification

### 1. Clara's knowledge of the Windward Passage geography

**Claim (line 140)**: "Clara had sailed these waters twice with her father."

**Trace**: Clara's father's Pacific/Caribbean trading is well established (bible: "family maintained trading connections across the Pacific, including Canton and Japan"; ch-16: father described San Francisco harbor; ch-19: "the Pacific her father had sailed"). In ch-22 (the next chapter), Clara says about Kingston: "Twice. He said Kingston was the truest harbor in the Caribbean." This confirms two Caribbean voyages with her father. The knowledge of "pirate calculus" is attributed to those voyages. **VERIFIED** -- consistent with established backstory and confirmed in the next chapter.

### 2. Clara's knowledge of Harper's quote to Samuel

**Claim (lines 180-181)**: "Open water was honest, Harper had told Samuel. She had heard it through the planking, lying in her berth."

**Trace**: In ch-20 (lines 157-158), Harper says to Samuel at the taffrail: "Open water is honest, Mr. Taylor. It shows you what it has. The trouble is what waits in the harbors." Earlier in that scene (line 127-128), it is established that "Clara and Lily had gone below with their instruments." Clara's berth could plausibly be near the taffrail (aft cabin), and she claims to have overheard through the planking. **VERIFIED** -- new detail, but the chapter provides its own sourcing. Clara was below, Harper and Samuel were at the taffrail directly above; overhearing through planking is physically plausible.

### 3. Clara's count of Colts at the Mercy Jane

**Claim (line 38)**: "She had carried six the night of the Mercy Jane."

**Trace**: Ch-01 establishes Clara carrying "multiple Colts" and dropping spent ones to draw fresh ones. The bible (continuity notes) says "custom holsters with body-mapped weapon placement" and "drops spent Colts and draws fresh ones mid-fight." The specific number "six" is not explicitly stated in ch-01 but is plausible given the established combat tactic of carrying many revolvers. **VERIFIED** -- consistent with established combat style. The continuity notes for ch-21 record this as a confirmed detail.

### 4. Clara's knowledge of Harper doubling the watch

**Claim (line 30)**: "He had doubled the watch three days ago without being asked."

**Trace**: Clara is the POV character and Harper's employer. She would naturally know about watch schedules aboard a hundred-foot ship. Harper acted "without being asked," which Clara notes approvingly -- consistent with their four-year professional relationship. **VERIFIED** -- plausible and consistent with their dynamic.

### 5. Clara's identification of Tierney

**Claim (line 132)**: "Tierney -- the quiet one, the last hired before they sailed, Thomas's recommendation."

**Trace**: Clara is the head of the company. She would know the identity and hiring history of crew members. Thomas selected the crew (ch-18: "A crew of twelve under a captain Thomas had selected"; ch-20: "eight men, all chosen by Thomas"). Clara knowing Tierney was Thomas's specific recommendation and the last hired is consistent with her role. **VERIFIED** -- plausible backstory knowledge for the company's principal.

### 6. Harper's report on Lily and Samuel below decks

**Claim (lines 156-157)**: Harper reports: "Intact. Mr. Taylor secured it. Miss Montgomery is unharmed."

**Trace**: Between the fight ending (line 134) and Harper finding Clara at the quarterdeck (line 148), there is a time gap during which Clara herself went below (lines 158-159) and Harper evidently checked or received a report. **VERIFIED** -- Harper as security chief would check on all sections of the ship after an engagement.

### 7. Clara's going below before anything else

**Claim (lines 158-159)**: "She had already checked -- had gone below before anything else."

**Trace**: This is presented as having happened between the fight and the current scene, before Harper's report. The narration uses past perfect ("had gone") to indicate this preceded the quarterdeck conversation. **VERIFIED** -- proper sequencing. Clara checked, then came topside, then Harper reported (confirming what she already knew).

## Errors (must fix)

### 1. Crew size discrepancy: eight vs. twelve ✓ RESOLVED (2026-03-05): Ch-18 corrected from "twelve" to "eight" and "twenty people" to "seventeen people." Ch-21's "eight men" is now consistent across all chapters.

- **Draft says (line 76)**: "The crew stood in a loose line -- eight men, rope-roughened and tanned from twelve days at sea."
- **Ch-18 says (line 107)**: "A crew of twelve under a captain Thomas had selected through three months of inquiry."
- **Ch-20 says (line 59)**: "The crew -- eight men, all chosen by Thomas."
- **Location**: Line 76 (ch-21); also line 59 (ch-20)
- **Severity**: This is a cross-chapter inconsistency first introduced in ch-20 and carried into ch-21. The manifest in ch-18 clearly states twelve crew members. Both ch-20 and ch-21 state eight. The ch-20 continuity notes record "Crew of eight men" as a "new detail" but do not flag the contradiction with ch-18's twelve. The ch-20 continuity checker caught an arithmetic error ("company of twelve" changed to "fifteen souls," which is 8 crew + 5 named company members + 2 dogs) but did not reconcile the crew count with ch-18's manifest.
- **Recommendation**: Reconcile across ch-18, ch-20, and ch-21. Either the manifest should say eight, or the later chapters should say twelve. Note: the smaller crew (eight) is used in all subsequent chapters (ch-22, ch-23, ch-24), so changing ch-18 to eight would be the least disruptive fix.

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. Name collision: Marsh (Harper's man) vs. Edward Marsh (Wallace's accountant)

- **[character naming]**: One of Harper's four men is named "Marsh" (first appearing ch-21 line 76). In ch-09, "Edward Marsh" is introduced as Wallace's captured accountant -- a significant plot character who surrenders intelligence about the trafficking network. Both characters are active in Book 1.
- **Risk**: Readers may momentarily confuse the two. Edward Marsh is referred to by full name and surname in several chapters (ch-09, ch-11). Harper's Marsh appears by surname only from ch-21 onward. The names do not create a plot contradiction, but the duplication is unusual given the Tierney/Brennan rename was done specifically to avoid a name collision with Samuel's warehouse night watchman.
- **Recommendation**: Author judgment. Consider renaming Harper's Marsh (e.g., to Martin, Morris, or similar) for clarity, especially since Edward Marsh's intelligence is still operationally relevant during the voyage.

### 2. "Sailed these waters twice with her father" -- new backstory detail

- **[backstory]**: Line 140 states Clara "sailed these waters twice with her father." This is the first explicit mention of Clara having personally traveled the Caribbean with her father. Previous chapters establish her father's Pacific trading connections (bible, ch-10, ch-16, ch-19) but do not specifically describe Caribbean voyages with Clara aboard.
- **Mitigating factor**: Ch-22 confirms this detail ("Twice. He said Kingston was the truest harbor in the Caribbean"), and the bible establishes Clara's father as a trader with Pacific connections, making Caribbean transit plausible. The father's scroll "survived three ocean crossings" (ch-10).
- **Recommendation**: Not an error -- consistent with the established family trading background. Should be logged in the bible under Clara's continuity notes and her father's trading connections.

### 3. Tierney as simultaneous crew member and Samuel's former warehouse employee

- **[character identity]**: Tierney appears in ch-21 as a crew member aboard the Caroline -- "Thomas's recommendation." In chs 12-15, a "Tierney" works the night watch at Samuel's warehouse. The continuity notes record that the ch-21 character was renamed from Brennan to Tierney to avoid collision with Samuel's warehouse night watchman -- but the rename actually *created* a collision rather than avoiding one.
- **Explanation from notes (line 1522)**: "Tierney renamed from Brennan to avoid collision with Samuel's warehouse night watchman (Chs 12-15)." This note appears to contain an error -- the rename was intended to avoid collision with a *different* character, but the chosen name "Tierney" is itself the name of Samuel's warehouse night watchman.
- **Mitigating factor**: It is plausible that the same Tierney from Samuel's warehouse was hired for the voyage, given Thomas organized both Samuel's workforce and the ship's crew. If this is intentional (the same person), it should be made clearer. If it is a naming error, a different name should be chosen.
- **Recommendation**: Author decision -- is this the same Tierney? If so, the dual-frequency deepens (Thomas placed his agent at both sites). If not, rename one of them.

### 4. Harper says "The passage is two days south" on the morning of the fifteenth

- **[geography/timeline]**: On April 15, Harper tells Clara the passage is "two days south." The pirate encounter then happens later that same day (midday, April 15), which means they are already in or approaching the passage, not two days away. The frontmatter confirms the timeline as April 15-16.
- **Mitigating factor**: "Two days south" could refer to the far end of the passage or the full transit, while the pirate encounter happens at the northern approach. The Windward Passage is roughly 50 miles long, so a ship could be in the general area but still have two days of transit remaining.
- **Recommendation**: Minor. The geography is defensible but could be clarified with "the heart of the passage" or "the narrows" to distinguish from the approach.

## Threads Advanced

### Clara-Samuel courtship
Clara sends Samuel below to protect Lily -- trust expressed as tactical deployment rather than sentiment. Samuel's face shows "not argument but the effort of restraining one." His compliance ("I understand") and the wordless exchange at line 96 deepen the courtship through restraint and unspoken connection. After the fight, Clara goes below before anything else and finds Samuel -- the priority revealing what she will not say aloud.

### Thomas Arlington's jealousy seeds (dual-frequency count: 100)
Two seeds this chapter, bringing the running total to 100:
1. **Tierney** (line 132): Thomas's recommendation, the last hired, positioned at the port rail during the fight with his pike unused. On first read: a frightened crew member. On re-read: Thomas's agent.
2. **The direct approach** (lines 140-144): The pirates came straight at the Caroline, not on a patrol pattern. Clara thinks "This had not felt random" but dismisses it as coincidence. On re-read: Thomas leaked the route.

### Harper's arc before death
Harper at his finest as combat commander -- predicting the starboard boarding, directing fire with "the precision of a man giving street directions," then the quiet post-battle assessment ("They were organized. Not opportunists"). Clara watching him walk away with the "unhurried stride" of four years deepens the pre-eulogy pattern. This is the last major combat scene before Harper's death on the Isthmus.

## Threads That Could Be Advanced Here

### Wallace's network
The organized pirates -- "someone is running them" -- could connect more explicitly to Wallace's trafficking operation or intelligence network. Clara and Harper note the organization but do not speculate on who. This is consistent with the dual-frequency approach (the reader should suspect Thomas, not Wallace), but a passing mention of Wallace's known Caribbean presence could deepen the misdirection.

### Clara's progressive disarmament pattern
Clara is fully armed throughout -- leather coat, four Colts, George at her heel. The pattern is consistent (she rearmed for the voyage in ch-19) but not explicitly noted in the chapter. The armament level could be linked to her emotional state with Samuel: she arms for combat but the moment of going below to find him is the chapter's most vulnerable beat.
