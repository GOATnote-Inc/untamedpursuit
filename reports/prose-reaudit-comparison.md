## Persistent Issues (Across All 16 Chapters)

### 1. Clara's Physical Invulnerability
After ch-01 (where she loses her blade and takes a knee), Clara is never physically hurt, never slips, never fails physically. In ch-08 the knife is yielded away cleanly. In ch-20 her bare feet grip where Samuel's boots slide. In ch-24 she does not slip on the trail. Her emotional constraints are well-drawn but her body is untested.

**Recommendation**: One physical cost in the late-book chapters (ch-21 or ch-23) would resolve this. A wound, a fall, exhaustion that limits her.

### 2. Thomas as Infrastructure Mary Sue
The competence trap has partially migrated from Clara to Thomas. He anticipates everything, decodes overnight without wrong turns, files everything on time, and his intelligence arrives too cleanly. His micro-moments (doorframe hand, pen pause, lingering gaze) are the series' best character work, but his *professional* competence is never tested.

**Recommendation**: Give Thomas one significant wrong turn or failed prediction. A piece of intelligence that misleads. A legal filing that backfires.

### 3. Hold Backstory Redundancy
The Lily rescue (six days, metal shard, twelve men dead) is recapped in ch-04, ch-12, ch-13, ch-17, ch-19, and ch-20. By ch-17 the reader needs one reference per chapter at most. By ch-24 they need none.

**Recommendation**: Cut all recaps after ch-13. Trust the reader.

### 4. Interchangeable Metaphors
"Living thing" (ch-20), "smile that survived everything" (ch-17, ch-19), "the jungle had not changed her" (ch-24) could belong to any narrator. Character-specific metaphors (locked doors, merchant inventory, unconscious inheritance of proximity) coexist with generic ones.

**Recommendation**: Audit all metaphors for POV specificity. Clara thinks architecturally. Samuel thinks commercially. Replace generic images with domain-specific ones.

### 5. Reflection Endings Are Formulaic
Chapters 01, 02, 03, 05, 07, 12, 17, 24 all end with a character in transit, reflecting on what happened. The series needs different exit structures — a scene that ends on dialogue, a cut to black, a shift to sensory detail without interpretation.

### 6. Thomas's Jealousy Needs a Surface Moment
Five chapters of subtext (doorframe, pen pause, lingering gaze, "uncomplicated affection" misread, chair pushed back) are well-planted. But the jealousy thread needs one moment where it breaks the surface — where Thomas says or does something that Clara cannot file as warmth. Without it, the subtext risks becoming invisible rather than building.

### 7. Bechdel Still Fails
Across 16 chapters, Clara and Lily never have a sustained conversation about anything not mediated by a male character. The ch-17 orchard scene is the closest (Lily's choice to join the voyage), but even there, Harper and Samuel are referenced throughout.

## What's Working

1. **Samuel's mother-hollow thread** carries across ch-04 → ch-07 → ch-20 → ch-24 with increasing depth. It gives him interiority independent of Clara.

2. **Thomas's micro-moments** are the series' best character work. "He conducted farewells in the language of preparation" and "I have nothing left to give you that isn't already aboard" are sentences that do triple duty.

3. **Voice independence is strong**. Speakers are distinguishable in 15 of 16 chapters. Thomas, Samuel, Clara, Lily, and Harper each have identifiable registers.

4. **Competence now has visible cost** in most chapters. The child's rejection (ch-01), the held secret (ch-03), Samuel's cost calculation (ch-12), the customs boat (ch-13), and the unresolved argument (ch-09) all make capability expensive.

5. **The yield/grant distinction** (ch-10), **the transparency argument** (ch-09), and **the locked doors metaphor** (ch-03) are genuine prose achievements that would survive extraction into an anthology.

6. **Clara's blindspot structure** — her inability to read Thomas accurately — is now consistent across ch-02, ch-05, ch-10, ch-13, ch-17, ch-19. It functions as her central flaw without the narrative announcing it.

## Recommended Next Actions (Priority Order)

1. **Clara physical cost** — Add one moment of physical vulnerability in ch-21 or ch-23 (already flagged as healthy chapters with room for a surgical addition)
2. **Thomas wrong turn** — Give his intelligence one failure in ch-09 or ch-14 (the decoded circuit should have a gap or misleading entry)
3. **Hold backstory trim** — Cut all recaps after ch-13. One reference maximum per chapter before that.
4. **Metaphor audit** — Search-and-replace generic metaphors ("living thing," "smile that survived") with POV-specific language
5. **Thomas surface moment** — One line in ch-11 or ch-16 (already LOW-priority chapters with Thomas present) where the jealousy breaks through
6. **Bechdel scene** — Draft a Clara-Lily conversation in ch-17 about something that isn't mediated by male characters (the orchard, the music, Lily's art)
7. **Reflection endings** — Vary exit structures in at least 3 chapters
