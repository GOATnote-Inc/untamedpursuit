- ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Changed "knelt beside the children" to "looked at the children pressed against George" — matching what actually happened in ch-01.

The closest equivalent is George, not Clara, being at the girls' level.
- **Location**: Line 152

Either ch-01 needs a revision to include this moment, or ch-22's reference should be rewritten to match what actually happened (e.g., Clara looking at the girls huddled against George, Clara's face in the gaslight afterward, or some other image that was actually established).

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. [Scene-internal knowledge]: Samuel identifying "George's weight" from below

- **Draft says** (line 38): "George's weight hitting something above him."
- **Concern**: Samuel is in the hold during the fight, listening to ambiguous sounds above. In the same passage, he emphasizes how the sounds were questions he could not answer -- "whose weapon, whose target, whose body falling." Then he identifies George's impact specifically. A 140-pound dog hitting a man hard enough to drive him over the side would produce a distinctive thud, plausibly different from human-on-human combat, so Samuel might reasonably identify it. However, the identification sits oddly in a passage whose entire point is Samuel's inability to identify what he heard.
- **Location**: Line 38
- **Recommendation**: Consider phrasing as inference or retrospective identification -- e.g., "What must have been George's weight" or "A heavier impact above him -- George, he realized later."

### 2. [Name collision]: Marsh (Harper's man) vs. Edward Marsh (Wallace's accountant)

- **Draft says** (line 58): "Davis and Marsh went ashore with a list."
- **Ch-09 establishes**: Edward Marsh, Wallace's accountant, captured during the Callowhill fight and turned informant.
- **Ch-21/22 establishes**: Marsh, one of Harper's four men (Davis, Marsh, Cole, Jennings).
- **Concern**: Two characters named Marsh -- one an enemy informant turned witness, the other a trusted member of Harper's team -- in the same book. This is unlikely to confuse readers in context (Edward Marsh appears only in ch-09), but the coincidence may be worth reconsidering, especially since Samuel knows both.
- **Location**: Established in ch-21; carried into ch-22 without change.

### 3. [Potential factual detail]: Harper purchasing firearms for Samuel and Lily

- **Draft says** (line 100): Harper says, "I am requesting permission from Miss Chen to purchase firearms for Mr. Taylor and Miss Montgomery."
- **Ch-18 establishes**: The manifest lists "Arms and powder for the company" among the provisions.
- **Ch-21 establishes**: Samuel was given "the pistol in the arms locker" for the fight.
- **Concern**: If arms are already aboard (as per the manifest), why does Harper need to purchase new firearms specifically for Samuel and Lily? This may be intentional -- Harper wants personal sidearms for the Isthmus crossing beyond what the ship's arms locker holds -- but the distinction is not made clear. The reader might wonder why they were not already armed.
- **Location**: Line 100
- **Recommendation**: If intentional (personal weapons vs. ship's arms locker weapons), a brief clarifying phrase would help. If unintentional, reconcile with the already-stocked armory.

### 4. [Crew count pre-existing discrepancy]: Manifest vs. aboard

- **Ch-18 manifest** (line 107): "A crew of twelve under a captain."
- **Ch-20** (line 59): "The crew -- eight men, all chosen by Thomas."
- **Ch-21** (line 76): "The crew stood in a loose line -- eight men."
- **Ch-22**: Does not directly state a crew count, so this chapter does not introduce the error, but it inherits it. The Caroline's crew is consistently eight in the narrative chapters but was listed as twelve on the manifest. This is a pre-existing discrepancy across ch-18/20/21.
- **Note**: This is logged here for completeness but is not a ch-22-introduced issue.

Samuel was below during the fight and would not have witnessed Tierney's hesitation, but he might notice Tierney in Kingston. The chapter does not reference Tierney at all. - **Wallace thread**: Kingston is a port where Wallace may have agents or interests. The chapter does not reference this connection. Given that Clara is tracking Wallace's network and this is a major Caribbean commercial harbor, a passing observation (even one Clara dismisses) might deepen the thread.
