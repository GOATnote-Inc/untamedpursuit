## Errors (must fix)

### 1. Timeline: "the weeks since the river walk" ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Changed "weeks" to "days"

- **Draft says** (line 189): "he had learned, in the weeks since the river walk, that Clara's truths arrived when the room was quiet enough to receive them."
- **Bible/outline says**: The river walk occurs in Ch-06. The outline places Ch-07 "a few days after Ch6," Ch-08 three days after Ch-07 (per the "Three days" exchange at the end of Ch-07), Ch-09 the morning after Ch-08, and Ch-10 the same evening as Ch-09. Total elapsed time from Ch-06 to Ch-10 is approximately 7-10 days -- not "weeks" (which implies at least 14 days).
- **Location**: Line 189
- **Suggested fix**: Change "in the weeks since the river walk" to "in the days since the river walk" or "since the river walk."

### 2. Timeline: "three days ago" for the housekeeper ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Changed "three days ago" to "a few days ago" — avoids the incorrect precision while preserving voice.

- **Draft says** (line 35): "the same efficient, unsmiling woman who had answered the door three days ago"
- **Bible/outline says**: The housekeeper answered the door in Ch-07 (Samuel's first visit to the townhouse). Per the outline, Ch-07 is "a few days after Ch6." Ch-08 occurs three days after Ch-07. Ch-09 is the next morning. Ch-10 is that same evening. From Ch-07 to Ch-10: 3 days + 1 day = approximately 4 days. "Three days ago" underestimates by about one day.
- **Location**: Line 35
- **Severity**: Minor -- "three days" vs. "four days" is within the margin of imprecision these characters use, and the frontmatter does not give exact dates. However, the Ch-07 "Three days" exchange explicitly anchors the Ch-07-to-Ch-08 gap at three days, and Ch-09/10 add another day. Worth reviewing.
- **Suggested fix**: Consider "a few days ago" or "four days ago" for precision.

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. Samuel's "four continents' worth of art" (new biographical claim)

- **Draft says** (line 47): "Samuel studied it with the collector's eye he had trained across four continents' worth of art"
- **Bible says**: Samuel is a Philadelphia-born self-made man, art collector who "recognizes a Rembrandt on sight, suggesting extensive education and cultural refinement." No travel history across four continents is established.
- **Location**: Line 47
- **Assessment**: Samuel is in his 30s in 1846 and already wealthy with intelligence connections. Transatlantic travel to Europe is plausible. But "four continents" implies extensive travel (North America, Europe, and two of Asia/Africa/South America) that has not been established. If this is intended as new characterization, it should be logged in the bible. If hyperbolic, it could be softened to "years of studying art" or "a collector's eye trained across galleries and auction houses."

### 2. "The hallway he had walked once before" -- visit count

- **Draft says** (line 35): "down the hallway he had walked once before"
- **Previous chapters**: Samuel walked this hallway in Ch-07 (training visit), Ch-08 (courtyard muster), and Ch-09 (midmorning visit after the battle). That is three prior visits, not one.
- **Location**: Line 35
- **Assessment**: However, in Ch-08, Samuel enters through the courtyard door (not the front hallway), and in Ch-09, Clara meets him at the front door and they go directly to Thomas's office. Only in Ch-07 does Samuel walk the full hallway from front door past the sitting room to the courtyard. The "once before" may refer specifically to this hallway route (front door to deeper rooms), which he walked only in Ch-07. But in Ch-09, the text says "She went to the door herself" and then "The office" -- implying Samuel entered the hallway and went to the office. The distinction is ambiguous. Author judgment needed.

### 3. George's greeting behavior inconsistency

- **Draft says** (lines 32-33): "His head lifted at Samuel's entrance and lowered again. No muzzle greeting, no inspection. The settled confidence of acceptance."
- **Bible says**: George's developing bond with Samuel is tracked through progressive greetings -- muzzle touch (ch-06), head in lap (ch-04), leading through house (ch-07), nosing wrist (ch-09). The bible entry for George notes "George has decided Samuel belongs."
- **Assessment**: The absence of a muzzle greeting in Ch-10 is presented as "the settled confidence of acceptance" -- i.e., George is so comfortable with Samuel that no greeting is needed. This reads as intentional character progression (beyond greeting, into family-level ease). Consistent with the established arc, but worth confirming the author intends this regression from active greeting to passive acceptance as a deliberate choice.

### 4. Clara "does not wear perfume" -- POV knowledge source

- **Draft says** (line 67): "Not perfume. Clara did not wear perfume."
- **Assessment**: This is stated as fact in Samuel's interiority, not as his observation. Samuel has been in close proximity to Clara multiple times (tea, Golden Compass, courtyard training, warehouse battle) and would plausibly have noticed the absence of perfume across these encounters. This is defensible as accumulated observation rather than unattributed knowledge. No bible entry confirms or denies Clara wearing perfume. The statement is reasonable but is new characterization.

### 5. Boston harbor painting vs. Golden Compass ocean painting

- **Draft says** (lines 47-48, 127-129): A "European harbor scene, ships at anchor in flat grey light. Boston or perhaps Nantucket" hangs in the dining room. Clara later identifies it as "Boston. The inner harbor, looking south."
- **Previous chapters**: Ch-04 describes an "ocean painting" in the Golden Compass gallery that Samuel admires, which Clara then shows him in the private gallery. Ch-10's painting is in Clara's dining room and is a harbor scene.
- **Assessment**: These appear to be two separate paintings -- the Golden Compass has an "ocean painting" while the dining room has a "harbor scene." This is fine, but the proliferation of nautical paintings could confuse readers. No bible entry tracks the dining room painting specifically. Author may want to ensure these are clearly distinct.

### 6. Thomas's awareness of the river walk disclosure

- **Draft says** (lines 141-149): Thomas tells the Cape of Good Hope story about Clara's father. Samuel contrasts Thomas's factual version with what he heard from Clara at the river -- "I can remember what others said about my father. At first I did not think it was true." Samuel concludes Thomas's version "did not contain" the deeper current.
- **Bible says** (relationship map, Clara-Thomas entry): "She had never shown Thomas the wound. She had shown him the empire built over it." This is consistent with Ch-06's closing reflection.
- **Assessment**: This is correctly handled. Samuel legitimately heard Clara's words at the river (ch-06) and is now contrasting his private knowledge with Thomas's public knowledge. The scene-internal logic holds: Samuel is drawing on his own prior experience, not accessing information he shouldn't have.

### 7. Courtyard described as "under gaslight" in Samuel's memory

- **Draft says** (line 29): "He had seen it in darkness -- the courtyard under gaslight, boot marks on packed earth, the muster of armed men."
- **Previous chapters**: In ch-08, when the muster occurs, the courtyard is described as "dark but for the moonlight finding its way past the ivy" (ch-08, line 217). No gaslight fixtures are mentioned in the courtyard across any chapter.
- **Location**: Line 29
- **Assessment**: Minor atmospheric detail. Could refer to lamplight or gaslight from the house spilling into the yard, but the courtyard itself has not been established as gas-lit. Consider changing to "under moonlight" for consistency with ch-08.

## Summary

Ch-10 is well-crafted and largely continuity-clean. The two errors are:

1. **"Weeks since the river walk"** -- should be "days" based on the established timeline (approximately 7-10 days from ch-06 to ch-10).
2. **"Three days ago" for the housekeeper** -- should be approximately four days based on the ch-07 to ch-10 gap.

The warnings are minor and largely matters of author judgment. The scene-internal knowledge logic is sound: Samuel's interiority draws exclusively on events he witnessed or plausibly learned through on-page interactions. The one borderline case (Thomas telling Samuel the dinner is a first) is plausible given the off-page time available but has no explicit on-page source.
