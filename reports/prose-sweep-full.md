## Cross-Book Patterns

### What's working

1. **Thomas is the most alive character.** Across 24 chapters, he has independent wants, visible cost, a distinctive voice, and micro-moments that do triple duty. Key beats: chrysanthemum scene (ch-11), "Then show him" (ch-11), "She builds. I hold what she builds" (ch-18), "I have nothing left to give you that isn't already aboard" (ch-19), "She did not see what the smile cost him" (ch-16). His absence after ch-19 is felt.

2. **Clara's gallows wit is her best voice marker.** Ch-14 ("undone by a man with a tape measure"), ch-21 ("a polite woman did not comment on what the dog had done to a man's throat"). When Clara is funny, she is most distinctly herself.

3. **Clara fails in ch-14 and ch-16.** The Osprey loss and Wallace diversion are real, consequential failures. These chapters prove the narrative CAN pressure Clara — it just doesn't do so consistently.

4. **The jungle (ch-23) is the first environmental constraint.** "A place that could not be shot, outmaneuvered, or hired" — this is the limitation the analysis called for. George's distress is the most effective animal-as-cost moment.

5. **Samuel's ch-15 and ch-22 show he can have independent interiority.** Father's legacy, merchant's eye, genuine physical struggle, his own wants. These chapters prove Samuel CAN be more than a camera.

### What's not working

1. **Clara's competency count is now 20+.** The original analysis found 16. Add: rescue compartment engineering, fleet management, sanctuary design, medicinal botany. All confirmed, most untested.

2. **Samuel reverts to camera in every chapter involving Clara's artistry.** Ch-04, ch-07, ch-20, ch-24 — whenever Clara performs (music, fighting, strategy), Samuel's POV collapses into admiration. He has independent interiority when thinking about himself; he becomes a lens when thinking about Clara.

3. **The authorial register leaks into Samuel's metaphors.** "The absolute, ungovernable refusal to admit the possibility of being stopped" (ch-24) — this is the author's voice, not a merchant's. Samuel should think in commercial/structural terms. He does in ch-15 and ch-22; he doesn't in ch-07, ch-20, ch-24.

4. **Operations that go perfectly are dead scenes.** Ch-03 (tea), ch-13 (cargo transfer), ch-20 (cello repeat) — when Clara's plans execute without friction, the narrative confirms rather than pressures. The best action scenes (ch-08 fight, ch-14 inspection, ch-21 pirate attack) have real resistance.

5. **Bechdel still mostly fails.** Lily's ch-04 dialogue is now her own (post-edit), and her ch-22 hold scene is strong. But across 24 chapters, Clara and Lily never have a sustained conversation about anything that isn't mediated by a male character's presence or arc.
