## Scene-Internal Knowledge Verification

### Beat 1: Carriage

**Clara's knowledge audit:**
- Clara has Thomas's report "bound in leather and thick with the work of a man who did not do anything by half measures." -- VALID. Clara received this report from Thomas. Ch-05 (line 140-142) establishes Clara ordered Thomas to prepare this report; the outline confirms Thomas delivered it.
- Clara references "the dark green silk from the tea" (line 26). -- VALID. Clara wore dark green silk to the Ch-03 tea with Samuel (ch-03, line 89). Clara POV in both chapters; she would remember her own clothing.
- Clara thinks "she had told him so three days ago" about the report being Thomas's finest work (line 28). -- VALID. Ch-05, line 154: "This is your finest work." The "three days ago" introduces a specific time gap between Ch-05 and Ch-06 that is new information but plausible given the outline says "a few days after Ch5."
- Clara reflects that Thomas "knew when Clara's decisions were invitations to discuss and when they were not" (line 28). -- VALID. Established backstory about their long friendship; consistent with years of partnership shown in Ch-02 and Ch-03.

**No issues found.**

### Beat 2: Arrival at Warehouse

**Clara's knowledge audit:**
- Clara reads Samuel's operation: "Three stories of Pennsylvania limestone, the mortar clean, the windows recently glazed" (lines 36-37). -- VALID. Direct observation.
- Clara notes employees' behavior: "careful respect that suggested they had been told she was coming" (line 38). -- VALID. Clara's inference from direct observation. Reasonable interpretation.
- George described as "a hundred and forty pounds of trained discipline" (line 38). -- VALID. George's weight established in ch-01 (line 88) and bible (george.md).
- Samuel's office: "solid walnut, not mahogany" (line 40). -- VALID. Direct observation. Also matches bible (locations.md: "walnut desk (not mahogany)") and Samuel's continuity notes.
- Maps described as "not tactical, like Thomas's, but commercial" (line 40). -- VALID. Clara comparing what she sees to Thomas's office, which she knows intimately (ch-02, ch-05).
- George greeting Samuel: "the greeting of an animal who had decided, sometime during the Golden Compass evening, that this man belonged to a kind worth approaching" (line 44). -- VALID. Clara was present at the Golden Compass (ch-04); however, this specific detail -- George's "decision" about Samuel -- raises a question. In ch-04 (Samuel POV), George's head settled in Samuel's lap (line 112), and George pressed his muzzle into Samuel's palm (line 76). Clara was not in the audience during the performance (she was performing on stage), so she would not have directly witnessed George settling in Samuel's lap. However, she would have seen George's greeting in the gallery (ch-04, line 74-77 -- though that chapter is Samuel POV, Clara is present for George approaching Samuel). She also would have seen George near Samuel for the rest of the evening (private gallery, dinner). The narrator's attribution of "sometime during the Golden Compass evening" is vague enough to be plausible -- Clara could have observed George's comfort with Samuel at any point during the evening's private portions.

**Minor concern but defensible. No error.**

Thomas told Clara exactly this in Ch-05 (lines 136-137): "Samuel was seen arriving with you. He was seen in the audience. He was seen departing through the private entrance." Clara is relaying Thomas's intelligence to Samuel.

**No issues found.**

### Beat 4: Riverfront Walk

**Clara's knowledge audit:**
- "You built something here" / "I read your operation the way you read my gallery" (line 98). -- VALID. Clara has just toured the warehouse (beat 2). Samuel "read" her gallery in ch-04 (she was present for his reaction in the private gallery afterward).
- Samuel's father story: "My father was a dock worker. Irish." (line 104). -- This is Samuel telling Clara something new. However, Clara already knows this from Thomas's report in Ch-03 (ch-03, line 46: "Father was a dock worker -- Irish, came over during the twenties."). The chapter does not indicate whether Clara already knows this, but this is acceptable because: (1) Samuel is sharing it in his own words as a personal disclosure, which is different from receiving it as intelligence; (2) Clara does not react with surprise, she "heard in this the echo of her own purpose" (line 106), which is appropriate for someone hearing the personal version of a fact she already knew on paper.
- "You asked me once, at the tea, why I do what I do" (line 108). -- VALID. Samuel asked this at the Ch-03 tea (ch-03, lines 108-110).
- "You said: because someone must, and because I can." (line 110). -- VALID. Clara's exact words from Ch-03 (line 113): "Because someone must. And because I can."
- Clara's father details: "My father was Buddhist. With Japanese elements -- the kami, the attention to harmony." (line 118). -- VALID. Matches bible (clara-chen.md): "Buddhist with Japanese Shinto elements" and "kami, harmony." This is Clara sharing her own background.
- "My mother was Christian." (line 118). -- VALID. Bible: Clara's mother was a devout Christian.
- Trading connections "Canton, Japan, Philadelphia" (line 118). -- VALID. Bible: "family trading connections across the Pacific, including Canton and Japan."
- "A Chen does not fail" (line 118). -- VALID. Established in bible as her father's phrase.
- "I can remember what others said about my father. At first I did not think it was true." (line 122). -- VALID. This is new disclosure, deliberately vague, reserved for future development (per open-threads.md, Thread #12).

**Samuel's knowledge audit:**
- "I hope to build something between us where you feel safe enough to share what you carry." (line 128). -- VALID. Samuel speaking from his own desire; no external knowledge required.

**Verification of Samuel's jaw description (line 130):**
- "the jaw that had been purple and was now merely a memory of yellow" -- VALID. In ch-01, Samuel's jaw took a wooden plank (ch-01, line 74). In ch-04, set ~one week later, his jaw was "still carrying the fading yellow of a bruise that had been purple a week ago" (ch-04, line 24). Ch-06 is set some time after Ch-05 (the morning after Ch-04). The progression purple -> yellow -> "memory of yellow" is physiologically consistent.
- "the eyes that had wept without shame in a room full of strangers" (line 130). -- VALID. Clara witnessed the aftermath of Samuel's tears (ch-04 is Samuel POV, but Clara references his tears in ch-05, line 72: "He wept." She also said in ch-04, line 162: "Your tears. Those I did not expect."). However, the specific detail "in a room full of strangers" requires scrutiny. Clara was performing on stage during the performance. She would not have directly witnessed Samuel weeping in the audience during the performance. She learned of his tears afterward -- from his wet face when she met him backstage (ch-04, line 120: "Samuel's face was wet with tears"). She could also plausibly have been told by Harper, George's handler, or inferred from Samuel's face. The phrase "in a room full of strangers" is a slight embellishment in Clara's interiority -- she knows he wept during a public performance, which amounts to the same thing. This is a borderline case but defensible as reasonable inference rather than unattributed knowledge.

**WARNING raised below.**

- "the hands that had fought six men on a dock to protect two girls he had never met" (line 130). -- REQUIRES SCRUTINY. Ch-01 describes Samuel as being surrounded by "eight, then ten, then more" men (ch-01, line 74). The chapter also states twelve men total lay on the dock afterward (ch-01, line 96). Clara counted these men herself. So why does her interiority say "six men"? Samuel's line in Ch-01 (via the ch-03 retelling) does not specify a number. The ch-01 text is unambiguous: Clara counted more than six men attacking Samuel. This is a factual error in Clara's interiority.

**ERROR raised below.**

### Warnings (review needed)

- **[Scene-internal knowledge -- borderline]**: Clara's interiority describes Samuel as having "wept without shame in a room full of strangers" (line 130). Clara was on stage during the performance and could not have directly observed Samuel weeping in the audience. She saw his wet face backstage afterward (ch-04, lines 120, 162). Describing him as weeping "in a room full of strangers" is a reasonable inference (she knows he was in the audience; she knows he wept; she can deduce the context), but strictly speaking, she did not witness the public act. This is defensible as natural inference. Author judgment call.

- **[Timeline -- minor]**: Clara reflects she has "carried [this wound] for twenty years" (line 146). Clara is 25. This dates the father wound to approximately age 5. This is the first time-specific reference to when the wound originated. It is not contradicted by any existing bible entry, but it narrows the timeline for Clara's father's story. If the author intends the father wound to have occurred later in her childhood, this number should be adjusted.

- **[Character detail -- Samuel's age]**: Thomas's report in ch-03 states Samuel is "thirty-one years old" (ch-03, line 46). The bible says Samuel was born "Circa 1810s (30s when he meets Clara in 1846)" (samuel-taylor.md). The ch-03 age of 31 is at the very edge of "the 1810s" (born ~1815) and at the low end of "30s." No contradiction yet, but worth tracking -- the bible's stated age range is "30s," and 31 qualifies, though barely.

- **[Clothing continuity -- very minor]**: Clara wears "the dark green silk from the tea" (line 26). In ch-03, Clara's dress at the tea is described as "a dress of dark green silk" (ch-03, line 89). This match is exact and correct. However, reusing the same dress for a visit to Samuel's offices is a deliberate character choice that some readers may notice -- she wore this particular dress the first time she presented herself to Samuel without armor. The recurrence feels intentional and thematic (blending registers: silk under leather coat this time), but the author should confirm this is deliberate.

- **[Disarmament pattern -- tracking]**: Ch-06's outfit is described as "the dark green silk from the tea, the leather coat over it, the sword cane against her knee. Not armored. Not unarmed." (line 26). This matches the disarmament thread in open-threads.md (Thread #11): "Ch-06: dark green silk under leather coat (blending registers)." Consistent and correctly tracked.
