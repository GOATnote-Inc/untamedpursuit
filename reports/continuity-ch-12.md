## Errors (must fix)

### E1. **[Scene-internal knowledge / Cross-reference]** ✓ FIXED (2026-03-05): Changed "at the river walk" to "at dinner". — Samuel misattributes the "worth keeping" line to the river walk instead of the dinner

- **Draft says** (line 149): "The same expression he had seen at the river walk when he had said her father sounded like someone who understood what was worth keeping."
- **Bible/chapters say**: Samuel's line "He sounds like someone who understood what was worth keeping" appears in ch-10 (line 147), during the dinner at Clara's townhouse -- not during the river walk (ch-06). The river walk (ch-06) is where Clara first discussed her father and Samuel listened, but the specific "worth keeping" remark was made at the dinner table after Thomas told the father's trading story.
- **Location**: Line 149
- **Severity**: Must fix. This conflates two distinct scenes. The river walk (ch-06) and the dinner (ch-10) are separate events with different emotional registers. Samuel would remember where he said this.
- **Suggested fix**: Change "at the river walk" to "at the dinner" or "last night" or "across the table."

## Warnings (review needed)

### W1. **[Physical description]**: Clara's dinner dress called "wine-colored silk" -- fabric not established

- **Draft says** (line 47): "Last night she had sat across from him in wine-colored silk"
- **Ch-10 says** (line 59): "A dress the color of dark wine -- cut simply, no ornament, the sleeves ending above the wrist." The fabric is not specified.
- **Location**: Line 47
- **Assessment**: Samuel inferring silk is plausible for a woman of Clara's wealth and station. However, the original description does not name the fabric. Author should decide whether to specify the fabric in ch-10 for consistency, or change ch-12 to "wine-colored dress."

### W2. **[Physical description]**: Clara's hair described as "gathered softly" -- slight compression of the original

- **Draft says** (line 47): "her hair gathered softly"
- **Ch-10 says** (line 59): "Her hair was gathered differently: softer, pulled back but not tightly, a few strands loose near her temples."
- **Location**: Line 47
- **Assessment**: "Gathered softly" is a reasonable compression of the ch-10 description as recalled from Samuel's memory. Not an error, but "softly" flattens the specificity of the original. Low priority.

### W3. **[Continuity notes discrepancy]**: Continuity notes (line 875) list "Tierney (night watch)" but chapter text says "Brennan"

- **Draft says** (line 145): "Brennan, who kept the night watch"
- **Continuity notes say** (line 875): "Tierney (night watch)"
- **Assessment**: The chapter text is correct. The continuity notes entry appears to have been written or updated after the ch-21 Brennan-to-Tierney rename (which applied to the ship crew character, not the warehouse watchman). The ch-12 warehouse watchman has always been Brennan and remains Brennan through chs 13-15. The continuity notes entry at line 875 contains a clerical error -- it should say "Brennan (night watch)." This does not affect the chapter draft.

### W4. **[Outline deviation]**: Passage time in outline vs. chapter

- **Outline says** (line 542): "Twelve hours in the hold"
- **Draft says** (lines 113, 163, 181): "Eighteen hours"
- **Assessment**: Already fixed per historian feedback (continuity notes line 935). The outline has not been updated to reflect the fix. Low priority but the outline's ch-12 entry should be updated for consistency.
