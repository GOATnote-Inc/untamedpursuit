## Errors (must fix)

### 1. [Discovery of Golden Compass] Bible contradicts chapter on how Samuel found the venue

- **Draft says** (line 52): "Samuel had found his way in three months ago through a business associate who knew his taste. He had walked past the building twice before that introduction."
- **Bible says** (samuel-taylor.md, Background + Continuity Notes): "First found the Golden Compass three months before the pivotal evening performance, drawn by a cello through an open window; visited four times before Clara's invitation."
- **Location**: Line 52
- **Analysis**: Two conflicting origin stories. The draft says a business associate introduced him, and he had walked past the building twice before. The bible says he was drawn by a cello through an open window. These are mutually exclusive discovery mechanisms. Since ch-04 is the source chapter for this fact and the bible was updated from it, the bible's Background section (line 22) appears to contain a version from earlier source material (Golden_Compass_Stage_Episode) that was not reconciled with the drafted chapter. One version must be chosen. Note: the bible's Continuity Notes (line 83) cite "book-01, ch-04" as the source but still record "drawn by a cello" -- this suggests the bible was updated from source material, not from the actual drafted chapter text.

### 2. [Scene-internal knowledge] Lily knows what Samuel was looking at in the gallery without an on-page source

- **Draft says** (lines 142-143): Lily tells Samuel, "I understand you've been studying our gallery. The ocean painting caught your attention."
- **Issue**: Lily was backstage preparing for and then performing during the time Samuel toured the gallery. She was never in the gallery with him. No character is shown relaying this information to her. Samuel's gallery tour and his interest in the ocean painting occurred while Lily was backstage/on stage.
- **Location**: Lines 142-143
- **Plausible explanation**: Clara or Harper could have told Lily about Samuel's gallery behavior between the performance and the backstage meeting. Clara orchestrated the evening and Harper was observing. However, this information transfer is not shown or referenced on-page. Per the agent instructions, unattributed knowledge is an error, not a warning.
- **Fix**: Add a brief beat establishing the information source -- e.g., Clara mentioning Samuel's gallery interests to Lily, or Lily saying "Clara tells me you've been studying our gallery," or Harper having observed and relayed this.

### 3. [Spatial continuity] Ocean painting appears in two locations during the same evening

- **Draft says** (line 64): Samuel sees an "ocean painting" in the public gallery during his pre-performance tour.
- **Draft also says** (line 132): "Clara stood near the ocean painting he had admired in the gallery -- it hung here in the private collection, he realized, the public display merely a loan from this more intimate room."
- **Issue**: Within the span of one evening (gallery tour, performance, backstage visit), the same painting cannot physically be in the public gallery and then in the private gallery. Paintings are not moved mid-event. The narration implies Samuel is seeing the same painting now hanging in the private gallery, but he saw it in the public gallery an hour earlier.
- **Location**: Lines 64 and 132
- **Note**: The continuity notes (line 250) record this was addressed in revision: "Rembrandt hangs in public gallery; ocean painting moved to private gallery for the backstage scene." However, the current draft text still shows Samuel seeing the ocean painting in both galleries during the same evening, with a retroactive "realization" that the public display was a loan. If the intent is that the painting is only in the private gallery and Samuel saw it on a previous visit, the line 64 reference to "an ocean painting" in the public gallery during this visit needs revision.

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. [Echo metaphor] "Pianist maps keys" appears in both Clara's and Samuel's interiority

- **Draft says** (line 68): Samuel thinks of Clara as "a woman who mapped holster positions the way a pianist maps keys."
- **Ch-01 says** (line 44, Clara's POV): "She had mapped these positions the way a pianist maps keys."
- **Assessment**: The continuity notes (line 247) already address this, deeming it "independent observation, not POV leak." This is defensible -- Samuel observed Clara's holsters on the docks and could independently draw a pianist comparison, especially given his documented appreciation for music. However, the identical phrasing across two different POV characters is conspicuous. Author may wish to vary Samuel's version of the metaphor slightly to make the independence clearer.

### 2. [Subjective accuracy] "Killed twelve men" overstates Clara's solo kill count

- **Draft says** (line 26): Samuel thinks of "the woman who had killed twelve men to save his life."
- **Ch-01 establishes**: Twelve men total are down, but George killed at least one (broke a man's neck, line 88) and Samuel himself retrieved a pistol and scanned for targets (lines 92-93). Clara killed the majority but not all twelve solo.
- **Assessment**: This is Samuel's subjective POV, and he was beaten and concussed during the fight, so his memory attributing all kills to Clara is psychologically plausible. In ch-01 (line 134), Samuel himself said "You also just killed twelve or more men in what seemed to be a blur." The exaggeration is consistent with his established perception. No fix required, but the author should be aware this sets a precedent for Samuel's version of events.

### 3. [Relationship dynamics] Clara calls Samuel "Sam" in the introduction to Lily

- **Draft says** (line 140): "Sam, I'd like you to meet one of my dearest friends."
- **Established pattern**: In all previous chapters and throughout this chapter, Clara addresses Samuel as "Mr. Taylor." Samuel addresses her as "Miss Chen." The sudden use of "Sam" is the first time Clara uses a familiar form of address.
- **Assessment**: This could be a deliberate intimacy escalation -- Clara is backstage in her private world, introducing him to her closest friend, and drops the formality. If intentional, it's a significant character moment that should be noted. If unintentional, it breaks the established address pattern. Author should confirm this is deliberate.

### 4. [Characterization] Samuel's description of finding the Golden Compass includes "walked past the building twice"

- **Draft says** (line 52): "He had walked past the building twice before that introduction, unaware of what waited behind the modest facade."
- **Bible says** (samuel-taylor.md): "drawn by a cello through an open window" -- implying he heard something that drew him in, rather than walking past unknowing.
- **Assessment**: Related to Error #1. If the bible version is chosen (drawn by a cello), the "walked past twice" detail must be removed. If the chapter version is chosen (business associate), the bible must be updated. Either way, the "walked past twice" vs. "drawn by a cello" question needs resolution.

### 5. [Timeline] Clara's smile "seen only once before" anchored to a specific moment

- **Draft says** (line 172): "the real one, the one he had seen only once before, in the gaslight on the docks when he had said he was surely dead if he was not at tea"
- **Previous chapters**: Samuel also spent an entire afternoon with Clara at the Chestnut Street parlor (ch-03), where they had a substantive, warm conversation ending in a handshake. It's possible Clara smiled genuinely during tea as well.
- **Assessment**: Samuel is tracking a specific quality of smile (the "real one"), distinguishing it from other expressions. This is defensible as Samuel's careful observation -- he may have seen other smiles at tea but not this particular one. However, the author should verify that no "real" or "genuine" smile from Clara was described in ch-03 that would contradict "only once before."
- **Verification**: In ch-03, Clara "laughed" at the end (line 139, "It was brief and genuine"), and earlier interactions describe her "expression" and "gaze" but not a specific radiant smile. The "real" smile distinction holds.
