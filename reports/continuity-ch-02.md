## Errors (must fix)

### 1. Internal timeline contradiction — "past midnight" opening vs. midnight clock strike at close

- **Draft says (line 24)**: "Past midnight -- perhaps half an hour since the carriage had carried her from the docks"
- **Draft says (line 158)**: "the grandfather clock began to strike midnight"
- **Issue**: The chapter opens with the time established as past midnight, then ends with the clock striking midnight. Time cannot move backward. If Clara arrived "past midnight," the clock should already have struck midnight before the scene began.
- **Location**: Lines 24 and 158
- **Recommendation**: Either change the opening to place Clara's arrival before midnight (e.g., "Nearly midnight" or "Approaching midnight"), or change the closing clock strike to 1 AM. The opening is less specific and easier to adjust without disrupting other scenes.

### 2. Book-01 timeline entry contradicts chapter text

- **Timeline says**: Chapter 2 occurs "The Following Morning"
- **Chapter says**: The scene takes place past midnight the same night as the Mercy Jane rescue, approximately half an hour after Clara left the docks
- **Location**: `/Users/thegoatnote/untamedpursuit/bible/timeline/book-01-timeline.md`, lines 13-18
- **Recommendation**: Update the timeline entry header from "The Following Morning" to "Same Night as the Mercy Jane Rescue" or "Immediately After the Rescue — Past Midnight." The chapter text is unambiguous: Clara arrives roughly half an hour after the carriage carried her from the docks.

### 3. Shifting deadline for Samuel Taylor dossier — "early afternoon" vs. "morning practice"

- **Draft says (line 74)**: "By our weapons practice in the early afternoon, I want everything you can find on Samuel Taylor."
- **Draft says (line 148)**: "Have that information ready by morning practice"
- **Issue**: Clara sets two different deadlines for the same deliverable within one scene. "Weapons practice in the early afternoon" and "morning practice" are two different times in Clara's established daily schedule (dawn blade practice vs. afternoon weapons practice). If the acceleration is deliberate (Clara pushing the deadline up as she departs), the text does not signal it. It reads as an unintentional inconsistency.
- **Location**: Lines 74 and 148
- **Note**: The book-01 timeline (Ch-03 entry) says Thomas delivers the report during Clara's weapons practice, suggesting the afternoon deadline is canonical. The Ch-01 schedule also places "Thomas delivers his report" at afternoon weapons practice.
- **Recommendation**: Either (a) change line 148 to "Have that information ready by weapons practice" to match the original deadline, or (b) if the acceleration is intentional, add a beat showing Clara consciously pushing the deadline up (e.g., Thomas reacting to the tighter timeline).

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. Thomas knows Samuel is "handsome as hell" — source unclear

- **Line**: 84
- **Issue**: Thomas says "Sam Taylor is handsome as hell." For this to be an honest assessment rather than performance, Thomas must have seen Samuel. The chapter later reveals Thomas already has property records ("We sold him a warehouse last year"), suggesting prior business contact. A warehouse sale plausibly involved in-person interaction, making the comment defensible. However, it is not explicitly established on-page that Thomas has seen Samuel.
- **Recommendation**: Low risk. The warehouse sale provides a plausible off-page source. If the author wants to tighten, a brief line like "I met the man during the warehouse negotiation" would eliminate any question. Alternatively, this could be Thomas performing — guessing based on Clara's demeanor — which is also consistent with his character.

### 2. Clara says "twelve men" — slight compression of Ch-01 events

- **Line**: 108
- **Issue**: Clara says Samuel "held ground for two children against twelve men." In Ch-01, the traffickers were "eight, then ten, then more emerging" — Samuel was initially surrounded by a subset, not all twelve simultaneously. Clara killed most of them. Samuel held his position while outnumbered, but he did not personally face all twelve at once.
- **Recommendation**: Acceptable as Clara's characterization of the encounter — she is emphasizing his courage, not providing a tactical briefing. The total body count on the dock was indeed twelve (Ch-01, line 96). No fix needed unless the author wants strict precision.

### 3. Harper "returned from the south docks" — possible minor inconsistency with Ch-01

- **Line**: 54
- **Issue**: Clara hears Harper's footsteps in the hallway and thinks "He must have returned from the south docks." However, in Ch-01, Harper doubled back before reaching the south docks when he heard the fight at the Mercy Jane (Ch-01, line 122: "He must have heard the fight and doubled back — his team would not have reached the south docks yet").
- **Recommendation**: Plausible if Harper resumed his south docks patrol after the cleanup, then returned to the townhouse. No fix needed, but the author should confirm this is the intended reading. If Harper went straight from the rescue scene to the townhouse, Clara's assumption is incorrect (which could itself be a valid characterization — Clara assuming routine operations continued).

### 4. Clara identifies Harper by gait alone through a closed door

- **Line**: 54
- **Issue**: "Harper's gait, the particular rhythm of a man who walked as though he were always counting exits." George alerts to the footsteps first, then they pass without stopping, and Clara identifies them as Harper's. This is attributed to years of familiarity, which is reasonable. However, the identification is very specific for sounds filtered through a closed door.
- **Recommendation**: Acceptable. George's prior alert supports it (George's reaction pattern differs for known vs. unknown persons, and Clara can read George). The bible also notes this as an established pattern (Harper bible, line 81: "Footsteps recognized by George in the hallway — consistent habit"). No fix needed.

### 5. Thomas's offer to research is framed as novel, but he already has preliminary data

- **Lines**: 80–82, 122
- **Issue**: Thomas says "Consider it done" (line 80) as though the research assignment is new, but by line 122 he already has property records and warehouse sale data ready: "some interesting patterns already exist in his property acquisitions." This is internally consistent (Thomas manages Clara's holdings and would naturally have this data on hand), but the transition between "I'll research him" and "actually I already know things" could read as slightly abrupt.
- **Recommendation**: Minor. The shift works because Thomas manages the holdings and would organically have this data. The teasing interlude (lines 84–120) creates enough distance that the transition feels natural. No fix needed.
