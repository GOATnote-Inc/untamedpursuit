## Errors (must fix)

### 1. [Scene-internal knowledge / New unattributed fact]: Dining room capacity "seated forty"

- **Draft says** (line 26): "the Golden Compass with its hidden Rembrandt and its dining room that seated forty"
- **Bible says**: The Golden Compass performance hall seats "approximately sixty" (locations.md). No dining room capacity is specified anywhere -- not in the bible, not in ch-04 (where the dining room is introduced), and not in any other chapter.
- **Issue**: Samuel references the dining room as seating forty, but this fact has never appeared on-page or in the bible. In ch-04, the dining room is mentioned ("I took the liberty of having a table prepared in the private dining room") but ch-04 ends at the threshold -- Samuel and Clara are entering, but the interior is never described and no capacity is stated. Samuel could plausibly know the dining room's size from an off-page dinner, but the specific number "forty" appears nowhere else in the canon and should either be added to the bible (locations.md, Golden Compass entry) or removed.
- **Location**: Line 26, opening paragraph.
- **Recommendation**: If "forty" is intended as canon, add to the Golden Compass entry in `bible/world/locations.md`. If not, remove the specific number.

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. [Scene-internal knowledge]: "the gesture she had used at the riverfront"

- **Draft says** (line 192): "He inclined his head -- the gesture she had used at the riverfront, returned now with the understanding of its weight."
- **Ch-06 says** (line 138): "Samuel inclined his head." (Clara's POV -- only Samuel's head-incline is narrated.)
- **Issue**: Ch-07 attributes the riverfront head-incline to Clara ("the gesture she had used"), but in ch-06 it is Samuel who inclines his head, not Clara. Clara may have reciprocated off-narration (ch-06 is her POV, and people don't always narrate their own gestures), but as written, the attribution is reversed. Samuel is claiming to return a gesture that was actually his own.
- **Location**: Line 192, departure section.
- **Recommendation**: Author judgment. Either (a) add Clara's reciprocal head-incline in ch-06 if it's revised, (b) change ch-07 to "the gesture he had offered at the riverfront, now returned by her in kind," or (c) accept as plausible off-narration reciprocity.

### 2. [Characterization]: "hidden Rembrandt"

- **Draft says** (line 26): "the Golden Compass with its hidden Rembrandt"
- **Ch-04 / Bible says**: The Rembrandt is hung "at eye level in an alcove" in the public gallery of the Golden Compass. It is visible to all subscribers.
- **Issue**: "Hidden" overstates the concealment. The Rembrandt is inside a private subscription society with a modest exterior, but it is openly displayed in the gallery -- not hidden. Samuel studied it on "four separate visits" (ch-04).
- **Location**: Line 26.
- **Recommendation**: Minor. "Hidden" could be read as "hidden from the general public" (since the Golden Compass is invitation-only), which is defensible. But "housed" or "concealed" might be more precise. Author's call.

### 3. [Knowledge sourcing]: Samuel's knowledge that Thomas "compiled the Wallace report"

- **Draft says** (line 44): "Thomas Arlington had compiled the Wallace report that now sat in Samuel's office."
- **Previous chapters**: In ch-06 (Clara's POV), Clara delivers a leather-bound report to Samuel. Thomas's handwriting is mentioned in narration ("where Thomas's handwriting was densest") but Clara never explicitly says "Thomas compiled this" in dialogue. Ch-07 line 44 also says "Clara had mentioned [Thomas] with the particular ease of long reliance," implying off-page conversations.
- **Issue**: The report was delivered by Clara in ch-06, and its authorship is clear from the handwriting and context. Clara has also apparently discussed Thomas with Samuel off-page. Samuel attributing the report to Thomas is plausible but technically unattributed on-page.
- **Location**: Line 44.
- **Recommendation**: Defensible. The report's handwriting, Clara's off-page mentions, and Samuel's intelligence background make this reasonable inference rather than unattributed knowledge. No action needed unless the author prefers strict on-page sourcing.

### 4. [Knowledge sourcing]: Clara knows Samuel "trains nightly with a weapons master"

- **Draft says** (line 68): Clara to Samuel: "You train nightly with a weapons master."
- **Source**: Thomas's dossier, delivered in ch-03. Clara heard this directly from Thomas.
- **Issue**: Clara openly revealing her intelligence on Samuel could feel aggressive, but by ch-07 both parties know they have investigated each other (ch-03 tea included mutual disclosure). Samuel would not be surprised Clara knows this.
- **Location**: Line 68.
- **Recommendation**: No issue. Clara's knowledge is sourced (ch-03 dossier). The open statement is consistent with the courtship's established transparency by this point.

### 5. [Timeline]: Thomas's age -- "early thirties"

- **Draft says** (line 46): Thomas is "early thirties."
- **Bible says**: Thomas Arlington born "Circa 1810s-1820s (30s in Era 1)." The story is set in Autumn 1846.
- **Issue**: If Thomas was born in the 1810s, he could be in his late thirties by 1846, not early thirties. If born circa 1820, early thirties works. The bible's range is broad enough to accommodate "early thirties," but the phrasing "1810s-1820s" leans toward the older end.
- **Location**: Line 46.
- **Recommendation**: Consistent within the bible's range (born ~1813-1816 would make him 30-33 in 1846). No change needed, but the bible entry could be tightened if desired.
