## Warnings (review needed)

- **[Scene-internal knowledge]**: Clara tells Samuel that Harper saw his workers arriving at the warehouse early (lines 205-206), attributing this to Harper's pre-dawn waterfront walk. However, Harper's on-page dialogue in Scene 3 does not mention Samuel's workers. The information is attributed to an off-page source (Harper's fuller report). This is defensible — Scene 3's conversation with Harper likely continued beyond what is rendered in dialogue — but the on-page chain of acquisition is incomplete. Consider adding a brief reference to Samuel's workers in Harper's waterfront report (Scene 3) to close the loop.
  - **Location**: Lines 200-206

- **[Timeline precision]**: Samuel stood at the water pump "eight hours ago" (line 190). Ch-08 places the water pump scene at approximately 2:50-3:00 AM (the grandfather clock strikes three at the chapter's final line). Samuel arrives at midmorning in ch-09. If midmorning is ~10:00 AM, "eight hours ago" would mean ~2:00 AM, roughly 50-60 minutes before the actual water pump scene. This is a minor imprecision in a character's subjective estimate, not a hard error, but it slightly compresses the timeline.
  - **Location**: Line 190

- **[Physical description]**: Clara's bruised wrist is not mentioned in ch-09, though ch-09 line 34 notes her "right wrist ached with the faint protest of a joint that had absorbed too many Colt discharges." The outline for ch-10 specifies a visible "wrist bruise" that Clara chooses not to hide at the dinner. If the wrist ache in ch-09 is intended to set up the visible bruise in ch-10, this tracks. If not, the author should confirm whether the ache produces a visible bruise.
  - **Location**: Line 34 (ch-09) vs. ch-10 outline beat 2

- **[Thomas's grin descriptor]**: Ch-09 line 238 describes Thomas's expression as "the grin that made him look ten years younger." This is consistent with ch-07 line 52 ("boyish and sharp") and ch-02 line 98 ("a gesture of triumph so boyish it belonged to a younger man"). No error, but the repeated "younger" descriptor is becoming a recognizable verbal tic across chapters. Author may wish to vary.
  - **Location**: Line 238

## New Facts Introduced

- **Edward Marsh**: Wallace's clerk/accountant who kept the secondary books. Name, role, and circumstances of capture established. Wound: right shoulder (Callowhill marksman). Talked from terror, not interrogation. Provides names: Haverford (Baltimore shipping agent), anonymous warehouse operator (New York), harbor official (Boston). Wallace sent Marsh to observe without warning of danger.
- **Nolan**: One of Harper's men. Knife wound between fourth and fifth ribs, muscle not organ. Wife and two children on Seventh Street. Perhaps twenty-five, dock worker's hands. First named in ch-09 (unnamed "one of Harper's men" in ch-08).
- **Harper's quarters**: Room above the garden gate at Clara's townhouse. Furnished by Clara years ago when Harper refused to live more than fifty yards from her door.
- **Wallace's coastal network architecture**: Seven ships, three-year operation, Baltimore-New York-Boston-Philadelphia circuit. Legitimate cargo on return legs; human cargo disguised as extra dock laborers on crew rosters. Contracted captains, paid agents, Wallace's hands never touch the cargo.
- **Clara's Baltimore and Boston contacts**: Agents watching ports for trafficking patterns since before the Mercy Jane. First revealed to Samuel in this chapter.
- **Haverford**: Baltimore shipping agent who arranges manifest alterations. Handles cargo transfers for three firms including Samuel's. Office on Pratt Street near the basin.
- **New York warehouse operator**: Near the East River wharves, south of the Fulton Market. Handles transfers with unusual discretion. Name unknown to Marsh.
- **Boston harbor official**: Ensures inspections find nothing. Name not yet established.
- **Two-week window**: Thomas's estimate before Wallace rebuilds his waterfront presence in Philadelphia.
- **The dinner invitation**: Clara has never invited a man to dine at her house. Thomas confirms this as unprecedented. Good silver. Table set for three.
- **Samuel's workers came to the warehouse unprompted**: Heard about trouble at the docks, came because they chose to. Reflects Samuel's relationship-building philosophy.

## Threads That Could Be Advanced Here

- **Clara's progressive disarmament pattern** (Thread 11): Ch-09 does not describe Clara's clothing or armament in detail. The chapter opens with her in the courtyard in boots (line 28), but her coat/weapons state is not specified for the rest of the chapter. Given that this thread has been carefully tracked through chapters 1-7, the absence of armament description in ch-09 is notable. The author may wish to note Clara's register: she does not practice with weapons, she does not arm herself before visiting Nolan or meeting Samuel. This could be read as a continuation of the pattern — the morning after combat, she is neither armed nor performing — or it could be an oversight.

- **Wallace's art-world patronage** (Thread 9): The chapter focuses on Wallace's shipping/trafficking infrastructure but does not reference his social respectability apparatus (established in ch-05 as the key vulnerability). The plan Thomas drafts focuses on legal complaints and direct action but does not mention the respectability-dismantling strategy that was identified as "the most important item" in ch-05. The author may wish to ensure the art-world angle is not dropped from the strategic planning.

- **Lily Montgomery**: Not present or mentioned in ch-09. Given that the dinner is being planned for that evening, and Lily is established as one of Clara's dearest friends (ch-04), her absence from the chapter is not an error but could be an opportunity — even a passing mention of whether Lily is aware of the night's events.
