## Warnings (review needed)

- **[Timeline / knowledge gap]**: Between ch-16 (Late Autumn 1846) and ch-17 (January 1847), approximately two months pass. Clara's interiority in the opening ride (lines 32-33) references several developments that occurred during this gap: "the commission inquiry held in suspension by Thomas's legal objections," "the first fleet vessel... taking shape in the yard," Thomas having "the transition documents underway." All of this is plausible given Clara's daily access to Thomas, but none of it was shown on-page. The reader must accept a winter of off-page preparation. The outline confirms this skip is intentional (ch-17 outline: "January 1847 -- midwinter, two months before departure"). No contradiction, but a significant information load delivered through summary interiority.

- **[Knowledge / Harper sharing operational intelligence]**: Harper telling Lily about the Osprey and "what it was built to carry" (line 172) represents a senior security operative sharing classified intelligence without Clara's authorization. The chapter handles this explicitly -- Clara processes it alongside the weapons training revelation -- but the Osprey's rescue compartments are among Clara's most dangerous secrets (the commission inquiry, Wallace's surveillance). Harper sharing this with Lily is consistent with his character (quiet devotion extending to Lily) and is addressed in the narrative, but it raises the question of whether Harper would share this level of operational detail. This is flagged as a character-judgment call for the author, not a contradiction.

- **[Timeline / kidnapping dating]**: "She had given Lily this place two years ago" (line 40) places the sanctuary gift circa early 1845. The kidnapping that preceded it is dated "~1846-1848" in the master timeline but "~1844-1845" in the continuity notes (ch-17 notes, line 1272). Ch-19 also says "Two years since the hold." The master timeline entry should be updated to reflect the ~1844-1845 dating that the drafted chapters establish. No in-chapter contradiction, but the bible's master timeline entry ("~1846-1848") is now inconsistent with the drafted text.

- **[Character / Lily's cello]**: Ch-17 shows a cello in Lily's garden room (line 104) and Clara references Lily as someone who "played violin and cello" (line 154). Lily's bible profile confirms "violin, cello." In ch-04, Lily appeared only as a violinist (duet with Clara). The cello at her sanctuary is consistent with her profile but has not been shown on-page before this chapter. The continuity notes confirm this was a deliberate addition (advisory from a prior continuity check, line 1329). No error -- new fact consistent with bible.

## Threads That Could Be Advanced Here

- **Wallace thread**: Wallace is referenced only in summary (line 116: "Wallace's move west") during Clara's disclosure to Lily. Given that this chapter is the first scene outside the waterfront/townhouse world, the absence of direct Wallace pressure is appropriate -- but the author might consider whether Lily's reaction to learning about Wallace (a man who integrates trafficking with port control) would carry additional weight given her history as a trafficking survivor. Currently Lily's response focuses entirely on the voyage decision, not the adversary.

- **Clara's father wound**: Clara mentions her father's Pacific trading routes in ch-16 and the decision to go west is partly driven by personal history. Lily -- as Clara's closest friend -- might be someone Clara would share this with, but the chapter does not reference Clara's father at all. This may be a missed opportunity for emotional depth, or it may be a deliberate choice to keep the chapter focused on Lily's agency.
