## Errors (must fix)

### 1. Headcount discrepancy: Harper's team size

- **Category**: Scene-internal consistency
- **Draft says**: Harper "came through the garden gate with three of his men -- the core of the team" (line 79). Later, "They approached from the north -- Clara, Samuel, Harper, and four of Harper's men" (line 115).
- **Issue**: Harper arrives at the muster with three men. During the approach, the count has grown to four. No additional arrival is described on-page.
- **Location**: Lines 79 and 115
- **Recommendation**: Either change "three" to "four" in the muster scene, add a line establishing that a fourth man arrived before departure, or change "four" to "three" in the approach scene (and adjust downstream references accordingly -- verify one wounded man still leaves enough for the fight choreography).

### 2. Scene-internal knowledge: Samuel references the "ten-item plan"

- **Category**: Unattributed knowledge
- **Draft says**: Samuel says "We are going to need more than a ten-item plan" (line 233).
- **Source of the plan**: Clara commissioned it from Thomas in ch-02 (line 68). Thomas delivered it to Clara in ch-05. Samuel was never present for either scene.
- **On-page disclosure to Samuel**: None. Samuel has received Wallace intelligence (ch-03 tea, ch-06 report delivery) and has been training at Clara's home (ch-07), but the specific "ten-item plan" was never mentioned to him or in his presence in any drafted chapter.
- **Location**: Line 233
- **Recommendation**: Either (a) add a brief line earlier in the book where Clara or Thomas mentions the plan to Samuel, (b) change Samuel's line to something he could plausibly know (e.g., "We are going to need more than strategy"), or (c) establish in this chapter that Clara told him about the plan off-page with a brief reference (e.g., "the plan Clara had outlined for him" in interiority).

## Warnings (review needed)

### 1. Tactical plan vs. execution: approach direction

- **Category**: Internal consistency
- **Draft says**: Clara's briefing: "Your team takes the northern approach. I enter from the south" (line 83). Execution: "They approached from the north -- Clara, Samuel, Harper, and four of Harper's men" (line 115). Then Clara "stepped from the shadow of the Callowhill warehouse" (line 133), and "Harper's team closed from the north" (line 139).
- **Issue**: The group approaches from the north together, but Clara's plan called for her to enter from the south. The execution can be read as the group moving north to a staging point, then splitting (Clara breaks off toward Callowhill, Harper's team continues north), but this is not made explicit. The reader may wonder why Clara is approaching from the north with Harper's team when she said she would enter from the south.
- **Location**: Lines 83, 115, 133, 139
- **Recommendation**: Add a sentence during the approach clarifying the split (e.g., "At the Callowhill corner, Clara signaled. Harper's team continued north. She and Samuel moved toward the warehouse shadow."), or adjust the briefing to match the execution.

### 2. George's position in Scene 6 (The Return)

- **Category**: Presence tracking
- **Draft says**: In Scene 5, George returns to Clara's side (line 153). In Scene 6 (Thomas's office), George is not mentioned. In Scene 7, "George lay near the weapons rack" in the courtyard (line 219).
- **Issue**: George was at Clara's side on the waterfront. Clara goes to Thomas's office. George does not appear in the office. George next appears in the courtyard. It is plausible George went to the courtyard while Clara went to Thomas (as he has done in prior chapters), but the gap in tracking is notable given the chapter's otherwise tight presence accounting.
- **Location**: Lines 153, 183-211, 219
- **Recommendation**: Minor. A brief mention of George's position when Clara enters the office (e.g., "George had gone ahead to the courtyard") or when she enters the courtyard would close the gap cleanly.

### 3. Clara's reference to "Thomas's patronage report" in interiority

## New Facts Introduced

- **Callowhill warehouse**: Clara owns a warehouse on Callowhill Street overlooking Samuel's dock section, suitable for elevated firing positions. New property not previously mentioned, consistent with her established real-estate network.
- **Spruce Street runner**: Clara has a message runner stationed on Spruce Street (the same street as her townhouse). New operational detail.
- **Water pump in courtyard**: A water pump stands near the eastern wall of Clara's courtyard. New physical detail.
- **Linen by the weapons rack**: Clara keeps linen near the weapons rack (for drying hands/wiping down). New detail.
- **Thomas as field coordinator**: Thomas positions marksmen, confirms access to the Callowhill building himself, and coordinates elevated fire support. Expands his role from desk intelligence to field logistics.
- **Thomas uses sharpshooters**: Thomas's marksmen are referenced; implies Clara's apparatus includes trained marksmen who answer to Thomas's positioning.
- **Three-tap lamp shutter signal**: Communication protocol -- three taps on a lamp shutter, visible only from a specified route, confirms marksmen in position. New tradecraft detail.
- **Harper arrived with "core team"**: The core team consists of the men Clara personally trained for night patrols. Implies Harper has a larger team beyond the core.
- **George guards wounded man**: George positions himself between a fallen friendly and the remaining fighters, teeth bared in silence. Consistent with his dual nature (protector/combatant) established in ch-01.
- **Wallace's fleet**: Seven ships, including the Mercy Jane. Routes from Baltimore through New York to Boston and back to Philadelphia. Coded ledger tracking human cargo. Crew rosters with wages and dates. This is the chapter's major revelation.
- **Wallace's field clerk/supervisor**: An unnamed man in a dark coat, non-combatant, carried a leather case with the manifests. Soft hands, counting-house coat. Wounded by Callowhill marksman (ball through right shoulder). Recognized Clara. First human intelligence source captured from Wallace's operation.

## Threads Advanced

- **Anti-trafficking campaign**: Major escalation. Wallace moves from background threat to direct assault on Samuel's warehouses. Twenty-three armed men, some from outside the city. Captured documents reveal a seven-ship trafficking fleet operating from Baltimore to Boston. The Mercy Jane was one route among seven.
- **Clara-Samuel courtship**: From training (ch-07) to shared combat. Samuel uses Clara's teaching in the fight (the redirect, the yielding). Clara observes and acknowledges it. The chapter ends with "We are going to need each other" -- Clara's most direct statement -- and a handshake that echoes the ch-03 parlor handshake, deepened by shared violence.
- **Thomas Arlington's jealousy seeds**: Thomas delivers the intelligence, anticipates Clara's orders ("Already sent word"; "I confirmed access this morning"), positions the sharpshooters, returns to his desk to process the captured manifests. He is the architect of the entire operation's infrastructure. Yet the chapter ends with Clara leaving Thomas at his desk to join Samuel in the courtyard. The physical movement -- from the office to the courtyard, from the man who builds to the man who fought beside her -- continues the migration pattern established in ch-06.
- **Wallace's operation scope (Thread 9: Wallace Betrayal)**: Escalated from art-world patronage cover to direct military-style assault and a seven-ship fleet. Wallace sent a clerk to supervise, suggesting organizational discipline.
- **Clara's progressive disarmament pattern (Thread 11)**: Ch-08 reverses the pattern -- Clara is fully armed (leather coat fastened, Colts in combat configuration, sword cane). The rearming mirrors ch-05's post-Golden Compass rearming but escalates to full combat load. The disarmament pattern is paused; the courtship now advances through shared violence rather than shared vulnerability.

## Threads That Could Be Advanced Here

- **Lily Montgomery**: Lily was introduced in ch-04 as Clara's dearest friend and violinist. She has not appeared since. A chapter involving a major operation and its aftermath could plausibly include a mention of Lily -- is she safe? Does Clara check on her? This is not an error, but an opportunity for the reader to feel the scope of Clara's concern.
- **Clara's Latin motto**: "Homo sum, humani nihil a me alienum puto" has been reserved for a significant moment. The discovery of a seven-ship trafficking fleet -- the revelation that the disease is far larger than the symptom -- could be that moment. Deferred to author judgment.
- **Harper's backstory/interiority**: One of Harper's men is wounded in this chapter. Harper responds with "practiced calm." Given that Harper dies during the voyage west, deepening his characterization during moments of violence could add weight to his later death. This is a character-analyst concern more than continuity, but flagged as an opportunity.
