# Regression Report: Book 1, Chapter 18

**Trigger**: Crew count changed from "twelve" to "eight" on the manifest (line 107).
**Date**: 2026-03-05
**Chapters cross-referenced**: ch-18 (revised), ch-20, ch-21, ch-19, ch-22, ch-23

---

## 1. Crew Count: ch-18 vs. ch-20 and ch-21

**Status: RESOLVED**

The revision corrected ch-18 line 107 from "A crew of twelve" to "A crew of eight." This now matches:

- Ch-20, line 59: "The crew -- eight men, all chosen by Thomas"
- Ch-21, line 76: "The crew stood in a loose line -- eight men, rope-roughened and tanned from twelve days at sea"

The crew count is consistent across all three chapters.

---

## 2. Provisions Line: "twenty people" Is Now Stale

**Status: ERROR -- must fix**

Ch-18, line 157: "the supplies that would feed **twenty people** for five months at sea."

This number was calculated from the old crew count:

| Old math (crew of 12) | New math (crew of 8) |
|---|---|
| 8 passengers (Clara, Samuel, Harper, 4 Harper men, Lily) | 8 passengers |
| 12 crew | 8 crew |
| 1 captain | 1 captain |
| **Total: 21 humans** (rounded to "twenty") | **Total: 17 humans** |

With the crew reduced to eight, "twenty people" overshoots by three. The line must be updated to reflect the actual headcount.

**Recommended fix**: Change "twenty people" to match the intended total. The options:

- **"seventeen people"** -- exact count (8 passengers + 8 crew + 1 captain). Precise, consistent with Samuel's ledger-man character.
- **"fifteen people"** -- matches ch-20 line 61 ("how much water fifteen souls consumed per day"). This would make both chapters agree, but it undercounts by two. Samuel may be excluding the captain and himself from a particular calculation, or "fifteen" is itself an error that should be "seventeen."

**Note on ch-20's "fifteen souls"**: With 17 humans aboard, "fifteen souls" in ch-20 is also imprecise. Two possible explanations:

1. Samuel is calculating water consumption for the people he is directly provisioning, excluding the captain (who manages his own stores) and possibly one other. This is a plausible maritime convention but is not stated on-page.
2. "Fifteen" was calculated from an earlier draft where the numbers differed and was never reconciled.

**Recommendation**: Fix ch-18's "twenty people" to the correct total. Then decide whether ch-20's "fifteen souls" should also be corrected to the same number, or whether "fifteen" is intentionally approximate. If "fifteen" is meant to be exact, it needs a rationale for excluding two people from the count.

## 3. New Contradictions Introduced by the Change

**Status: CLEAN -- no new contradictions**

The change from "twelve" to "eight" does not create any new problems in:

- **Ch-19** (departure): References "the crew" generically, does not specify a number. No conflict.
- **Ch-21** (Windward Passage): Already said "eight men" for the crew. Now matches ch-18.
- **Ch-22** (Kingston): References crew generically. No conflict.
- **Ch-23** (Chagres River): The company splits into two bungos. No crew count specified. No conflict.
- **Ch-21 Tierney reference** (line 132): Tierney is described as a crew member ("the last hired before they sailed, Thomas's recommendation"). With eight crew and one captain, Tierney is one of the eight. He is also named separately from Harper's four men (Davis, Ward, Cole, Jennings). No conflict -- Tierney fits within the eight-man crew.
