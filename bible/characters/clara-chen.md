# Clara Chen

## Physical Description

Small-framed with pale skin that carries a faint flush when ill. Dark hair that develops silver threads in later years. In her prime she dresses in performance gowns designed for practical elegance — beautiful but functional. Wears London-tailored leather coats that conceal weapons. Carries a silver-handled sword cane. Her appearance shifts across the series: younger and vital in Era 1 (Fortunes Tide), visibly aging and marked by illness in Era 2 (FIERCE).

## Background

Chinese-American. Her father was Buddhist with Japanese Shinto elements — kami, harmony with nature — and her mother was a devout Christian. The family maintained trading connections across the Pacific, including Canton and Japan. Through these family trading connections she received training in Japanese martial arts. She trained for three years at Canton Missionary Hospital, then studied medicine further in an anatomy lab alongside Kate. By age 25 she had built a shipping empire in Philadelphia, amassing enormous wealth — described as "the fortune of many kings." She owns the Golden Compass music hall in Philadelphia through proxy ownership. Later she relocates to San Francisco and builds the Glass Garden estate on the Monterey coast — a redwood-and-glass cliffside compound with gun ports disguised as architectural flourishes. She is the central figure of the entire series: protagonist of Era 1 (Fortunes Tide) and mentor figure in Era 2 (FIERCE).

## Personality

- **Core trait**: Strategic brilliance masked by grace and beauty
- **Fatal flaw**: Need for control / inability to delegate until forced by illness
- **Deepest fear**: That her network won't survive without her
- **Driving desire**: To build something that protects the vulnerable and outlasts her
- **How she sees herself**: "I am a spy. I am an aunt. I am a friend. I am grateful."
- **How others see her**: A woman of impossible depth — artist, warrior, philanthropist, spy

## Speech Patterns

Measured and deliberate — described as having "river-stone smoothness" and a quality of "silk and steel." Direct but layered; her words carry surface meaning and subtext simultaneously. She shifts between formality and warmth depending on context and audience. Uses dark humor, particularly about her illness ("My breasts seem to be killing me," "my left tit seems too jealous of its formerly bigger right companion"). Speaks Latin and quotes Terence: "Homo sum, humani nihil a me alienum puto." Proverbial in her phrasing. Uses sign language with Eve. Multi-lingual and culturally fluent.

**Example phrases:**
1. "Hi there. I'm Clara Chen." (casual introduction amid corpses on the docks)
2. "I also wish to be a young woman and artist tonight. I acknowledge the risks."
3. "Be fierce, dear ones. Be fully yourselves while serving necessary purpose."

**Speech habits:** Tends toward proverbs and layered statements. Delivers brutal truths with gentleness. Uses dark humor as deflection when discussing her mortality. Never raises her voice when she can lower it. Switches to sign language for private communication with Eve.

## Internal Contradictions

- Wants others to find their own path but has manipulated everyone into position
- Values freedom but controls everything in her network
- Teaches yielding and surrender but cannot let go herself until forced by death
