# Thomas

Two separate characters sharing a first name. Thomas Mitchell is likely Thomas Arlington's son or protege, which explains the name overlap, Jack's generational distrust, and Clara's "knowing reaction" to Mitchell.

---

## Thomas Arlington (Era 1)

### Physical Description

Early thirties in 1846. Sharp-dressed with a sharper mind. Silver at the temples, sharp-featured, ink on both hands. Carries himself with analytical composure; a boyish grin when pleased. Ground floor office with Persian rugs and tactical maps — his environment reflects his nature: strategic, cultured, precise. Handsome — "handsome as hell" per his own assessment of others, but the description fits him too.

### Background

Clara Chen's oldest friend. Never her love interest — a friend, but a jealous one. Manages Clara's holdings — financial, property, and intelligence assets. Operates from the ground floor of Clara's townhouse. Part of Clara's inner circle from the beginning. Their rapport is teasing, familiar, comfortable — Thomas knows Clara's "type," her habits, her schedule.

When Clara meets Samuel Taylor and then Jack Garrett, Thomas is left to manage her affairs while her world expands. He was the one who knew Clara first, who built the infrastructure, who ran the details. And he watched her give her heart, her trust, and her partnership to others. The jealousy is not romantic — it is the jealousy of the indispensable man who discovers he is dispensable. Clara doesn't need him the way she once did.

Thomas betrays Clara. The specifics of the betrayal are TBD, but the motivation is clear: he was sidelined, and he chose revenge over loyalty. Jack Garrett saw it — "I don't trust Thomas" during the voyage west. Jack's instinct was right.

Thomas Arlington is ultimately killed by Lily Montgomery.

### Personality

- **Core trait**: Analytical wit — sees the angles before others see the board
- **Fatal flaw**: Jealousy masked as strategic thinking. Convinced himself that his betrayal was rational when it was emotional.
- **Deepest fear**: Being replaceable — that Clara's world can function without him
- **Driving desire**: To matter. To be the one Clara turns to first.
- **How he sees himself**: The architect of Clara's empire, undervalued and overlooked
- **How others see him**: Witty, sharp, charming — and then, too late, dangerous

### Speech Patterns

Analytical and witty. Quick with observations that are half-compliment, half-assessment. Teasing rapport with Clara — familiar, irreverent, comfortable. Combines flattery with strategic probing. After the betrayal, his words curdle — the same wit turns bitter, the same familiarity becomes weaponized.

**Example phrases:**
1. "Sam Taylor is handsome as hell."
2. "What are you wearing?"
3. "I don't manage most of your holdings and not know your type."

**Speech habits:** Compliments that double as intelligence gathering. Humor with an edge — always watching, always calculating. His wit is his armor and his weapon.

|-------|------|---------|
| Early Era 1 | Trusted friend / manager | Clara's oldest friend. Manages her holdings, provides strategic counsel, teases Clara about Samuel. |
| Mid Era 1 | Sidelined friend | Clara's world expands — Samuel, Jack, Kate, Lily. Thomas manages the details but is no longer the center. |
| Late Era 1 | Betrayer | Jealousy curdles into action. Thomas betrays Clara. Jack's instinct vindicated. |

### Key Scenes

1. **Office with Persian rugs** — Establishes his role as Clara's strategic manager.
2. **"Sam Taylor is handsome as hell"** — Teasing Clara, assessing Samuel. The familiarity that will sour.
3. **Left behind** — Clara departs or expands; Thomas manages affairs alone. The seed of jealousy.
4. **The betrayal** — Specifics TBD. Thomas acts against Clara's interests.
5. **Death at Lily's hand** — Lily kills Thomas. The artist who chose strength proves it was no empty gesture.

### Internal Contradictions

- Brilliant and loyal for years — then betrays the person he served most faithfully
- His jealousy is not romantic, which makes it harder to see coming
- Jack distrusted him; Clara did not. Clara's blindspot was trusting the familiar.
- His betrayal validates Lily's decision to train with weapons: "Not out of fear... Out of choice."

## Thomas Mitchell (Era 2)

### Physical Description

Younger man. Connected to the Durand family. Arrives at the Glass Garden. Physical description TBD — distinct from Arlington.

### Background

Eve Garrett's love interest. A Durand by blood who arrives at the Glass Garden estate under suspicion. Initially distrusted — Jack's wariness of the Thomas name transfers generationally. Clara's knowing reaction upon meeting Mitchell is explained by her recognizing his connection to Arlington (possibly his son or protege). Mitchell must prove his loyalty through action, not words.

### Personality

- **Core trait**: Courage to confront his own family
- **Fatal flaw**: The weight of his family name and the distrust it carries
- **Deepest fear**: That he is defined by where he came from, not who he chooses to be
- **Driving desire**: To earn trust on his own terms
- **How he sees himself**: A man trying to be better than his origins
- **How others see him**: Suspicious, charming, unproven — until the moment he proves himself

### Arc

| Phase | Role | Summary |
|-------|------|---------|
| FIERCE Act I | Suspicious arrival | Arrives at Glass Garden. Eve is wary. Jack warns her. |
| FIERCE Act II | Chemistry under pressure | Midnight encounters. Forced together when group splits. Durand connection revealed. |
| FIERCE Act III | Loyalty proven | Eve's impossible shot saves him. Thomas confronts his own family. |
| FIERCE Act IV | Full partner | Accepted by the network. By Eve's side. |

### Key Scenes

1. **Arrival at Glass Garden** — Eve is suspicious. Clara watches with knowing eyes.
2. **Midnight encounter** — First chemistry. Training interrupted.
3. **Durand revelation** — Thomas reveals his family connection in the tunnels.
4. **Eve's impossible shot** — Saves Thomas's life. Trust earned through action.
5. **Confronting his family** — Thomas chooses Eve's side over the Durands.
