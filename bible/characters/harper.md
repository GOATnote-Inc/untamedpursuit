# David Harper

## Physical Description

40s. Average height, average build, weathered in a way that suggests exposure rather than labor. Clean, unremarkable clothes. Still hands. Eyes that read rooms — entrances, exits, sight lines. His presence is felt through attentiveness, not display. Not flashy; not large. A wall shaped like a man. (Source: book-02, ch-07 — Clara's memory of his first day, 1843)

## Background

Clara Chen's security chief. Devoted to Clara's safety with a professional deference that never wavers — addresses her as "Miss Chen" throughout. Resigned to Clara's independence and her insistence on taking personal risks, though he never stops trying to protect her. Follows Clara in her shadows when she ventures into danger. Escorts Irish workers as part of the network's operations. Presents Samuel Taylor with flowers backstage at the Golden Compass. Dies during the voyage west — Clara loses her security chief before reaching San Francisco.

## Personality

- **Core trait**: Quiet, unwavering loyalty
- **Fatal flaw**: Resignation — accepts Clara's risks rather than challenging her more forcefully
- **Deepest fear**: That he will fail Clara at the moment it matters most
- **Driving desire**: To keep Clara safe — a task he knows is impossible but pursues anyway
- **How he sees himself**: A man with a duty
- **How others see him**: Professional, gentle, quietly heroic — Lily preserves his memory through stories of kindness

## Speech Patterns

Professional and restrained. Quiet concern underlies every word. Deferential — "Miss Chen" is his default address. Does not argue; he advises, then follows. His silence often communicates more than his words.

**Example phrases:**
1. "Miss Chen..." (with quiet concern, the unfinished sentence saying everything)
2. (Presents flowers to Samuel backstage — actions over words)
3. (Follows Clara in shadows — duty expressed through presence, not speech)

**Speech habits:** Formal address ("Miss Chen"). Sentences trail off with unspoken worry. Professional vocabulary. Never raises his voice. Communicates devotion through action and proximity rather than declaration.

## Key Scenes

1. **First day in Clara's service** — Philadelphia, 1843, summer. Arrived with a letter of introduction. Had watched Clara for three days first. Stood in the parlor: "Where do you need me." Clara: "Everywhere." Nodded and took his position beside the door. (book-02, ch-07)
2. **Following Clara in shadows** — Quiet vigilance as she takes risks he cannot prevent. (Fortunes Tide)
3. **Presenting flowers to Samuel** — At the Golden Compass. Bouquet for Samuel to give Clara, arranged with specific instruction. "Miss Chen will receive visitors in the private gallery." Eyes showing warmth — the nearest thing to approval his reserve would allow. (book-01, ch-04)
4. **Escorting Irish workers** — Network operations in action. (Fortunes Tide)
5. **Silence on the mule trail** — Samuel checks his pistol at dawn. Harper looks at the pistol, looks at Samuel. Says nothing. Looks away. The compliment he would never deliver. (book-01, ch-24)
6. **Death** — Mortally wounded in mule-trail ambush (book-02, ch-01). Surgery and night conversation (ch-02). Dies before dawn (ch-03). Last words: "Miss Chen."

## Internal Contradictions

- Devoted to protecting a woman who is more dangerous than most of her enemies
- Professional deference to a woman he clearly loves (in the manner of service)
- Resigned to danger he cannot prevent — loyalty as beautiful futility
- A man of action whose defining quality is patience and restraint
