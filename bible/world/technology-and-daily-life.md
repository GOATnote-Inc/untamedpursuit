# Technology & Daily Life

<!-- What exists and what doesn't in the story's era. How people travel, eat, work, and communicate. -->

## Transportation

<!-- How do people get around? Travel times between key locations. -->

- **Carriages**: Urban travel in Philadelphia; an hour's carriage ride along Ridge Pike from Philadelphia to Lily's sanctuary at Wissahickon Creek
- **Ships**: Sailing vessels (masts, rigging) for coastal and trans-oceanic travel. Philadelphia's harbor is a forest of ship masts.
- **Pony Express** (1860): Jack's occupation. Short-lived mail delivery system.
- **Trains**: New technology, cutting through the landscape ("each mile of track piercing the land"). Represents both progress and disruption.
- **Horseback**: Primary frontier transportation. Characters routinely ride long distances in Colorado Territory and the West.
- **Panama crossing**: Route to California before the transcontinental railroad. Part of the Philadelphia-to-San Francisco voyage.

## Communication

<!-- Letters, messengers, word-of-mouth. How fast does information travel? -->

- **Coded messages**: Used within Clara's network for covert communication
- **Sign language**: Clara and Eve's private system -- a personal code beyond standard signs
- **Hand signals for dogs**: Commands for George and Ghost
- **Letters**: Primary long-distance communication. Kate writes to Pasteur in France.
- **Pony Express**: Fast mail delivery across frontier (Jack's occupation, 1860)

## Food & Drink

<!-- What do people eat? How is food prepared? Meals and their social role. -->

- **Ginger tea**: Clara's preference; a recurring personal detail
- **Wine**: Red wine shared between Clara and Lily; aged red wine in Clara's study at the Glass Garden
- **Heritage apple varieties**: Rhode Island Greenings, Northern Spys, Baldwin apples grown in Lily's orchard at Wissahickon Creek
- **Fishermen with nets**: Monterey coast -- local food source near the Glass Garden

## Medicine & Health

<!-- What do people know about illness? Available treatments. Common ailments. -->

- **Quinine**: Used for fever treatment (Kate administers during the 1854 outbreak)
- **Stethoscope**: In use -- Eve examines Clara with one
- **Anatomy lab study**: Cadaver dissection with formaldehyde and camphor. Fifty examination stations.
- **Germ theory**: Actively debated. References to Pasteur, Koch, and Lister. Kate writes to Pasteur.
- **Carbolic acid treatments**: Lister's antiseptic methods
- **Frontier surgery**: Performed on rough-hewn tables in improvised conditions
- **Metastatic breast cancer**: Understood by doctors but untreatable in this era
- **Woman's Medical College of Pennsylvania**: Founded 1850 -- where women could train as physicians

## Weapons

- **Percussion cap firearms**: Modified for quieter firing in covert operations
- **Multiple Colts (revolvers)**: Clara's primary firearms
- **Winchester rifles**: Jack's and Eve's primary weapon
- **Gatling gun**: Used in train attack sequence
- **Sword canes**: Silver-handled, hidden blade mechanism
- **Dynamite**: Jessie's specialty; also used in mining operations
- **Practice swords (wooden)**: Training weapons used in the Glass Garden courtyard

## Work & Trade

<!-- Occupations, crafts, commerce. How do people earn a living? -->

- **Dock work**: Samuel's father was a dock worker; Irish dock workers on Philadelphia's waterfront
- **Shipping and trade**: Clara's primary business empire, operating through proxy ownership
- **Medical practice**: Kate and Eve as frontier physicians
- **Bounty hunting**: Jessie's background profession
- **Pony Express rider**: Jack's occupation
- **Military service**: Captain Andrews, cavalry units
- **Mining**: Referenced in connection with dynamite use and frontier economy

## Domestic Life

<!-- Homes, furnishings, clothing, daily routines. -->

- **Clara's townhouse**: Persian rugs, marble fireplace, mahogany desk, grandfather clock -- elegant but practical
- **Glass Garden estate**: Redwood and glass construction, armored conservatory panels, hidden passages, Japanese garden
- **Lily's sanctuary**: White brick house, terraced gardens, stone paths, reflecting pools, garden room with tall windows
- **Whale watching**: Seasonal activity from the Glass Garden's western stone porch (humpback and orca, April-June)
