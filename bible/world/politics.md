# Politics & Power

## Power Structures

<!-- Who holds power? How is it organized? -->

- **Dock consortiums**: Corrupt business interests controlling Philadelphia's waterfront. Key antagonists include Wallace and Marcus Blackwood. They represent entrenched economic power threatened by Clara's operations.
- **Clara's network**: A covert empire operating through proxy ownership, hidden wealth, and loyal operatives. Extends from Philadelphia to San Francisco.
- **Military presence**: Captain Andrews and cavalry units represent federal authority in the West. Their relationship with Clara's network is complex -- sometimes allied, sometimes at cross-purposes.
- **Bounty hunting as profession**: Jessie's background. Operates in a gray zone between law enforcement and freelance violence. Provides income and intelligence-gathering opportunities.

## Political Events

<!-- Major political shifts that affect the story. Cross-reference with timeline. -->

- **Gold Rush economics** (1848+): Competition for port control drives conflict. San Francisco becomes a critical expansion target. The Panama crossing route exists because the transcontinental railroad is not yet complete.
- **Child trafficking operations (Mercy Jane)**: A criminal enterprise that Clara's network works to dismantle. Represents the darkest dimension of the era's lawlessness.
- **Cross-border operations and "specialized missions"**: Clara's network conducts covert operations that extend beyond any single jurisdiction. These blur the line between justice and vigilantism.
- **Deliberate well poisoning**: Biological warfare discovered by Jack. An act of intentional contamination -- a political weapon disguised as natural disaster.

## Economic Power

- **Proxy ownership**: Clara's method of controlling assets (the Golden Compass, shipping interests, real estate) while remaining invisible as the true owner. A necessity given the era's restrictions on women and Chinese-Americans holding visible wealth.
- **Shipping and port control**: The economic engine driving much of the series' conflict. Whoever controls the docks controls the flow of goods, information, and people.
- **Frontier economy**: Mining, ranching, and medical practice in places like Silver Creek. Less structured than Eastern commerce but no less contested.
