# Book 1 — Detailed Timeline

<!-- Chapter-level chronology for Book 1. -->
<!-- Set ~Autumn 1846, Philadelphia. Exact dates are approximate; sequence is canonical. -->

### Night of the Mercy Jane Rescue — Chapter 1
- **Event**: Clara's Night Watch operation rescues two girls (older ~10, younger ~5) from the trafficking ship Mercy Jane. Twelve-plus traffickers killed. Samuel Taylor intervenes during the rescue — first meeting between Clara and Samuel.
- **Location**: Philadelphia docks, Clara's private dock (northern end of the port)
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Harper, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Inciting event of the book. Clara and Samuel meet in combat. Samuel had conducted 3 weeks of surveillance on the Mercy Jane before the rescue. Both men know each other's names before introduction — implies intelligence connections on both sides.

### Same Night — Chapter 2
- **Event**: Clara debriefs with Thomas Arlington in his ground-floor office. Thomas assesses Samuel from existing property records. Clara assigns Thomas to prepare a full report. "I love you, Thomas" / "And well, you should" exchange.
- **Location**: Thomas's office, Clara's townhouse
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Establishes Clara-Thomas dynamic. Thomas is trusted, central, "indispensable." First dual-frequency seeds planted.

### Dawn, ~One Day Later — Chapter 3
- **Event**: Clara practices weapons in her courtyard. Thomas delivers his report on Samuel. Clara and Samuel meet for tea at the Chestnut Street parlor — first real conversation. Samuel reveals Wallace's rejected partnership offer and connection to trafficking. Clara discloses a fraction of her shipping holdings. Handshake at the end.
- **Location**: Clara's courtyard, Chestnut Street parlor
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Thomas, George (absent from tea — deliberate)
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Wallace named as antagonist. Samuel's background established (Irish father, ethical business philosophy). Clara's progressive disarmament continues (dark green silk, no leather coat, sword cane only).

### ~One Week After Chapter 1 — Chapter 4
- **Event**: Samuel attends the Golden Compass for Clara's performance. Clara plays cello, duets with Lily (violin), sings Italian arias, plays piano, performs original composition. Samuel weeps openly. Harper presents Samuel with flowers backstage. Lily introduced. George's head settles in Samuel's lap. Chapter ends at the threshold of the private dining room.
- **Location**: The Golden Compass music hall
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Lily, Harper, George
- **POV**: Samuel (first Samuel POV chapter)
- **Significance**: Clara unarmed for the first time. Samuel sees her artistic depths. Lily's first appearance. Courtship crosses a threshold (hand-take).

### Morning After Chapter 4 — Chapter 5
- **Event**: Clara rearmed (leather coat, holsters, Colts, sword cane). Debriefs with Thomas. Thomas: "Harper tells me you sang like you used to. Confident. Bold." Thomas delivers the ten-item strategic plan (anti-trafficking). Wallace's art-world patronage and respectability network revealed. Clara orders Wallace's network fully mapped.
- **Location**: Thomas's office, Clara's townhouse
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Wallace thread deepens — power built on social respectability. Thomas dual-frequency seeds intensify. Clara's rearming after the Golden Compass continues the disarmament pattern.

### Shortly After Chapter 5 — Chapter 6
- **Event**: Clara visits Samuel at his warehouse on Front Street. Delivers Thomas's Wallace report. Samuel agrees to help dismantle Wallace's network. Riverfront walk — Samuel shares his father's story (Irish dock worker); Clara partially discloses her father wound ("A Chen does not fail"). Clara returns home carrying something she will not share with Thomas.
- **Location**: Samuel's warehouse (Front Street), Delaware riverfront
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, George, Thomas (off-page — prepared the report)
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Clara gives Samuel something she has never given Thomas — a glimpse of the wound beneath the empire. The emotional migration from Thomas to Samuel begins.

### Shortly After Chapter 6 — Chapter 7
- **Event**: Samuel arrives at Clara's townhouse for first training session. Thomas and Samuel meet for the first time — warm handshake in Thomas's office. Clara teaches Samuel martial arts in the courtyard (yielding principle from the Chen family art). Samuel put on the ground three times in three minutes. Thomas brings water and Wallace auction house intelligence (Chestnut Street, 14 lots in 6 months). Wooden practice blades for blade work.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse, Thomas's office, courtyard
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Thomas, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Thomas-Samuel first meeting. Samuel observes Clara-Thomas dynamic from outside. Clara most physically unguarded (practice clothes). Thomas dual-frequency seeds accumulate.

### ~Three Days After Chapter 7 — Chapter 8
- **Event**: Thomas's intelligence reveals Wallace is moving against Samuel's warehouses tonight. Clara assembles her full apparatus — Harper's team, Thomas's sharpshooters, and Samuel — for a coordinated defense. Twenty-three of Wallace's men attack Samuel's dock section. Captured documents reveal the true scale: seven ships, the Mercy Jane only one. One of Harper's men wounded.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse, Samuel's dock section, the waterfront
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Thomas, Harper, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: First coordinated operation. Wallace escalates from background threat to direct assault. Captured manifests reveal a fleet spanning Baltimore to Boston. Thomas as architect (sharpshooters, intelligence) but never beside Clara in the field.

### Morning After Chapter 8 — Chapter 9
- **Event**: Clara debriefs with Thomas (who worked through the night decoding captured documents). Seven ships, three ports, years of construction. Clara visits Harper's wounded man Nolan. Clara, Thomas, and Samuel hold their first three-way planning council at Thomas's desk. Samuel's commercial knowledge fills intelligence gaps. Clara invites Samuel to dinner — the first such invitation she has ever extended.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse — Thomas's office, Harper's quarters, the courtyard
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, Samuel, Harper, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: First three-way working scene. Samuel enters Clara's strategic inner sanctum. The dinner invitation marks an unprecedented personal gesture. Thomas registers the shift.

### Same Evening — Chapter 10
- **Event**: Samuel's first evening in Clara's home. The good silver, the dining room, Clara's father's Chinese landscape scroll. Thomas is brilliant at the table — warm, witty, knowing every preference. Thomas tells Clara's father's story (Cape of Good Hope, three crossings) — accurate but incomplete. When Thomas departs, Clara and Samuel cross from "Miss Chen" / "Mr. Taylor" to "Clara" / "Samuel." Hands meeting over the good silver.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse — the dining room
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Thomas, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: First-name threshold crossed. Thomas knows the father's facts but not the wound — the gap between fact and feeling. Thomas reads as generous on first pass; on re-read, the pen behind the wall resumes.

### Next Morning — Chapter 11
- **Event**: Clara returns to Thomas's office fully rearmed. Thomas presents the labor-fraud legal strategy against Wallace's trafficking fleet. Wallace retaliates: harbor complaints against Clara's three ships (Meridian, Cathay, Osprey) via sham concern Ashford and Crane. The Osprey carries hidden compartments for Clara's coastal rescue work. To save the work, Clara must bring Samuel into the deepest layer of her double life. Thomas recognizes this, accepts it, returns to work.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse — Thomas's office
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Wallace shifts from violence to legal/commercial warfare. Clara's three ships named for the first time. The Osprey's rescue function — and its vulnerability — revealed. Thomas's "Then show him" is professional advice and surrender.

### Same Morning — Chapter 12
- **Event**: Clara arrives at Samuel's warehouse fully armed. Takes him aboard the Osprey, where he sees the hidden compartments — blankets, medical supplies, forged documents — the physical apparatus of her coastal rescue work. 63 people carried total. Samuel commits his dock section, his workers, and himself. The courtship crosses from personal trust to working partnership.
- **Location**: Samuel's warehouse, Philadelphia docks, the Osprey
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Samuel enters the deepest layer of Clara's double life. His father's legacy (Irish dock worker from Wexford) connects to the Osprey's passengers. Thomas absent — working the counter-complaint at the desk while Samuel boards the Osprey with Clara.

### Same Afternoon/Evening — Chapter 13
- **Event**: Clara returns from the Osprey to find Thomas working counter-complaints. That night, Clara oversees the cargo transfer from the Osprey to Samuel's warehouse: medical supplies, documents, personal effects, blankets. A customs boat passes — ten seconds of held breath. Clara holds the child's coat for the first time. She walks home through docks that feel different than the night she killed twelve men.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse, Samuel's warehouse, Philadelphia docks
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, Samuel, Harper, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: The cargo move executed. Clara recognizes the child's coat — someone washes it and she doesn't know who. Thomas sent to the desk while Clara and Samuel execute the operation. The Chapter 1 bookend: same docks, different purpose.

### Two Days Later — Chapter 14
- **Event**: The Meridian and Cathay pass inspection. The Osprey is last — the compromised inspector Hale is assigned. Clara and Thomas go aboard. Below deck, Hale finds the concealed compartments — empty. Thomas argues the legal boundary. The inspector closes the complaint but notes "structural features of undetermined purpose" — language Thomas suggested. The Osprey is finished for rescue work. Clara goes to Samuel: "Then we build what comes next."
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse, Philadelphia docks, the Osprey, Samuel's warehouse
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, Samuel, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: The Osprey's compartments are now in the commission's record permanently. Thomas's most brilliant and loyal work — arguing from inside the hull, controlling how the secret enters the record. The same skill that will make his betrayal devastating.

### Shortly After Chapter 14 — Chapter 15
- **Event**: Wallace responds with commercial warfare — pulling Samuel's contracts and probing the warehouse through a commission clerk. Clara brings Samuel into Thomas's office for the new fleet design: six rotating vessels with temporary frames instead of one concealed ship. Samuel contributes the shore-trade strategy (Chesapeake provisions, Eastern Shore routes). After Thomas leaves, Samuel commits: "I choose the cargo."
- **Location**: Samuel's warehouse, Clara's townhouse (Thomas's office)
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Thomas, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Samuel enters Thomas's office — Thomas's territory. The new fleet system replaces the compromised Osprey. Samuel's "I choose the cargo" echoes his father's dock work. Thomas watching Clara watch Samuel is the sharpest dual-frequency seed.

### Late Autumn 1846 — Chapter 16
- **Event**: Thomas's intelligence reveals two threats: a formal commission inquiry into Samuel's warehouse, and Wallace securing Pacific shipping contracts — building toward San Francisco. Clara goes to Samuel and together they decide to go west. Clara returns to Thomas that night: he will manage the east while she and Samuel sail for California. Thomas converts his own displacement into documentation. The pen does not move.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse (Thomas's office), Samuel's warehouse, Thomas's office (night)
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, Samuel, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: The pivot west. Thomas's intelligence produces the decision that displaces him. Clara's father's Pacific routes give the decision personal weight. Thomas as eastern manager — the man left behind.

### January 1847 — Chapter 17
- **Event**: Clara rides north to Lily's sanctuary at Wissahickon Creek. Discovers Harper has been training Lily with rifles since autumn. Clara tells Lily about the voyage west. Lily asks to come: "Not out of fear. Out of choice." The expedition gains an artist who can shoot.
- **Location**: Wissahickon Creek — Lily's sanctuary
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Lily, Harper (off-page — trained Lily), George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Lily's choice transforms her from rescued to rescuer. Harper's secret kindness revealed — weekly visits, training, conversation. The orchard as the sanctuary Clara built for Lily's healing, now the place Lily chooses to leave.

### Late March 1847 — Chapter 18
- **Event**: Samuel closes his warehouse for the last time. Goes to Thomas's office for the formal transfer of Clara's affairs. Thomas presents the transition plan and the sailing manifest — every name written in his hand, his own name absent. "She builds. I hold what she builds." The Caroline waits at the south wharf. Three days.
- **Location**: Samuel's warehouse, Clara's townhouse (Thomas's office), the south wharf
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Thomas, Clara (off-page), George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Thomas alone with the manifest he wrote — his name absent. Samuel signs below Clara's name. Callaghan takes over the warehouse. The Caroline berthed, departure in three days. Thomas's correspondence system (coded letters, three ports: Kingston, Panama City, Monterey) extends his reach across the journey.

### April 3, 1847 — Chapter 19
- **Event**: Clara leaves the townhouse before dawn. Thomas's office is empty — he has already gone to the dock. At the south wharf, Thomas delivers his last confirmations. The company boards the Caroline: Samuel, Harper, Lily, the crew, the dogs. The lines are cast. Philadelphia recedes. Thomas stands on the pier, watching, until the pier itself is gone. Clara turns to the bow.
- **Location**: Clara's townhouse, the south wharf, aboard the Caroline, the Delaware River
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Thomas, Samuel, Harper, Lily, George, Ajax
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: The departure. Thomas at the dock before Clara — needing to witness. "I'll hold the east." The city shrinking behind the stern. Clara's full armament returns as she leaves safe territory. Lily on a ship for the first time since the kidnapping — steady, curious, drawing before they clear the bay.

### April 3–7, 1847 — Chapter 20
- **Event**: The Caroline clears Delaware Bay and enters the Atlantic. Samuel finds his role aboard — provisions, counting, the arithmetic of survival. On the third afternoon a squall tests the company. In the evening Clara plays her cello on the deck. Harper and Samuel talk at the rail under the stars. Harper's warning: the Isthmus.
- **Location**: Aboard the Caroline — Delaware Bay — the Atlantic Ocean
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Harper, Lily, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Shipboard life established. Harper's human depth revealed — not just security chief but keeper of people. Harper-Samuel bond formed at the rail. "Open water is honest — it's the harbors that lie." The Isthmus warning plants fear.

### April 15–16, 1847 — Chapter 21
- **Event**: The Caroline enters the Caribbean. A pirate schooner closes on them in the Windward Passage. Clara commands the defense — Colts, George, Harper's men. The company fights off the boarding. Samuel sent below to protect Lily. Clara notes one crew member who didn't fire and a thought she can't dismiss: the pirates came directly, as though they knew where to find her.
- **Location**: The Caribbean Sea — the Windward Passage
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Harper, Lily, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Caribbean violence as threshold. Clara's combat interiority. One of Thomas's crew members doesn't fire — planted seed. The pirates' direct approach hints at betrayal. Harper at his tactical finest.

### April 17–18, 1847 — Chapter 22
- **Event**: The Caroline puts in at Kingston for water and repairs. Samuel processes the fight he was kept from — what Lily said in the hold, what the sounds meant through the planking. Harper begins preparing the company for the Isthmus crossing. Samuel and Clara stand at the rail in the evening, not speaking about what they cannot yet say.
- **Location**: Kingston, Jamaica
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Harper, Lily, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Kingston as threshold between the known and the unknown. Samuel-Lily bond forged below during the fight. Harper's Isthmus preparation — quinine, water discipline, the jungle's arithmetic. The unspoken weight between Samuel and Clara deepens.

### April 22–24, 1847 — Chapter 23
- **Event**: The company arrives at the Chagres River mouth and begins the Isthmus crossing. Dugout bungos, local boatmen, the guide Reyes. Harper enforces the disciplines that keep people alive. George struggles with the jungle. Samuel writes by firelight. The correspondence code reaches its limit — the company beyond Thomas's reach for the first time.
- **Location**: Chagres, Panama — the Chagres River
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Harper, Lily, Reyes, George, Ajax
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Peak Harper competence — water discipline, mosquito netting, guide consultation. The jungle does not notice whether the things inside it live or die. The correspondence system breaks down — Thomas's infrastructure no longer reaches. Clara and Samuel: separate boats by day, shared firelight by night.

### April 25–26, 1847 — Chapter 24
- **Event**: The company reaches Cruces and begins the twenty-mile mule trail toward Panama City. Samuel's merchant body protests the jungle while his identity transforms — the pistol shifting from instrument to extension. Reyes finds signs of organized human presence on the trail. At a fireless night camp, Clara sits beside Samuel without touching. At dawn, Samuel checks his pistol the way Clara checks hers. Harper notices. Says nothing.
- **Location**: Cruces, Panama — the mule trail toward Panama City
- **Characters involved**: Samuel, Clara, Harper, Lily, Reyes, George, Ajax
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Samuel's transformation — unconscious imitation of Clara's weapon discipline. The trail signs suggest organized threat ahead. Clara's certainty becomes the thing Samuel loves. Harper's final morning as the man who stood between other people and what threatened them.
