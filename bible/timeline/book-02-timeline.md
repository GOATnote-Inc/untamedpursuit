# Book 2 — Detailed Timeline

<!-- Chapter-level chronology for Book 2. -->
<!-- Continues directly from Book 1, ch-24 (April 25-26, 1847, mule trail toward Panama City). -->

### April 26, 1847 — Chapter 1
- **Event**: Mule-trail ambush. Organized attackers — positioned, informed, disciplined. Harper mortally wounded covering the company (musket ball, belly, below ribs). "It's alright, Miss Chen." / "It is not." Company carries Harper toward Panama City.
- **Location**: Mule trail, Isthmus of Panama (8 miles from Panama City)
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Harper, Lily, Reyes, George, Ajax
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Inciting event of Book 2. Harper's last act of service. The ambush was organized — someone knew the route.

### April 26--27, 1847 — Chapter 2
- **Event**: Panama City. Clara operates on Harper — perforated bowel, death deferred not prevented. The night conversation: Harper reveals his past (wife who died young, daughter never met, war). Harper passes the manual — watch rotation, protocols, signals. Lily plays violin through the wall.
- **Location**: Panama City, room above a cantina
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Harper, Samuel, Lily, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Harper without formality. The conversation they never had. Clara operating on a man she cannot save.

### April 28, 1847 — Chapter 3
- **Event**: Harper dies in the hour before dawn. Last words: "Miss Chen." Clara: "Mr. Harper." Burial above the city, facing the Pacific. Pipe tobacco in the grave. George sits at the grave's edge. Clara turns toward the harbor.
- **Location**: Panama City
- **Characters involved**: Clara, Samuel, Lily, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Harper's death completed. Burial above the Pacific — the ocean he never crossed.

### Late April -- Early May 1847 — Chapter 4
- **Event**: Cholera in Panama City. Clara channels grief into medicine, treating strangers. Two crew fall ill (quarantined). Samuel manages provisions. Clara absorbs Harper's habits — scanning rooms, checking exits. Reyes finds a ship: the Resolute.
- **Location**: Panama City
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara, Lily, Reyes, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Cholera as obstacle. Clara unconsciously becoming Harper.

### Early May 1847 — Chapter 5
- **Event**: Samuel negotiates passage on the Resolute with Captain Morales. Samuel writes coded letter to Thomas (second of three ports — Panama City). Reports the ambush and Harper's death. Withholds suspicion about the organized nature of the attack. Two crew dead from cholera. Company diminished.
- **Location**: Panama City harbor
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara, Lily, Captain Morales, George
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: The coded letter — what Samuel writes and what he withholds. The correspondence system as both lifeline and leash.

### ~May 3, 1847 — Chapter 6
- **Event**: The Resolute clears Panama City harbor. Clara assigns cabins, maps the ship. Takes Harper's watch rotation (4--6 AM). The Pacific opens — vast, flat, featureless.
- **Location**: Panama City harbor / aboard the Resolute
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Lily, Captain Morales, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Departure. Clara deliberately inherits Harper's habits.

### ~May 4--5, 1847 — Chapter 7
- **Event**: Clara's first pre-dawn watch. Different constellations than Philadelphia. Memory of Harper's first day in her service. The company settles into shipboard life.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Lily, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Grief processed through duty. Harper's beginning remembered from the vantage of his end.

### ~May 6--10, 1847 — Chapter 8
- **Event**: Open water. Clara establishes new rhythms — morning weapons practice alone, afternoon review, evening with Lily and Samuel. Trains with blades against the mast. George waits for Harper. Clara has not removed her leather coat since Harper died.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Lily, Captain Morales, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Clara absorbing Harper's duties. The coat as grief and armor.

### ~May 10--14, 1847 — Chapter 9
- **Event**: Samuel writes in his private journal — the record Thomas's codes cannot hold. Conversation with Captain Morales about California (Monterey, SF, the settlements). Samuel records the ambush in his journal, leaves a blank space for the explanation. Lily's violin through the wall.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara, Lily, Captain Morales
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Samuel keeping two records. The blank space where the ambush explanation should go.

### ~May 15--18, 1847 — Chapter 10
- **Event**: Clara and Samuel talk about their parents for the first time. Clara tells the full father story (Canton, three Pacific crossings, "A Chen does not fail"). Samuel tells Clara about his mother (dock worker's wife, died quietly). Clara leans against Samuel — twelve inches collapsed.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean (stern, evening)
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Clara's father wound deepened. The courtship crosses from wanting to belonging. The intimacy of irrelevance.

### ~May 19--21, 1847 — Chapter 11
- **Event**: **Lily's first POV chapter.** Lily paints a Pacific sunset. Her filter revealed: everything measured against the six days in the hold. What she paints (light, George, beauty) and what she omits (shadows, Harper's empty space). Lily WATCHES — observation as survival skill from the hold. Sunset as choice: "The hold is six days. The sunsets are the rest of her life."
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean
- **Characters involved**: Lily (POV), Clara, Samuel, George
- **POV**: Lily
- **Significance**: Lily's interiority revealed. The measurement system established: beauty as proof against darkness.

### ~May 22--25, 1847 — Chapter 12
- **Event**: Clara and Lily's daily rhythm: morning tea, afternoon painting, evening music. Wine evenings — bottles from Wissahickon Creek. Lily plays a melody Harper used to whistle, transformed and made hers. Clara realizes she no longer thinks of Lily as someone who needs protection.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Lily, Samuel
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Clara's perception of Lily shifts. Harper's whistle transformed into Lily's melody.

### ~May 26--28, 1847 — Chapter 13
- **Event**: **Lily's second POV chapter.** Lily tells Samuel about Harper's teaching. The cost: the hold returns every time she speaks of it. The teaching sequence: rifle -> knife -> dog -> "He wanted me to choose safety." Why she tells Samuel: she has measured him and found him worthy. Samuel's response: "Thank you." Two words.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean (foredeck, morning)
- **Characters involved**: Lily (POV), Samuel
- **POV**: Lily
- **Significance**: Lily's measurement system escalated to measuring people. Harper's legacy transmitted. Samuel-Lily bond forged.

### Late May -- Early June 1847 — Chapter 14
- **Event**: Samuel processes Lily's story. Journal: "He taught her what I could not" crossed out, replaced with "What I would not." California coast appears. Samuel thinks about Thomas — distance as both control and separation. A letter to Thomas composed in his mind but never written.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean (approaching California coast)
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara, Lily
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Samuel's reckoning with his own choices. California sighted.

### ~June 5--7, 1847 — Chapter 15
- **Event**: **Lily's third POV chapter.** Monterey ashore. Lily observes Clara and Samuel reading the settlement differently. Lily and Samuel walk the shore. Lily's question: "Clara trusts him entirely. Do you?" Samuel: "Thomas has managed everything Clara built." Lily: "That is not the same thing." The measurement system's verdict: Thomas has built everything but risked nothing.
- **Location**: Monterey, California
- **Characters involved**: Lily (POV), Samuel, Clara
- **POV**: Lily
- **Significance**: The Thomas thread cracked open. Lily measures Thomas from 3,000 miles and finds him wanting. The question that will echo through Book 3.

### ~June 7--9, 1847 — Chapter 16
- **Event**: Third coded letter to Thomas (Monterey). Samuel writes facts, omits Lily's question. Intelligence: Wallace's agents scouting SF. Samuel seals with Thomas's wax — wonders whether the system serves Clara or Thomas. The Resolute prepares for SF.
- **Location**: Monterey, California
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara, Captain Morales
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Third and final coded letter. Samuel doubting the correspondence system's loyalties.

### ~June 9--10, 1847 — Chapter 17
- **Event**: Wallace intelligence distilled — agents in SF, harbor positioning. Clara plans without Harper: maps approaches with Captain Morales. Clara's strategy: prevent Wallace from rooting in the west. The Golden Gate. Resolute enters San Francisco Bay.
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute / San Francisco Bay
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Captain Morales
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Strategic planning for Wallace confrontation. San Francisco Bay entered.

### ~June 10--11, 1847 — Chapter 18
- **Event**: San Francisco Bay. The settlement — a few hundred people, Yerba Buena recently renamed. Clara reads the harbor. Lily paints the bay. A boat approaches the Resolute.
- **Location**: San Francisco Bay
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Lily, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Arrival. The western anchor. A city waiting to be built.

### ~June 12--14, 1847 — Chapter 19
- **Event**: Company goes ashore. Jack Garrett appears — early twenties, lean, weathered, gun oil and sage. He's been working horses and running dispatches. Heard about the woman from Philadelphia with the black dog. Jack meets Clara, Samuel, Lily, George. George allows the touch.
- **Location**: San Francisco settlement
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Jack, Samuel, Lily, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Jack's entrance. George's positive assessment. A new variable.
- **Continuity note**: [Jack's age in 1847 requires reconciliation with Pony Express (1860-1861) timeline — see ch-19 outline.]

### ~June 15--17, 1847 — Chapter 20
- **Event**: Jack provides intelligence on Wallace's agents (shipping clerk, harbor scout). Then: "I don't trust Thomas." Clara's fury — hot, personal. "You don't know Thomas." Jack: "I'm telling you what I see." Clara dismisses him. George stays on the porch, looking after Jack.
- **Location**: San Francisco settlement
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Jack, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: "I don't trust Thomas" — first external accusation. Clara's blind spot tested. George's split read.

### ~June 17--19, 1847 — Chapter 21
- **Event**: Samuel heard the argument. His interior: Lily's question + Jack's accusation + his own blank space. Does not confront Clara. Clara: "Do you agree with him?" Samuel: "I don't know enough to agree with anyone." The crack — not between them, between Clara and her certainty. The blind spot holds.
- **Location**: San Francisco settlement
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Samuel's silence louder than Jack's accusation. "I don't know enough" is not "No."

### ~June 19--22, 1847 — Chapter 22
- **Event**: Clara maps SF — infrastructure, commerce, opportunity. Secures a harbor building. Samuel anchors through contracts. Lily's paintings attract attention (and are also maps). Jack rides in and out, building an informal network — no ciphers, just personal trust.
- **Location**: San Francisco settlement
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Lily, Jack
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: San Francisco foothold established. Jack's informal network vs. Thomas's formal system.

### ~June 23--25, 1847 — Chapter 23
- **Event**: Wallace's agents move — attempt exclusive harbor contract. Clara intercepts with a better proposal. Night retaliation: Wallace's men attack the trading post. Clara ready — Harper's protocols. George at the door, Samuel with pistol, Jack with rifle from the rooftop. Fight brief and decisive. Two captured, scout escapes. Clara holds the harbor.
- **Location**: San Francisco settlement and harbor
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), Samuel, Jack, George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: Wallace confrontation. Clara running Harper's security protocols in combat. Jack proves competence through action.

### ~June 25--27, 1847 — Chapter 24
- **Event**: Captured men provide intelligence. Wallace's SF operation was scouting, not invasion — real force in the east. But Wallace expected Clara in California. He knew her route. The question: who told Wallace? Thomas's coded letters route through three ports. Samuel almost follows the thread. Closes his journal.
- **Location**: San Francisco harbor
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Wallace SF operation defeated. The informed-source thread connects ambush to Wallace to Thomas's network. Samuel refuses to finish the thought.

### ~June 28--30, 1847 — Chapter 25
- **Event**: Clara traces the mule-trail ambush — organized, positioned, someone in Thomas's correspondence network informed the wrong people. Clara chooses not to follow the thread. Not denial — decision. The cost of certainty is higher than the cost of trust. The blind spot holds, but it is no longer invisible.
- **Location**: San Francisco settlement
- **Characters involved**: Clara (POV), George
- **POV**: Clara
- **Significance**: "Victory with cost." Clara aware of the thread, choosing not to follow it. The cost of empire.

### ~July 1, 1847 — Chapter 26
- **Event**: Jack departs south — "defensible base of operations." Lily paints the SF waterfront at sunset, keeps it. Samuel's final journal entry: inventory of gains and losses. Clara on the waterfront, George at her side. Final image: Thomas in Philadelphia, in the office with Persian rugs, the pen moving, the manifest with every name but his own. The golden shore.
- **Location**: San Francisco waterfront
- **Characters involved**: Samuel (POV), Clara, Jack, Lily, George; Thomas (final image — Philadelphia)
- **POV**: Samuel
- **Significance**: Jack's departure (Book 3 seed). Lily keeping her painting. The final image — Thomas three thousand miles east, the crack in the foundation. The golden shore.
