# Relationship Map

> Cross-reference with individual character profiles in `bible/characters/`.

## Core Relationships

### Clara <-> Eve
- **Type**: Mentor-Student → Peer → Legacy
- **Summary**: Clara trains Eve from childhood to inherit her network and philosophy.
- **Clara's perspective**: Sees Eve as her greatest achievement — "You have fire." Trusts Eve to evolve, not imitate. "Your turn now. Make it better."
- **Eve's perspective**: Clara is everything — idol, teacher, peer. "You taught me everything I know, Clara. And not just about fighting or surviving. You've taught me how to live."
- **Evolution**:
  - Era 1: Eve's summers at Glass Garden (age 5+). Sword, medicine, philosophy.
  - Late Era 2: Clara dying, reveals full network. "My dear, you started years ago."
  - Post-Clara: Eve teaches Jessie the same lessons. "A sword is not a weapon..."
- **Key scenes**: First sword lesson (Claras_Fortress), Diagnosis (unravelled_clara), Sign language handoff (Clara_death_speech), Deathbed (Clara_with_Eve_on_her_death_bed), Epilogue letter (Epilogue_Claras_Final_Words)
- **Tension points**: Eve must find her own way, not merely replicate Clara. Clara's manipulation vs. Eve's autonomy.

### Clara <-> Samuel Taylor
- **Type**: Romantic → Partnership → Co-leadership. Samuel is Clara's river.
- **Summary**: From mutual assessment to marriage and building an empire together. Samuel is Clara's competent companion, lover, partner — the water in her world of pain, sadness, and struggle. He is her river: the life-giving current that sustains her.
- **Clara's perspective**: Attracted to his curiosity, ethics, and boldness. "He sits at our table intellectually." Enjoys the irony of her hidden depths surprising him. He is her anchor, her calm, her river.
- **Samuel's perspective**: Awed by Clara's depth. Weeps at her music. "A businessman who knows when words are inadequate." Devoted, gives her space.
- **Evolution**:
  - 1846: Night Watch rescue → tea → courtship
  - Golden Compass: "Crossed an important threshold"
  - Marriage: Co-leadership of the network
  - Clara's illness: Samuel stoic but fractured. Clara asks Eve to help him cope.
- **Key scenes**: Night Watch rescue (Episode I), Golden Compass performance (Golden_Compass_Stage_Episode), Diagnosis (unravelled_clara), Death gathering (Claras_Words_at_Sunset)
- **Tension points**: Clara hides illness from Samuel; his grief threatens to consume him.

### Clara <-> Kate Blackwood Garrett
- **Type**: Professional → Deep Friendship → Operational Partnership
- **Summary**: Two brilliant women who recognize each other as equals across cultures and disciplines. Kate's maiden name is Blackwood.
- **Clara's perspective**: Kate is the only person who deduced her secret life through observation alone. Respects Kate's uncompromising standards. "[Kate] died a fucking legend."
- **Kate's perspective**: Clara is a puzzle and a partner. "Anyone who can stitch like that while being shot at has already joined the family."
- **Evolution**:
  - Meeting: Multiple versions exist across source files (anatomy lab, fever outbreak, Colorado ambush) — to be reconciled by author
  - Partnership: Medical and operational collaboration
  - Kate sends Eve for summers at Glass Garden — trusts Clara completely
- **Key scenes**: Anatomy lab (kate_and_clara_in_anatomy_lab), Fever outbreak 1854 (kate_meet_clara), Colorado ambush 1875 (Clara_Meets_Kate)
- **Tension points**: Kate is alive at Clara's death. Kate dies later in the train attack. Clara does not carry grief for Kate — Kate carries grief for Clara.

### Clara <-> Lily Montgomery
- **Type**: Rescue → Healing → Artistic Bond → Lifelong Companion
- **Summary**: Forged in six days of captivity, deepened through art and mutual healing.
- **Clara's perspective**: Lily protected her when she was starving and helpless. The debt is incalculable. "Friend seemed too simple a word."
- **Lily's perspective**: Clara showed her a different kind of strength. Requests training "Not out of fear... Out of choice."
- **Evolution**:
  - Kidnapping: Six days of captivity forge an unbreakable bond
  - Sanctuary: Wissahickon Creek orchard — Lily heals through growing things
  - California: Lily moved by sunsets, preserves Harper's memory
  - Violin duets with Clara at the Golden Compass
- **Key scenes**: Kidnapping and rescue (Lilys_orchard), Orchard request (Lilys_orchard), Golden Compass duet (Golden_Compass_Stage_Episode)
- **Tension points**: Lily's vulnerability vs. growing strength. Her grief at Harper's death.

### Clara <-> Thomas Arlington
- **Type**: Oldest Friendship → Jealousy → Betrayal
- **Summary**: Clara's first and oldest friend. Never a love interest — a friend, but a jealous one. Thomas managed Clara's affairs and was the architect of her infrastructure. When Clara's world expanded to include Samuel, Jack, and others, Thomas was left to manage the details while she gave her heart and trust to others. His jealousy was not romantic — it was the jealousy of the indispensable man who discovers he is dispensable. Thomas betrays Clara. He is killed by Lily Montgomery. (Note: Thomas Arlington is a separate character from Thomas Mitchell in Eve's era. Mitchell may be Arlington's son or protege.)
- **Clara's perspective**: Trusted him completely. "I love you, Thomas" / "And well, you should" — a rare, genuine exchange between them (book-01, ch-02). Clara's blindspot — she trusted the familiar and did not see the jealousy curdling beneath.
- **Thomas Arlington's perspective**: Devoted at first. Then sidelined. The wit turns bitter, the familiarity becomes weaponized. He convinced himself his betrayal was rational when it was emotional.
- **Evolution**:
  - Early: Clara's oldest friend, intelligence chief, teasing confidant. Managed her social calendar for years — deflecting suitors, declining marriage proposals. Has attended her Golden Compass performances.
  - Ch-06 pivot: Clara chooses not to tell Thomas about her father disclosure to Samuel — "She had never shown Thomas the wound. She had shown him the empire built over it." Clara begins giving Samuel things she never gave Thomas.
  - Mid: Clara meets Samuel and Jack. Thomas left to manage affairs. The center shifts away from him.
  - Late: Jealousy curdles into action. Thomas betrays Clara. Jack's instinct ("I don't trust Thomas") vindicated.
  - Death: Killed by Lily Montgomery.
- **Key scenes**: Post-Night Watch debrief (Episode I), Discussion of Samuel (Fix_beginning), "I don't trust Thomas" (outline_2_16), The betrayal (TBD), Death at Lily's hand (TBD)
- **Tension points**: Jack was right all along. Clara's greatest weakness was trusting her oldest friend. Lily's weapons training pays off in the most devastating way.

### Lily <-> Thomas Arlington
- **Type**: Executioner
- **Summary**: Lily kills Thomas Arlington when he betrays Clara. The gentle artist who requested weapons training "Not out of fear... Out of choice" proves that her choice was real. This is the payoff of Lily's entire arc.
- **Key significance**: The gentlest character in the story commits the most personally devastating act of violence. Validates Theme 7 (Strength Redefined) — true strength is the wisdom to act when necessary.

### Clara <-> Harper
- **Type**: Professional → Security Chief → Trusted Protector
- **Summary**: Harper follows Clara in shadows, resigned to her independence, devoted to her safety.
- **Clara's perspective**: Relies on him completely for security. Trusts his judgment.
- **Harper's perspective**: Concerned, deferential, quietly proud. "Miss Chen, I am far from defenseless."
- **Evolution**: Harper serves Clara faithfully until his death during the voyage west.
- **Key scenes**: Night Watch (Episode I), Backstage flowers for Sam (Golden_Compass), Death on the voyage (outline_2_16 Scene 21)
- **Tension points**: Harper's death is a major loss. Lily carries his memory.

### Eve <-> Jessie
- **Type**: Crisis Bond → Best Friends → Chosen Family → Operational Partners
- **Summary**: Forged on the train attack, deepened through shared grief and danger. 10 years of friendship.
- **Eve's perspective**: Jessie is her anchor. The person she tells "meaningless stories" to so memories don't fade.
- **Jessie's perspective**: Eve is brilliant but too serious. Jessie provides levity, loyalty, and dynamite. "You can be so needy."
- **Evolution**:
  - Train attack: Jessie saves Eve with three dynamite sticks
  - Partnership: Silver Creek operations, Snake River crossing
  - Grief: Monterey cliffs — "Can I tell you stories that are meaningless?"
  - Glass Garden: Eve teaches Jessie the sword — "A sword is not a weapon..."
- **Key scenes**: Train rescue (Iron_and_Blood), Monterey cliffs grief (Pacific_Monterey_cliffs), Snake River crossing (Chapter_Eight_Spring_Melt), Sword lesson (Claras_Fortress), Art quarter invitation (Jessie_considers_Art)
- **Tension points**: Jessie's wildness vs. Eve's restraint. Jessie's romance with Captain Andrews could pull her away.

### Eve <-> Jack Garrett
- **Type**: Father-Daughter → Peers → Loss
- **Summary**: Jack teaches Eve shooting, tracking, and "reading air." Their shared ritual is shooting practice.
- **Eve's perspective**: Dad smelled of "gun oil and sage." Taught through silence. His Winchester is her most treasured possession after his death.
- **Jack's perspective**: Quiet pride. "Smart woman, your mother." Sees Eve as a peer, not just a child.
- **Evolution**: From childhood training to shared professional respect. Ended by the train attack.
- **Key scenes**: Shooting practice (Eve_and_shooting_with_Jack), Train attack (Iron_and_Blood)
- **Tension points**: Jack's death is the defining trauma alongside Kate's.

### Eve <-> Kate Blackwood Garrett
- **Type**: Mother-Daughter → Medical Mentor → Loss
- **Summary**: Kate teaches Eve medicine, precision, and pattern recognition. Her accent sharpened when worried.
- **Eve's perspective**: Mother taught through precision. "Not the big moments. But the small ones."
- **Kate's perspective**: Demands excellence. "She has her own mind."
- **Evolution**: Kate's exacting standards shape Eve's surgical skill. Ended by the train attack.
- **Key scenes**: Train attack — "Go! Now! We hold here. This is our train." (Iron_and_Blood)
- **Tension points**: Kate's death. Eve inherits her medical bag and standards.

### Eve <-> Thomas Mitchell
- **Type**: Suspicion → Chemistry → Trust → Romance
- **Summary**: Thomas Mitchell is a separate, younger man from Thomas Arlington (Clara's era). He arrives at Glass Garden connected to the Durand family — he is himself a Durand. Eve is warned about him by Jack. (Mitchell may be Arlington's son or protege, carrying forward a generational dynamic.)
- **Eve's perspective**: Wary, then drawn to him despite warnings. His loyalty proven in battle.
- **Thomas Mitchell's perspective**: Drawn to Eve's capability. Confronts his own family for her.
- **Evolution**: From suspicion through midnight chemistry to partnership. Eve's impossible shot saves him.
- **Key scenes**: Arrival at Glass Garden, Midnight encounter, Tunnel retreat (outline_for_fierce_11_27)
- **Tension points**: Durand family connection (Thomas Mitchell is a Durand by blood). Jack's warning about the Thomas name — distrust may transfer generationally from Arlington to Mitchell.

### Eve <-> Ghost
- **Type**: Inherited Bond → Companion
- **Summary**: Ghost was Jack's dog. After the train attack, Ghost becomes Eve's — carrying Jack's "steady patience."
- **Evolution**: Ghost howled for 3 days after the attack. Now takes point, provides flank security, and is Eve's most loyal companion.
- **Key scenes**: Train attack (Iron_and_Blood), Silver Creek raid (Chapter_Seven), Snake River (Chapter_Eight), Monterey cliffs (Pacific_Monterey)

### Kate <-> Jack
- **Type**: Crisis → Love → Marriage → Frontier Partnership
- **Summary**: Met during the 1860 Philadelphia riot. Fell in love within a week. Built a life on the frontier.
- **Kate's perspective**: Balances Jack's warmth with her precision. Partners in everything.
- **Jack's perspective**: "Smart woman, your mother." Humorous foil to Kate's intensity.
- **Key scenes**: Philadelphia riot (Clara_Meets_Kate prologue), Shooting practice (Eve_and_shooting_with_Jack), Death together on the train (Iron_and_Blood)

### Lily <-> Harper
- **Type**: Student → Grief
- **Summary**: Harper showed Lily kindness and taught her during the voyage west. His death is a defining loss for her.
- **Evolution**: Harper's weekly lessons. Lily preserves his memory through storytelling.
- **Key scenes**: Lily tells the story of Harper's kindness (outline_2_16)

### Samuel <-> Thomas Arlington
- **Type**: Colleagues → Rivals (one-sided)
- **Summary**: Both serve Clara's vision initially. Thomas Arlington handles intelligence; Samuel handles operations and finance. But Thomas's jealousy of Samuel — the man who became Clara's river, her companion, her partner — is the seed of Thomas's betrayal. Samuel didn't steal Thomas's place; Clara simply found something deeper. Thomas could not accept it.
- **First meeting**: Thomas greets Samuel warmly in his office — handshake, compliments on the Wallace report, "She's mentioned you rather more than she mentions most people." (book-01, ch-07) On first read: charming hospitality. On re-read: Thomas sizing up his replacement.

### Eve <-> Morgan
- **Type**: Student → Colleague → 6+ Year Partnership
- **Summary**: Morgan was placed near Eve by Clara's network. They work together at the clinic.
- **Evolution**: From "protecting your family's interests" to genuine working partnership.
- **Key scenes**: Clinic inventory (kate_meet_clara framing), One year after Venezuela
