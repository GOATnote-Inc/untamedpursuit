# Compaction Anchor — Book 2, Chapter 11

## Chapter
- **Book**: 2
- **Chapter**: 11
- **Title**: "What She Paints"
- **POV**: Lily Montgomery
- **Timeline**: ~May 19--21, 1847
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean

## POV Character State
Lily is healing through art. She paints every sunset since Panama — each one is evidence filed against the six days in the hold. She measures the world chromatically: everything on a scale from zero (the hold, darkness, enclosure) to maximum (this sunset, this light, this moment). She does not fight the hold — she outnumbers it. She is the most observant person on the ship because observation is how she survived captivity.

## Outline Beats
1. Lily's first POV — how she sees the world through color, light, and the gap between palette and reality
2. Pacific sunset painting — pigments mixed, the line of fire on the water
3. The hold as zero point — everything measured against six days of darkness. Beauty as proof.
4. What she paints (light, George) vs. what she omits (shadows, Harper's empty space). Omissions deliberate.
5. Lily WATCHES — Clara's pre-dawn walk, Samuel's journal, crew avoiding George. Observation as survival skill.
6. Harper's core gift through Lily's lens: choice. "Not out of fear. Out of choice."
7. Sunset as proof: "The hold is six days. The sunsets are the rest of her life."

## Voice Notes
- Metaphor domain: natural, artistic, sensory (color, light, seasons, pigments, textures)
- Measurement filter: scale from zero (hold) to maximum (this moment)
- The hold: "six days" — appears as lens, not wound. She looks through it, not at it.
- Short sentences for impact. Long sentences for the reaching quality of mixing color.
- No military, commercial, or legal metaphors.
- Key principle: voice should feel like painting — reaching for something true, accepting the gap.
