## Chapter
- **Book**: 2

- **Title**: "Music Through the Wall"
- **POV**: Clara Chen
- **Timeline**: ~May 22--25, 1847
- **Location**: Aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean

## POV Character State
Clara is still wearing the coat, still running Harper's watch rotation, still holding the architecture of control. But something is shifting. She leaned against Samuel three days ago (ch-10). She has been watching Lily paint every evening. The grief is not gone but it has developed a rhythm — the watch, the blade practice, the coat — and into that rhythm Lily has inserted herself with morning tea and evening music. Clara has not asked for any of it. Clara does not ask.

## Outline Beats
1. Shipboard routine deepening — Clara and Lily's daily rhythm: morning tea (Lily brings it, Clara never asks), afternoon painting on the stern, evening music
2. Wine evenings — Lily produces bottles from Wissahickon Creek orchard provisions. "Something that grows. That changes with the seasons."
3. The cello — Lily convinced Captain Morales to bring it topside from the hold
4. Music through the wall at night — Lily plays a melody Harper used to whistle, transformed and made hers
5. Clara's recognition: she has stopped thinking of Lily as someone who needs protection. Lily has changed — or Clara's perception has changed.

## Previous Chapter Landing (ch-11)
Lily's first POV chapter. Eighteen sunsets painted since Panama. The hold as zero on her scale. What she paints vs. omits. Lily watches Clara walk like stitches in a wound. The coat as bark. Harper gave Lily back the choosing. Chapter ends with Lily in her cabin, afterlight behind her eyelids, Clara's boots above.
