# Compaction Anchor — Book 2, Chapter 7

## Chapter
Book 2, Chapter 7 — "Harper's Watch"

## POV
Clara Chen. She has taken Harper's pre-dawn watch rotation (established ch-06). This is her first full watch alone — 4 to 6 AM. She is processing grief through duty. Her metaphor domain is architectural/spatial.

## Outline Beats
1. Clara's first pre-dawn watch. George at her side. The stars over the Pacific — different constellations than Philadelphia. She stands where Harper would have stood and sees what Harper would have seen.
2. The company settling into shipboard life. Lily sketches at the stern. Samuel reviews the provisions manifest. The crew of the Resolute keeps their distance from Clara's company — they've seen George, they've seen Clara's Colts.
3. Clara thinks about what she's building. Philadelphia is three thousand miles and two months behind. Thomas holds the east. She is moving toward something she cannot yet see. Harper would have said nothing. He would have stood at her shoulder and watched the horizon.
4. A memory: Harper's first day in her service, four years ago. What he said. What she said. How he nodded and took his position and never left it until a musket ball on a jungle trail made the decision for him.
5. Dawn breaks. Clara's watch ends. She goes below. The ship moves west.

## Bible Facts Loaded
- Clara: architectural/spatial metaphors, leather coat not removed since Harper's death, Colts in holsters, sword cane. Takes pre-dawn watch 4-6 AM. Father crossed Pacific three times. "A Chen does not fail."
- Harper: 40s, weathered face, loyal eyes. Watched Clara for three days before presenting himself with letter of introduction. "Miss Chen" always. Security chief four years. Death: April 28, 1847. Pipe tobacco (Virginia leaf). Wife Margaret died while he was in Florida.
- Samuel: merchant metaphors, Paterson loaded, reviewing provisions
- Lily: artist, violin, paints, steady. Sketches at the stern. Brought tea ch-06.
- George: 140 lbs, responds to Clara's commands. Walks with Clara on watch. Nails click on deck.
- Captain Morales: practical, unsentimental, running the ship
- Company: Clara, Samuel, Lily, Reyes, Cole, Jennings, George, Ajax — six passengers, two dogs

## Previous Chapter Landing
Ch-06 ended with Clara and Lily at the stern rail drinking tea, watching the Pacific light. "The Pacific moved beneath them, and the ship moved west, and Clara did not remove the coat, and the morning held."
