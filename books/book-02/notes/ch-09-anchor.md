# Compaction Anchor — Book 2, Chapter 9

## Chapter: Book 2, Chapter 9 — "Samuel's Journal"
## POV: Samuel Taylor — the merchant's mind applied to uncountable things. Provisions done; now inventorying people, trust, suspicion. Not yet suspecting Thomas — but recording a blank space where the explanation should go.

## Outline Beats
1. Samuel writes in private journal — not Thomas's codes but a separate record. What codes cannot hold: the color of the water, Lily's violin through the wall, Clara walking the deck at 4 AM.
2. Samuel's interior — counting what he has instead of what he's lost. Clara, Lily, George, the Resolute, provisions, distance to California. The letters to Thomas and the things he leaves out.
3. Conversation with Captain Morales about California — Monterey, SF Bay, settlements. A few hundred Americans, Mexican rancheros, mission remnants. Empty and available.
4. Samuel thinks about Thomas — correspondence codes, the manifest with Thomas's name absent. Does not suspect — but records the ambush and leaves a blank space.
5. Through the wall: Lily's violin. Samuel stops writing, listens.

## Bible Facts Loaded
- Samuel: 30s, Irish father (Wexford docks), merchant metaphors (ledgers, cargo, inventory), Paterson Colt, boxing-trained, art collector. Candid speech, comfortable with silence. First combat use of Colt in ambush (ch-01). Weeps at Clara's music.
- Thomas: cipher system (substitution, port-specific keys — Kingston: Adam Smith, Panama: Blackstone). Red wax seal (brass case, T.A. initials, Chestnut Street engraver). Manifest written with his own name absent.
- Morales: brown-skinned, weathered, Spanish accent, clay pipe (held but unlit), competent and incurious. Has sailed the California coast.
- Clara: 4-6 AM watch, leather coat not removed, blade practice alone on foredeck
- Lily: violin, painting systematically, apple wood frames, linseed oil
- Company: Clara, Samuel, Lily, Reyes, Cole, Jennings, George, Ajax
