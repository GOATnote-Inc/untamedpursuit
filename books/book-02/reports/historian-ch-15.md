# Historian Report — Book 2, Chapter 15

## Errors

- **Custom House description (line 49)**: "the Custom House — the largest building in the settlement, which was not large, one story of adobe with a broad veranda." By 1846, the Custom House had two-story wings with balconies at each end (Larkin additions 1841-46). The central section was one story, but the wings were two-storied, hip-roofed, with balconies. Describing it as simply "one story of adobe" is inaccurate.
  - Source: SAH Archipedia, NPS, Monterey County Historical Society
  - Suggestion: "the Custom House — the largest building in the settlement, adobe at the center with two-storied wings at each end, balconies above, and an American flag hanging slack in the windless air."

## Anachronisms (mild)

- **"The golden hour" (line 165)**: Photography/cinematography term. Exact coinage date uncertain but most commonly associated with late 19th-20th century technical usage. Natural enough as a general description of warm sunset light. Retainable if desired; alternatives: "the last light," "the hour before sunset."
