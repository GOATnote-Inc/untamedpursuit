# Prose Brilliance Report — Chapter 12

## What Works

- **Wine scene**: Lily's dialogue about the vines ("Adequate means the vines have decided to stay") is specific, unsentimental, and does triple duty. The chapter's best writing.
- **Music-through-the-wall**: Clara hearing Harper's whistle rebuilt by Lily is genuinely moving. The carpenter metaphor (taking apart, studying joints, rebuilding) is Clara's domain applied to Lily's creation — the first moment where Clara's lens serves Lily's art rather than her own control.
- **"The melody held"**: Final sentence does double duty — music + architecture. Clara's vocabulary applied to Lily's creation. The chapter's thesis in two words.

## Issues Addressed in Revision

1. **Coat removal offscreen** → Dramatized: fingers on buttons, weight leaving shoulders, muscles confused by missing load, air sharp against linen. "She had not decided to take it off." The undoing is not a decision but an arrival.

2. **Admiration ratio ~7:1** → Complicating observation added: "Lily never went anywhere without Ajax at her heel — not once since the orchard. Clara filed this without classifying it." Not a misreading — a gap in the thesis.

3. **Tell-after-show / triple restatement** → Cut two of three layers. Anaphora trimmed 5→3 items. Vine/root passage removed from recognition section. Final vine simile in music section cut.

4. **Samuel as furniture** → Added one line of dialogue: "'Not bought,' he said. Not a question. Lily almost smiled." Shows merchant's palate, independent assessment.

5. **Vine metaphor in Clara's POV** → Kept one instance (line 105, the music-as-inheritance simile) where it's earned by the scene's emotional logic. Cut second use (recognition section) and third use (music coda). The single slip of Clara reaching for Lily's vocabulary is the point — the music is changing how she thinks.

6. **Exit structure** → "The melody held." — Active verb (bearing weight) rather than passive reception. Breaks the 5-chapter pattern of stillness/receiving endings.

## Remaining Note

- **5 competencies confirmed, 0 tested**: Clara reads everything correctly in real time. The only "failure" is retrospective (she was wrong about Lily). The coat removal adds physical cost but not perceptual cost. Acceptable for a quiet recognition chapter, but the book needs combat/challenge chapters to test Clara's capabilities against real resistance (ch-17–18 should deliver this).
