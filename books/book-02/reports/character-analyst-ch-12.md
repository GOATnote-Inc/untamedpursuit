# Character Analyst Report — Chapter 12

## Clara Chen (POV)

Voice consistent. Architectural metaphor domain rigorously maintained — rooms, walls, load-bearing structures, structural integrity. The chapter's central revelation ("Lily does not need protecting") lands precisely because the metaphor is Clara's own: she discovers a wall was a curtain, a room was outgrown.

Internal contradiction alive: Clara recognizes her protective framework was a limitation, but can only describe the recognition through the same architectural lens that created the limitation. Even self-awareness operates through the system being questioned.

## Lily Montgomery

Voice distinct: natural/artistic/sensory. "Something that grows. That changes with the seasons." Speech is spare, image-driven, rooted in the organic world. Practical observation ("Chop breaks the reflection") shows craftsman competence. All experiential grounding verified — hold, orchard, Ajax, weapons training, Golden Compass.

Post-revision: complicating observation added — Lily never goes anywhere without Ajax. Clara files this without classifying it. Adds texture to the independence thesis.

## Samuel Taylor

Pre-revision: geometric furniture — no dialogue, no desire, no agency. Post-revision: one line added ("Not bought" — merchant's palate reading the wine). Brief, in character, shows independent assessment capability.

## Harper (memory)

Exists as absence and structural memory. Idle whistle as personal habit — new fact, first named here but defensible given four years of proximity. Lily's transformation of the whistle into violin architecture is the chapter's strongest cross-character element.

## Relationships

- **Clara-Lily**: Pivots from rescue/healing to partnership. Earned through accumulated daily rhythm.
- **Clara-Samuel**: Warm, stable, backgrounded. Appropriate for this chapter's focus.
- **Lily-Harper (memory)**: Preservation through transformation — not replication. Vine metaphor (inheritance through tending).
