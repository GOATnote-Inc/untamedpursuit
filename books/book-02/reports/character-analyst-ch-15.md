# Character Analyst Report — Book 2, Chapter 15

## Lily Montgomery (POV)

Voice strongly consistent with profile. Natural/artistic/sensory metaphor domain maintained: colors, palettes, brushstrokes, pigments, canvases. Legal/evidentiary framework (scales, measurement, verdict) continues from ch-11 and ch-13.

### Arc Progress
Culmination of three-chapter arc: beauty (ch-11) → people (ch-13) → absent person (ch-15). The measurement system now produces verdicts about characters who are not present. Lily has moved from observed to observer to judge.

### Experiential Grounding

All claims verified:
- Hold dimensions and teaching → ch-11 (lines 46-65)
- Loose button on Clara's coat → ch-11 (lines 131-137)

She hears the pen through the cabin wall and notes two registers but "did not know the contents of either." This is authorial knowledge leaking into Lily's POV.

**Two weakly grounded items**:

- Thomas's office details — rugs, maps, clock (line 77): From Clara's interiority. Defensible as information Clara would share with her closest friend.

- **Lily/Samuel**: Deepened. Lily trusts Samuel with "something harder" than Harper's story. Samuel's silence echoes ch-13 (hands out of pockets, quality of stillness changing). Bond consistent with bible.
- **Lily/Clara (observed)**: Lily watches Clara read exits. Notes the coat, the loose button, George at her side. Sees that Clara "does not see the difference" because she loves Thomas. Consistent with "sees what Clara won't."
- **Lily/Harper (memory)**: Present through his absence — his verbs weighed against Thomas's. Structural, not decorative.
