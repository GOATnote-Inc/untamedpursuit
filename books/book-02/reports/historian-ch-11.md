# Historian Report — Chapter 11

## Errors (Fixed in Revision)

1. **"Alizarin crimson"** — Synthetic alizarin (Graebe & Liebermann, 1868). Unavailable in 1847. → Fixed: "crimson lake" (natural cochineal/madder-based pigment, period-appropriate).

2. **"Cadmium red"** — Not commercially available until ~1910-1919. → Fixed: "vermilion" (mercury sulfide, dominant red pigment of the period).

3. **Green flash "she had read about it"** — No published scientific account before Jules Verne (1882) or Joule's 1869 letter. → Fixed: "heard sailors speak of it" (oral tradition is the plausible 1847 source).

## Verified Period-Correct

- Cadmium yellow: Available from ~1840 (discovered 1817, commercial from ~1840). Correct.
- Cadmium orange: Available from 1840s. Correct.
- Burnt sienna, raw umber, ultramarine, vermilion: All standard period pigments. Correct.
- Crimson lake: Natural pigment from cochineal or madder, widely available. Correct.
- Phosphorescence in ship's wake: Period-documented phenomenon. Correct.
