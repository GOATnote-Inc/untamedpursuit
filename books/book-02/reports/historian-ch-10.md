# Historical Accuracy Report: Book 2, Chapter 10

**Chapter**: "The Intimacy of Irrelevance" (Clara POV, ~May 15-18, 1847, aboard the Resolute, Pacific Ocean)
**Reviewer**: Historian agent
**Date**: 2026-03-07

---

## Errors

None found.

## Anachronisms

### 1. "reclassification" (line 137, pre-revision)
- First attested 1849 (OED). Scene set May 1847.
- **Severity**: Low — narrator's voice, not dialogue.
- **Fix applied**: Restructured to "a redrawing."

### 2. "perimeter" in quasi-tactical sense (line 137, pre-revision)
- Military sense not attested until 1860s-1940s. Clara's spatial/geometric usage borderline.
- **Fix applied**: Changed to "border."

## Verified Accurate

- **Phosphorescence / bioluminescence**: Well-documented by 1840s. Darwin described it during the Beagle voyage. "Phosphorescence" was the standard period term. Green color accurate for dinoflagellates.
- **"Natural philosophy"**: Standard 1840s term for natural science. Period-appropriate.
- **Fujian White Crane "founded by a woman"**: Fang Qiniang (mid-17th c.) is the historical founder.
- **Canton-Nagasaki trading corridor / Tojin Yashiki**: Chinese compound in Nagasaki housed ~2,000 merchants. Operated 1688-1859.
- **Canton trade to Philadelphia**: Philadelphia was a major hub from the 1780s. Silk, tea, porcelain were primary imports.
- **Wexford parish school**: Parochial schools existed in Wexford from the 1820s.
- **Irish dock workers in Philadelphia**: Well-documented Irish Catholic immigrant community from 1820s-1840s.
- **Death from coal dust / poor ventilation**: Anthracite coal burning in working-class homes caused chronic respiratory disease. Historically accurate.
- **Lavender on windowsill with one hour of sun**: Botanically authentic — lavender requires 6-8 hours to thrive. Three struggling stalks on one hour of sun is precise.
- **Clubs / port wine**: Philadelphia Club founded 1834. Port wine was a staple of gentlemen's social drinking.

## Summary

Historically sound. Two minor anachronisms found and fixed ("reclassification," "perimeter"). All major claims verified. Material culture details (three dresses, rosary, lye soap, coal dust, lavender) are authentic and evocative.
