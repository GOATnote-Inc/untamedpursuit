## Chapter 1: The Night Watch

- **POV**: Clara Chen
- **Location**: Philadelphia docks, near the Mercy Jane
- **Time**: Night, ~1846
- **Word count target**: 3,000–3,500

### Scene Beats

1. **Opening — The Dock** (~800 words): Clara stands at the edge of her private dock at night. George at her side. Sensory world-building: tar, rope, salt, violence. Harper approaches, expresses concern. Clara insists on walking alone — "I also wish to be a young woman and artist tonight." Harper departs south with his team. Clara begins her walk. Establish her weapons (sword cane, multiple Colts, modified percussion caps), George's training (hand signals, shadow movement), her duality (elegant coat concealing lethal capability).

2. **The Scream** (~400 words): A child's scream shatters the night. Not a drunken sailor — raw terror. Clara and George move as one. Years of training. George splits away at a hand signal to flank.

3. **The Fight** (~1,200 words): Clara arrives to find a man (Samuel) fighting six-plus traffickers near the Mercy Jane. Two girls whimpering. Samuel is outnumbered, his gun knocked away, taking hits but refusing to yield — protecting the girls, not running. Clara enters the fight. Sword cane becomes blade. Colts drawn with impossible speed. George materializes from shadows. Combat is surgical, economical, devastating. 12+ traffickers dead. Modified percussion caps near-silent. George guards children afterward.

4. **The Meeting** (~600 words): "George, hold." Clara introduces herself casually amid corpses. Samuel, bleeding and amazed, introduces himself. Mutual assessment — he notes her training, weapons, dog's discipline. She sees courage, ethics, intelligence. The tea invitation: "Three o'clock at this tea house." Her card. Departure into waiting carriage.

5. **Aftermath** (~400 words): Clara in the carriage. Instructions to Harper — arrange cleanup, protect children's families. George assigned to escort children home. Clara replays what she saw in Samuel. Forward-looking: tasks for Thomas. Tomorrow will be interesting.
