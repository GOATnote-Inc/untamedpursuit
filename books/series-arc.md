> **Structure:** 9-book continuous historical fiction series spanning two eras.
> Story beats use generic names. Material mapped from 45+ source files.
> All discrepancies resolved. See `bible/discrepancies.md` for the full resolution log.

---

## Series Premise

A multi-generational story of women who build and inherit a covert protection network spanning from 1840s Philadelphia to the 1880s American frontier. Clara Chen — spy, artist, healer, merchant — constructs an empire of hidden purpose. Eve Garrett — her protege, a healer-warrior — inherits it and must decide what to keep, what to shed, and what to make her own. The series asks: what do we owe the future, and what survives the people who built it?

## Series-Level Beat Sheet

> 15 generic story beats mapped against available source material.

### 1. Opening

- **Series-level**: Clara on the Philadelphia docks at night, age 25. Or: Eve on a mountain ridge, taking an impossible shot at a human trafficker.
- **Function**: Establish the world's duality — beauty concealing danger, ordinary surfaces hiding extraordinary depths.
- **Available material**:
  - `Episode I 1.31` (batch6 + batch7) — Clara walks the docks with George, rescues children from Mercy Jane crew
  - `outline_for_fierce_11_27` Act I — Eve on mountain ridge, the impossible shot
  - `Iron_and_Blood` — Eve on the train (alternate opening for Eve's era)
- **Author decision needed**: Does the series open in Era 1 (Clara) or Era 2 (Eve)? Both eras are canon (T5/T6 resolved).

### 2. Theme Stated

- **Series-level**: "Nothing human is foreign to me" (Latin: *Homo sum, humani nihil a me alienum puto*). Protection on a scale most people never see. The belief that creating beauty is an act of resistance.
- **Function**: Plant the philosophical spine — the network exists because these women refuse to accept cruelty as inevitable.
- **Available material**:
  - `Unit_creation_Shifting_Light` — "protection on a scale most people never see"; "authentic purpose expressed through individual gifts"
  - `Clara_with_Eve_on_her_death_bed` — shared Latin motto
  - `Clara_death_speech_The_Last_Gathering` — "I am a spy. I am an aunt. I am a friend. I am grateful."
  - `Claras_Words_at_Sunset` — Clara's philosophical farewell
  - `key_questions_for_novel_hooks` — thematic hooks and questions

### 3. Setup

- **Series-level**: Clara's empire in Philadelphia — docks, Golden Compass, townhouse, key relationships. The world she's built and the people who populate it.
- **Function**: Establish the scope of Clara's dual life before it's tested. Show the network operating at peak.
- **Available material**:
  - `Episode I 1.31` — Clara's docks, George, Thomas's office, the Night Watch rescue
  - `Golden_Compass_Stage_Episode` — the music hall, Clara's performances, Samuel's courtship
  - `Lilys_orchard` — Lily's sanctuary, the kidnapping backstory, Wissahickon Creek
  - `Fix_beginning_follow_up_from_Clara` — Thomas and Clara discuss Samuel, network operations
  - `___Outline NOVEL___` Acts I-II — medical school, dock operations, early network
  - `dinner_with_sam_and_clara_drafts` — social world, Samuel's character

### 4. Catalyst

- **Series-level**: The Mercy Jane rescue — Clara discovers the scope of the trafficking operation and meets Samuel Taylor. This event transforms her from a solitary operator into someone who builds partnerships.
- **Function**: Force Clara out of her contained world into something bigger.
- **Available material**:
  - `Episode I 1.31` — Night Watch rescue, kills 12+ men, rescues two girls, meets Samuel
  - `Clara_rescues_Sam` — alternate/expanded version of the rescue
  - `Fix_beginning_follow_up_from_Clara` — Thomas's debrief, anti-trafficking plan

### 5. Debate

- **Series-level**: Clara weighing the cost of her dual life. Can she let Samuel in? Can she trust the network to survive without her central control? Later: Eve resisting Clara's legacy — is she her own person or Clara's creation?
- **Function**: Internal conflict before commitment. The characters argue with themselves about what they're willing to risk.
- **Available material**:
  - `Fix_beginning_follow_up_from_Clara` — Clara's calculation about Samuel
  - `key_questions_for_novel_hooks` — "How do you build trust when your entire life is a lie?"
  - `Clara_teaches_Eve_Weight_of_Awareness` — Eve grappling with the burden of perception
  - `Clara_teaching_awareness_16yr_Eve` — Eve at 16, questioning her training
  - `Part_One_Summer_Memories` — Eve reflecting on what Clara built and what she inherits

### 6. Crossing the Threshold

- **Series-level**: The voyage west — Clara leaves Philadelphia for California. Later: Eve accepting her role as network leader ("When do we start?" / "My dear, you started years ago.").
- **Function**: Point of no return. The old world is behind them.
- **Available material**:
  - `outline_2_16` — Full voyage sequence: Panama crossing, Caribbean pirates, Harper's death, San Francisco landing
  - `Outline_episode_Fierce_Book1_Season1` — Episodic breakdown of the voyage (Eps 6-12)
  - `Part_One_Summer_Memories` — The reveal: Eve learns about the covert network, accepts her role

### 7. B-Story

- **Series-level**: Clara and Samuel's love story (Era 1). Eve and Thomas Mitchell's romance (Era 2). Both test the theme — can you build love on a foundation of secrets?
- **Function**: The emotional mirror to the plot. Love relationships that embody the series' themes of trust, hidden depths, and partnership.
- **Available material**:
  - `Golden_Compass_Stage_Episode` — Samuel weeps at Clara's music; "crossed an important threshold"
  - `dinner_with_sam_and_clara_drafts` — Courtship dynamics

  - `Eve_and_Jessie_dreaming_by_shore` — Eve's emotional life, dreams of connection

### 8. Fun and Games / Promise of the Premise

- **Series-level**: The network in action — rescues, training montages, the Glass Garden thriving, Eve and Jessie's frontier operations, Clara's spy craft.
- **Function**: Deliver what the reader came for. Show the premise at its most satisfying before complications set in.
- **Available material**:
  - `Lilys_orchard` — Lily's healing, the sanctuary, Ajax
  - `Claras_Fortress` — Glass Garden revealed: gun ports, sword courtyard, herb garden, hidden passages
  - `Chapter_Seven_First_Snow` — Silver Creek raid: "What followed was a dance they'd practiced a hundred times"
  - `Chapter_Eight_Spring_Melt` — Snake River crossing under fire
  - `Eve_and_shooting_with_Jack` — Training: Winchester, "reading air," Jack's teaching
  - `kate_and_clara_in_anatomy_lab` — Kate and Clara as medical students; Kate deduces Clara's secrets
  - `kate_meet_clara` — Frontier medicine: fever outbreak, treating attackers
  - `Scene_First_Meeting` — Jessie and Storm's bond
  - `Jack_meet_Clara` — Jack's tracking skills, Ruby, well poisoning discovery

### 9. Midpoint

- **Series-level**: Clara's terminal diagnosis. The empire she built has an expiration date — her own mortality. Everything shifts from building to passing on.
- **Function**: False victory or false defeat that raises the stakes. The clock starts.
- **Available material**:
  - `unravelled_clara` — Eve examines Clara; breast mass with metastasis; medically detailed
  - `clara_and_eve_discuss_breasts` — Alternate/expanded diagnosis scene
  - `Part_One_Summer_Memories` — "6-8 months"; Samuel reveals the full network
  - `Unit_creation_Shifting_Light` — Team assembled, Clara has 6 months to guide

### 10. Escalation / Bad Guys Close In

- **Series-level**: Clara dying. Enemies sensing weakness. Glass Garden assaulted by forces who know its layout. The Durand family conspiracy. Wallace's betrayal. The network under attack from multiple fronts.
- **Function**: Everything the characters built is threatened. Enemies exploit the leadership vacuum.
- **Available material**:
  - `outline_for_fierce_11_27` Acts II-III — Full assault on Glass Garden, tunnel retreat, Thomas's Durand connection revealed
  - `Outline_episode_Fierce_Book1_Season1` Ep5-9 — Wallace corruption, betrayal, San Francisco conflict
  - `outline_2_16` — Wallace's men attack Clara's docks from another city
  - `Jack_meet_Clara` — Well poisoning: "testing delivery methods for something worse"
  - `Chapter_One_Eve_and_Clara` — Clara diminished, time running out

### 11. Crisis / All Is Lost

- **Series-level**: The triple blow — Harper dies during the voyage west. Clara dies. Kate and Jack are killed in the train attack. Eve loses every mentor, every parent, every anchor.
- **Function**: Strip the protagonist of all support. Force her to stand alone.
- **Available material**:
  - `outline_2_16` Scene 21 — Harper's death during the voyage
  - `Clara_with_Eve_on_her_death_bed` — Clara's final hours, river speech, Latin motto
  - `Clara_death_speech_The_Last_Gathering` — "My breasts seem to be killing me"; farewell speech
  - `Claras_Words_at_Sunset` — Philosophical farewell on the western stone porch
  - `Claras_Death_Party` — Death gathering (alternate version)
  - `Iron_and_Blood` — Train attack: Gatling gun ambush, Kate's last words ("Go! Now! We hold here."), Jessie's three dynamite sticks, Jack and Kate killed

### 12. Dark Night of the Soul

- **Series-level**: Eve alone. Clara dead. Parents dead. The network fractured. Ghost howling for three days. Eve on the Monterey cliffs telling "meaningless stories" so the memories don't fade.
- **Function**: The emotional bottom. Eve must choose whether to rebuild or walk away.
- **Available material**:
  - `Epilogue_Claras_Final_Words` — Eve finds Clara's undelivered letter; reads it aloud; plays Bach at midnight
  - `Pacific_Monterey_cliffs` — Eve and Jessie on the cliffs; "Not the big moments. But the small ones"; Ghost between them
  - `Eve_and_Jessie_dreaming_by_shore` — Processing grief by the ocean
  - `Eve_and_Gus` — Eve alone with her horse (alternate version)

### 13. Breakthrough

- **Series-level**: Eve fully steps into leadership. She returns to the Glass Garden and begins teaching. New students in the practice yard. The cycle of mentorship continues — not as imitation but as evolution.
- **Function**: The protagonist finds her own version of the mentor's strength. The legacy transforms.
- **Available material**:
  - `Claras_Fortress` — Eve returns to Glass Garden with Jessie; reveals the hidden world; new students training; Eve teaches: "A sword is not a weapon..."
  - `Unit_creation_Shifting_Light` — Eve assembles her own team; "authentic purpose expressed through individual gifts"
  - `Part_One_Summer_Memories` — Eve's inner transformation: "the weight of responsibility — not as a burden, but as a purpose"
  - `One_year_after_Venezuela_crisis` — Network thriving under Eve's leadership; dual-use platforms; Madison's tech company

### 14. Climax

- **Series-level**: Eve's impossible shot saves Thomas. Final battle for Glass Garden. Thomas confronts his own family. The network defeats the Durand conspiracy.
- **Function**: The protagonist uses everything she's learned — from every mentor, every tradition, every loss — in one decisive action.
- **Available material**:
  - `outline_for_fierce_11_27` Act III — Battle for Glass Garden; Eve's impossible shot; Thomas confronts the Durands
  - `Outline_episode_Fierce_Book1_Season1` Ep10 — "Master plan; victory with cost"
  - `Chapter_Seven_First_Snow` — Eve's combat capability demonstrated: "a dance they'd practiced a hundred times"
  - `Chapter_Eight_Spring_Melt` — Eve under fire: tactical thinking, medical precision, frontier toughness

### 15. Resolution

- **Series-level**: Eve training a new recruit, mirroring Clara's first lesson. The series ends as it began — a mentor and a student, a sword, and the words: "A sword is not a weapon." But Eve's version is her own.
- **Function**: The cycle continues. The network survives because it was built on connections, not dependency.
- **Available material**:
  - `Claras_Fortress` — New students in the practice yard; Eve teaching Jessie the first lesson
  - `Epilogue_Claras_Final_Words` — Clara's letter: "Be fierce, dear ones"; Samuel's toast; Eve plays Bach
  - `outline_for_fierce_11_27` Act IV — "Network accepts Eve as leader. Final scene mirrors opening with Eve as mentor."
  - `Jessie_considers_Art` — Life goes on: Jessie's art quarter, Eve's solo mission, the network operating independently

## Character Arcs Across the Series

### Clara Chen (Books 1-6)
- **Book 1-2**: Builder. Creates the network from scratch. Central control.
- **Book 3-4**: Mentor. Trains the next generation. Begins releasing control.
- **Book 5**: Diagnosed. Forced to confront mortality and succession.
- **Book 6**: Farewell. Dies having built something that outlasts her. "Then I did what I was meant to do."

### Eve Garrett (Books 4-9)
- **Book 4**: Student. Learning every tradition Clara can teach.
- **Book 5**: Heir. Inherits the network. Wary of Thomas.
- **Book 6-7**: Orphan. Loses Clara, then Kate and Jack. Alone.
- **Book 8**: Leader. Rebuilds on her own terms. "Not as a burden, but as a purpose."
- **Book 9**: Mentor. Teaches the next generation. The cycle continues.

### Jessie (Books 5-9)
- **Book 5-6**: Wild card. Dynamite and humor. Eve's anchor.
- **Book 7**: Savior. Three dynamite sticks on the train.
- **Book 8-9**: Partner. Grows from bounty hunter to network operative to artist.

### Samuel Taylor (Books 1-6)
- **Book 1**: Outsider. Ethical businessman drawn into Clara's world.
- **Book 2-4**: Partner. Co-leader of the network. Manages finance and operations.
- **Book 5-6**: Griever. Clara's illness and death threaten to consume him.
- **Note**: Samuel is the hero. Antagonist version from abandoned alternate (Fierce_Untamed_Pusuit_Series_1) is non-canon.
